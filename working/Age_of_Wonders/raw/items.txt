Items
in
Age of Wonders
provide various benefits to
heroes
. Items provide bonuses to four eligible
stats
(attack
, damage
, defense
, resistance
) and/or additional
abilities
to the hero. Only heroes are capable of picking up and using items. Non-hero units cannot use items.
Overview
Types
Items are categorized into one of six types:
Attack
- Attack items are equipped in the weapon slot and generally provide bonuses to the attack and damage stats, as well as abilities that enhance the hero's melee capabilities. Many attack items provides an ability that infuses melee attacks with a
damage type
. Such items also provide
Strike
, but this is redundant because all heroes already have Strike.
Defense
- Defense items are equipped in the shield slot and generally provide defensive stats and abilities.
Torso
- Torso items are equipped on the torso slot and come in two sub-types: "cloaks," which provide concealment abilities, and "armor," which provides defensive stats and abilities.
Head
- Head items are equipped in the headgear slot and provide defensive bonuses and a wide range of abilities.
Ring
- Rings are equipped in one of two ring slots, and provide a wide range of bonuses.
Use
- Items of the "use" type only need to be in one of the hero's 8 storage slots in the inventory in order for the item to be used. Use items generally provide the hero with a combat ability, typically a ranged attack attack. Ranged attack abilities from item benefit from
Marksmanship
.
Transfer value
Items have an assigned "
value
," which governs the transfer cost require to carry over the item from one scenario to another in the single player
campaign
. Default transfer values listed in the game's editor are given below. Since campaign scenarios have a certain transfer limit, items with low transfer values are preferred over those with higher transfer values, if the bonuses are comparable. In some cases, certain items that have very low values given in the default item library are given adjusted actual values in the single player campaign. In these instances, the campaign values are listed in parentheses.
Acquisition
Items can be obtained from map structures of the Monster Lair, Castle Ruins, and Ziggurat types. Items available from such locations are randomly set at the start of the scenario. The "
availability
" property governs the rarity of the item, with "exceptional" being the most rare, and "frequent" being the least rare. Exceptional items are capable of dropping from structures with average item value, although the chances are higher for those with high item value. In rare instances, locations providing item loot may be set to have fixed (i.e., non-random) loot.
