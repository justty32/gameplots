This page lists
heroes
that appear in the
single player campaign
in
Age of Wonders
.
Overview
Non-leader heroes
Most non-leader heroes are standard heroes from the
hero library
. These heroes generally begin with their standard starting stats and abilities set in the hero library. In some cases, customized non-leader heroes also appear in the campaign.
Heroes who can offer to join the player belong to one of the following two categories:
Free heroes
are those that are not initially placed on the map, but are eligible to emerge if emergence conditions are met or if the
Call Hero
spell is used. Emergence of free heroes depends on the current number of heroes that a player already has and diplomatically favorable conditions, which generally requires that the player (or AI faction) has sufficiently high relations with the race of the hero. Most free heroes are designed to potentially appear on the side of the player by being of a race that is aligned with the race of the player-controlled faction, but some have an alignment for which it is unlikely for them to join the player-controlled faction.
Independent heroes
(i.e., not aligned with any faction) that are set to "
refugee
" status will offer to join upon being approached by a unit controlled by the player. These heroes are labeled "Independent-refugee" in the list below.
Heroes not belonging to one of the above categories are pre-assigned to one of the AI-controlled factions on the map. These heroes cannot be directly recruited by the player, but can nonetheless be captured by the player using
Dominate
,
Charm
, or
Seduce
.
Leader heroes
Leaders
are not drawn from any standard list but instead have names and starting stats/abilities customized to each campaign scenario. Besides the leader heroes listed below, the campaign also features a player-created protagonist, who is, by default, an Elf named Elric in the
Keepers campaign
or a Dark Elf named Tallic in the
Cult of Storms campaign
.
Leader customization
is permitted for the protagonist leader, if desired by the player.
Heroes with unique portraits
In the data below, the "
unique
" superscript tag refers that have a unique portrait and are also not part of the hero library. These heroes, which are typically leader heroes, are generally characters that have significance to the plot of
Age of Wonders
. Besides the unique portrait, these characters function as standard heroes and leaders for purposes of gameplay.

A
hero
in
Age of Wonders
is a special
unit
with the freedom to choose which
abilities
to learn and
stats
to improve when they advance in
experience level
.
Overview
Leaders and non-leader heroes
Heroes are classified into
leader
heroes and non-leader heroes. Leaders have special gameplay properties and are "essential" in that any leader on the map must remain alive or else their faction is defeated.
Leader customization
can be used to customize a leader's starting stats and abilities. For additional details on how leaders differ from ordinary heroes, please see the above-linked article for leaders.
Non-leader heroes serve as additional heroes besides the leader. Non-leader heroes do not have customizable starting stats and abilities, but can acquire new stats and abilities through level-up in the same manner as leaders. In the
campaign
and
standalone scenarios
, non-leader heroes are typically drawn from the standard hero set in the
hero library
and start with their starting stats and abilities as defined in the library.
Non-leader heroes cost
5 plus
2 for each level of the hero in
upkeep
per turn. In the campaign, heroes have a transfer value of 37 + level × 25 for purposes of transferring them between successive scenarios.
Hero leveling
Main article:
Hero leveling
Heroes can advance up to an experience level of 30. Heroes begin with 25 base skill points and acquire 10 additional skill points for each level beyond level 1. The 10 additional skill points gained by leveling can be spent on stats and/or abilities. The skill point cost to increase each stat by 1 is as follows:
Attack (
) - 5 skill points
Defense (
) - 5 skill points
Damage (
) - 10 skill points
Resistance (
) - 5 skill points
Health (
) - 5 skill points
Movement (
) - 2 skill points
Abilities can cost anywhere from 5 to 20 skill points, and some abilities such as
Turn Undead
and
Marksmanship
have multiple levels that can be invested in. Some abilities are not available in the level-up interface but can be acquired during leader customization. Heroes are the only units that can cast
spells
(i.e. the only units that can possess the
Spell Casting
ability) or use
items
.

Acquiring heroes
Heroes can be acquired through multiple means:
Each faction on a map starts with a leader (except in a
non-campaign scenario
in which the "leaders on map" setting is switched off).
Free heroes
may offer to join as a random event as a leader acquires fame.
Free heroes can be summoned through the
Call Hero
spell, after which they may offer to join when approached if diplomatic conditions are favorable.
Independent heroes encountered in the campaign may offer to join when approached.
Heroes may offer to join when freed from dungeons.
Heroes can be captured using
Dominate
,
Charm
, or
Seduce
.
Heroes can be
resurrected
or
reanimated
through spells.

A
leader
is a
hero
in
Age of Wonders
with additional properties and gameplay functions. A leader represents a certain faction and its diplomatic relations. In scenarios in which the leader is on the map, the leader is also an indispensable hero for that faction—the loss of a leader in battle results in immediate defeat for the faction (or an immediate game over screen if that faction is controlled by the player). Graphically, leaders use in-game sprites different from those of non-leader heroes.
Gameplay
As a
unit
, leaders have all functions of heroes, including the same set of
statistics
, as well as the following additional properties:
All leaders begin with
Spell Casting
as a free base
ability
, whereas non-leader heroes must spend 20 skill points to learn Spell Casting (or have it pre-learned with 20 skill points pre-allocated).
Leaders are immune to unit-capturing abilities, namely
Charm
,
Seduce
,
Dominate
, and
Possess
. Leaders can be targeted by such abilities, but use of the ability on them will always fail.
Leaders cannot desert a faction.
Leaders do not have any upkeep cost, and do not have any transfer cost between successive scenarios in the
campaign
.
A leader that is on the map provides an income of 5 mana per day (this mana income is not given if "leader on map" is disabled).
Leaders can be customized through the
leader customization
interface at the start of the campaign or a standalone scenario. This enables leaders to acquire certain create-only abilities that cannot be acquired by non-leader heroes (unless the non-leader hero is preconfigured with that ability in the hero library).
Leaders are present on the map of all
campaign scenarios
. For
standalone scenarios
, the "leaders on map" setting determines whether leaders are present on the map. If not present on the map, the name and portrait of the leader will still be displayed in the diplomacy screen.
Portraits
Portraits for leaders include those of all heroes in the
hero library
, as well as the following portraits that are exclusively available to leaders. During leader customization, the player may select from among any of the following portraits, regardless of their depicted race.

In
Age of Wonders
, the
hero library
includes a set of premade non-leader
heroes
, each with default starting
statistics
,
abilities
, and an assigned character portrait. The term "hero library" is used in the map editor to refer to a set of pre-made heroes that can be used in a
scenario
.
Use of the hero library
Most
campaign scenarios
and all
standalone scenarios
use heroes from the hero library. As such, the hero library can be regarded as a "standard" set of heroes that appear in the game. Scenarios typically use a selection of heroes from hero library. Standalone scenarios generally use a fairly large selection of heroes in the library as
free heroes
who can emerge during the course of the game, whereas campaign scenarios generally use only a small selection of library heroes.
Some campaign scenarios use custom heroes that are not in the library. Nonetheless, heroes from the library frequently appear in campaign scenarios, especially on the side of factions not under the player's control. For a full list of heroes that appear in the campaign, please see the main article on
campaign heroes
.
Heroes in the library are used as non-leader heroes only.
Leaders
, on the other hand, are customized on a map-by-map basis, and also have a separate set of default portraits different from those that are used for the hero library heroes listed below.
List of heroes in the hero library
The hero library includes a total of 50 standard heroes. Heroes of the same
race
have a set of common base stats and abilities, which are listed below on the first row for each section corresponding to a race. Each individual hero also starts with various
additional
starting stats and abilities, i.e., stats and abilities that are in addition to the racial base stats and abilities. For example, a value of "+1" for attack (
) means that the hero has +1 point in attack in addition to the hero's race-derived base stats.
The additional stats and abilities listed for individual heroes correspond to pre-allocated skill points of the hero. For example, a level +1 hero will have a total of +25 skill points worth of additional starting stats and abilities, whereas a level +2 hero will have a total of +35 skill points worth of additional starting stats and abilities.
Heroes in the library often have
abilities
that distinguish them from one another. For example, many heroes have abilities that are normally only available in the
leader customization
interface and not in the
level-up
interface (such as
Life Stealing
and
Regeneration
), while some have abilities that are available in neither. For example,
Resh Redhead
has the
Fire Breath
ability, which cannot be normally acquired.
