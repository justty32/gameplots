Story
As empress of the Elves, Eleanor gave birth to prince Meandor in 878 LIR after 8 years of pregnancy. However, Eleandor died in childbirth and her body was taken to
Evermore
. The celebration of the new heir’s birth is overshadowed by the obligation to mourn the loss of the Elven Queen.
After Eleanor's death, Inioch married
Elwyn
, who later gave birth to princess
Julia
in 969 LIR. The birth of Elwyn's daughter implicated unease in the Kingdom due to overpopulation as a result of the recent arrival of the humans. To address this matter, Inioch proclaimed that the newly born princess was meant to be a "replacement" for Eleanor (in the context of the Elven practice of balancing newborns with those who migrate to
Evermore
for purposes of population control
), and that the princess would be named Julia, a name used by Eleanor. Inioch's remarriage and the birth of princess Julia caused resentment from Meandor, which was a factor that contributed to the fracturing of Inioch's court.

Age of Wonders
series
In the
timeline
in the game manual for

, Inioch's first wife was named Julia. The timeline also stated that Elwyn's daughter Julia was named after her:

In 2005, Lennart Sas of Triumph Studios noted that the name "Julia" in the original lore material was an error, and that Inioch's first wife was instead intended to be name Eleanor.
“
"878 LIR: After 8 years of pregnancy, Inioch's wife gives birth to Meandor, but the celebrations are cut short. Inioch's first wife, Julia, dies in childbirth."
An unfortunate typo crept in this entry. I had to dig up a 6 year old design doc to find that Inioch's first wife was named Eleanor.
— Lennart Sas, January 21, 2005 post on
Age of Wonders Heaven
forums (
external link
)
Later media in the
Age of Wonders
series refer to Inioch's first wife as Eleanor.

Elwyn

Story
Elwyn married
Inioch
in 898 LIR, becoming queen and empress
of the Verdant Kingdom. At the time, Inioch already had a child,
Meandor
, with his previous wife
Eleandor
, who died giving birth to Meandor. Meandor, still only a child, resented Elwyn and found the haste of Inioch's marriage to her an insult to his mother. Decades later, Elwyn gave birth to princess
Julia
in 969 LIR, which caused further resentment from Meandor.
In 970 LIR, the palace of the
Elven Court
came under siege from the rebel Humans.
During attack, Elwyn realized that the Court had been betrayed. Wanting to stay behind with Inioch, Elwyn commanded her bodyguard Torlill to flee with Julia, but Torlill reminded her that only she can care for the baby princess. As Torlill held off the Human attackers, Elwyn flee with Julia through a tunnel passage. Elwyn's escape was the first time she had stepped outside the boundaries of the Elven Court.
Queen of the Elves of Aldor
After the fall of the Elven Court, Elwyn and her followers wandered for almost thirty years until finally arriving at the Isle of
Aldor
. There, she met a halfling priest,
Ham Binger
, who offered to give the Isle to Elwyn in exchange for a kiss from Julia. Hardly believing her luck, Elwyn agreed and adopted Aldor as her home and the home for her followers.
The Elves of Aldor accept her as ruler, and the Halflings back her appointment with unprecedented enthusiasm.
As queen of the Elves of Aldor, Elwyn's dominion was only a fraction of Inioch's former Kingdom. The Dark Elves recognized Meandor as the rightful ruler, while Elves of Silvanus vowed to remain independent of both Meandor and Elwyn. However, Elwyn did find an important ally in the
Keepers
, who accepted Elwyn as their patron. Julia would later join the ranks of the Keepers, eventually becoming their leader.
Assassination
In 1216 LIR, a star appeared over the Valley of Wonders, prompting Julia to bring her Keeper forces to reclaim the Valley. This, however, unwittingly played into Meandor's hands, as it left Aldor vulnerable.
While Julia's forces were away, Meandor organized a force of Goblins to assassinate Elwyn. Due to treachery within Elwyn's ranks,
the assassination was successful.
After the battle of the Valley of Wonders, the Keepers brought Elwyn's body from the Isle of Aldor to the Sanctuary Woods. There, a burial chamber was constructed for Elwyn close to the place where she was earthborn.
Centuries later, Elwyn's spirit was visited by her granddaughter Sundren.

Inioch
was an Elven emperor and wizard-king of the
Verdant Kingdom
. He is a major character in the
campaign
and backstory of
Age of Wonders
.
Story
Reign as emperor and wizard-king
Inioch ruled the Verdant Kingdom of the Blessed Continent for nearly a thousand years. The calendar year 0 LIR (Lord Inioch’s Reign) marked the beginning of the reign. In that year, Inioch was crowned emperor of the Elves, while his father retired to
Evermore
.
Inioch ruled over all living beings on the Blessed Continent through the Elven Court located in the Valley of Wonders. As an emperor who was also a Wizard-king, Inioch maintained a delicate balance of light and shadow and saw that the life of each subject in his Kingdom was not burdened by any great evil.
Eight hundred years into Inioch's reign, the Highmen first appeared before his Elven Court.
The Highmen warned that the Humans will soon arrive on the Blessed Continent
and requested that Inioch and the Elves depart to Evermore, so that Humans may thrive freely on the Blessed Continent. This request was based on an old covenant between the Highmen and the first Elves, under which the Elves would develop and render habitable worlds discovered by the Highmen, but would eventually cede the world to the Humans, who are the creation of the Highmen.
However, Inioch, who had grown fond of the Continent and his role as emperor, refused to leave for Evermore and honor the Highmen's request.
Instead, he intended to incorporate the soon-to-arrive humans into his Kingdom. In doing so, he formed an order of scholars known as the
Keepers
, who were tasked with maintaining peace among the races in his realm. However, the Keepers were not informed of the Highmen's intentions and the full communications between Inioch and the Highmen.
In 878 LIR, Inioch's wife
Eleanor
gave birth to prince
Meandor
, but she died in childbirth. After Eleanor's death, Inioch married a new empress,
Elwyn
, who later gave birth to a princess
Julia
in 969 LIR. Inioch's marriage to Elwyn and the birth of Julia caused resentment from Meandor, who considered Inioch's actions to be an insult to his mother.
Fall of the Elven Court
The Humans began arriving at the outskirts of Inioch's realm in 962 LIR, clashing with the Dwarves and other local races at the Blood Isles before reaching the Valley of Wonders three years later.
At the insistence of the Keepers, the Humans were permitted to settle in the Valley. However, the Human colonies faced hardship under Inioch's rule.
Inioch's rule proved to be too strict for the Humans.
He sought to subjugate the Humans,
which eventually drove them to rebellion.
In 970 LIR, the Humans rebelled against Inioch. Together with their allies, the Humans struck against many of the Kingdom’s most precious holdings, including Inioch’s Court. Amidst political infighting in the Kingdom, the Elven Court falls to a force of Humans, and Inioch is slain by Human hands.
During this episode, Meandor had allied with a secret faction of Elven rebels known as
Dark Elves
,
and betrayed Inioch by guaranteeing that his father would not be able to escape.
The fall of the Elven Court resulted in the destruction of Inioch's Kingdom and the wars between races of the continent. Elwyn and Julia would settle in Aldor, and would later support the Keepers, who continued their goal of achieving harmony between all races, including the Humans. Meandor, who was severely wounded during the event, came to harbor hatred for humanity. In 985 LIR, Meandor founded the
Cult of Storms
, dedicated to the destruction of humanity. Over time, the Cult of Storms found itself to be tolerant of the Undead, and incorporated Necromancers among its rank.
The Cult of Storms maintained a high level of reverence for Inioch, whom they consider to be the greatest Elven Lord. In 1216 LIR, Meandor was seen creeping about the central Court Ruins, sifting through the bones of the dead,
with the intention of resurrecting Inioch.
Resurrection and defeat
After a star appeared over the Valley of Wonders, Julia, now leader of the Keepers, brought her forces to the Valley of Wonders. During this conflict between the Keepers and the Cult of Storms, Meandor resurrects Inioch as an Undead lich. The resurrection occurred at a mountain formation known as the Skull.
However, instead of allying with the Meandor, Inioch unleashes the Undead across the land, seeking to "purify" all Elves, including the Dark Elves, in undeath,
while also turning the star above the Valley blood red.
Inioch's return forced Meandor to treat, leaving the son in a state of torment. His nights were fitful and his days filled with visions only he can see. Inioch sent Undead forces to capture Meandor, but they were repelled. After Meandor freed himself from the torment, he ceased to hope for his father.
Leading the Undead forces, Inioch attacked both the Keepers and the Cult, as well as the independent elves of Silvanus.
Inioch also directed his forces to retake the Valley of Wonders from the Humans, in order to stop the plan that the Highmen had set in motion during his reign. The Highmen also return to fight Inioch's Undead. Eventually, the forces of Julia's Keepers, Meandor's Cult, Inioch's Undead, and
Gabriel
's Highmen all converged for battle at the Valley of Wonders. Ultimately, Julia was victorious, defeating all other forces at the Valley, including those of Inioch.
The Wizard's Throne
After his defeat at the Valley of Wonders, Inioch plotted his return while lingering in spirit form. He regarded Julia as treacherous
and maintained his desire to tear asunder all creation to rid the world of the Age of Men and to reform the world into something that never existed.
Inioch enticed a group of traitorous wizards of the
Circle of Evermore
. These traitor wizards captured Julia and allowed Inioch's spirit to possess her body, while also handing Inioch the Wizard's Throne in Evermore. However, his brief reign in Evermore came to an end when Merlin captured the Wizard's Throne and banished Inioch's spirit from Julia's body.

Julia

Story
Julia was born in 969 LIR to Inioch and Elwyn at a time of unease. The recent arrival of the Humans had degraded comfort of life in the Kingdom. Inioch’s political enemies viewed the new child as evidence that there will soon be no room for both the growing Elven population and the ever-burgeoning humanity.
Since the Elves maintained population by balancing newborns with those who migrate to
Evermore
,
Inioch sought to alleviate concerns by proclaiming that the new child was meant to replace his previously deceased wife
Eleanor
. Thus, Inioch named the new child Julia, a name also used for Eleanor.
Julia's birth resulted in resentment from the late Eleanor's son, prince Meandor, who was already upset that Inioch hastily married Elwyn after Eleanor's death. Meandor allied with a secret faction of Elven rebels known as the
Dark Elves
,
and later betrayed Inioch when the Humans rose up in rebellion.
The rebel Humans sacked the Elven Court, killing Inioch in the process.
Resentful of the humans, Meandor formed the
Cult of Storms
, an order dedicated to the destruction of humanity.
Early life
During the fall of the Elven Court, Elwyn reluctantly fled while carrying the baby Julia in her grasp. For almost thirty years, Elwyn, Julia, and their followers wandered the land until finally arriving at the Isle of Aldor. There, the halfling priest
Ham Binger
offered to give the Isle to Elwyn in exchange for a kiss from Julia. Ham insisted that the trade was fair, since Julia had been untouched by any mortal. Elwyn agreed and Julia gave Ham the kiss, resulting in Elwyn's elves adopting Aldor as their new home.
Some say that this kiss granted Ham immortality.
Elwyn, now queen of the Aldorian Elves, supported the Keepers, believing the Keepers’ credo that the Humans could turn to the ways of peace and good.
In 1025 LIR, Julia joined the Keepers, and began her training among them. She was celebrated as the youngest Elf to join their order.
Under the tutelage of Elwyn and the Keepers, Julia learned to foster the hope of peace. Many of the people in the land looked to her as the hope of a true heir to Inioch’s fallen Kingdom. Inwardly, they prayed that she might be the key to return the world to balance and stop the endless wars. She grew quickly among the Keepers, every day more eager to learn. The Keepers taught her the tokens and signs that controlled the very powers of Life.
Return to the Valley of Wonders
Achieving greater success in the ranks of the Keepers, Julia was appointed as the Keepers' leader in 1204 LIR. In 1216 LIR, a star appeared over the Valley of Wonders. Within days of its appearance, Keeper spies spotted Meandor walking among the shadows of Inioch’s ruined Court, searching for something. As leader of the Keepers, Julia gathered her forces and set off for the Valley of Wonders to rebut whatever conspiracy her half-brother had in mind.
While Julia was away, the Cult of Storms assassinated Elwyn in Aldor on the order of Meandor. Despite the loss of Elwyn, one Keeper, self-proclaimed as the "Chosen of the Keepers," was able to rally Halfling forces to prevent further losses at the hands of the Cult of Storms. At the direction of Julia, the Keeper also aided the Dwarves, who had lost Deepmir to an earthquake caused by
Melenis
, in establishing a new homeland.
Eventually, the chosen Keeper gathered a force of Halflings, Dwarves, and Elves to reinforce Julia at the Valley of Wonders. In the ensuing battle, Julia and the Keepers emerged victorious over Meandor, Inioch, and the Highmen, who had arrived to fight the Undead and protect the Humans, thereby reclaiming the Valley from the Humans.
The victorious keepers vowed to rebuild and share the Valley according to Julia's vision, while the Highmen, who conceded Julia's victory, asked the Keepers to look after the Humans.

The
protagonist
of the
Keepers campaign
in
Age of Wonders
(who is named
Elric
by default) is the
Keeper
that provides the narrative perspective of the campaign, as well as the player's

Name
By default, the Keeper protagonist is an Elf named Elric. However, the player is free to customize the protagonist's name and race, as well as the protagonist's portrait, starting stats, attributes, and selection of magic spheres (by way of
leader customization
). If customized, the protagonist's race can be Elf, Dwarf, Halfling, Azrac, or Lizardman.
Although the protagonist has a default name of Elric, the development team decided not to explicitly reference the protagonist in the story of subsequent games in the series due to the character's variability.
Therefore, even though the Keepers loyalist ending was adopted as canon, the Keeper protagonist does not have a specific name or identity in the canon of the series.
References in the Cult campaign
In the Cult of Storms version of the campaign, the narrative text occasionally mentions "the Keeper" in connection with the events of the loyalist path of the Keepesr campaign. Specifically, the Undead path of the the Cult campaign refers to "the Keeper that murdered"
Melenis
,
referring the events in
The Safelands
in which the protagonist defeats Melenis. It also refers to using a captured "Dwarven hero that knew the Keeper," a reference to the events of
Wizard College
, in which a Dwarf named Moljnir who fought with the protagonist in the Safelands invites the latter to a site that would become an ambush.
The name
Elric
is referenced once in the Cult campaign, in the
final scenario
of the Dark Elves path of the campaign (which is available only in the Cult of Storms campaign). In this version of the map, the name of Elric is used for a high-level hero belonging to the Elves faction led by
Julia
. In the Highmen and Undead versions of the final scenario, which can also be played in the Keepers campaign (such that Elric can be present as the player character), the hero in this role is named
Khelan
instead, a name that does not appear anywhere else in the game. However, the Dark Elves, Highmen, and Undead endgame routes are non-canonical in terms of the outcome.
Background and personality
Little is mentioned of the Keeper protagonist's background, except for being a member of the Keepers who has met Julia before. Based on the story text of the campaign, the Keeper protagonist displays a relatively balanced personality, as compared to the Stormlord protagonist who notably displays more arrogance. If the protagonist strays from the Keepers and follow the most evil path, the protagonist will, at the end, admit that they made a mistake by succumbing to the forces of Blackwater lake.

Meandor
is the son of
Inioch
and
Eleanor
, and the half-brother of
Julia
. Originally a prince of Inioch's
Verdant Kingdom
, Meandor survived the Kingdom's fall and later founded the
Cult of Storms
, serving as its leader through the events of the
campaign
in
Age of Wonders
.
Story
Meandor was born in 878 LIR as Inioch's first child and the heir apparent of the Verdant Kingdom. However, Meandor's mother died in childbirth, and Inioch married a new queen,
Elwyn
, just after the minimal period of mourning had expired. Inioch's marriage to Elwyn caused resentment in Meandor, who found the haste of his father's remarriage to be an insult to his mother. Later, when Elwyn was pregnant with Inioch's second child, factions of the Elven Court led by Meandor refused to celebrate the child’s coming.
The fall of Inioch's court
The birth of Elwyn's child, Julia, ultimately played a role in Meandor turning against his father. At the time, there was unease in the Kingdom due to overpopulation from the recent arrival of the Humans, whom the Keepers permitted to settle in the Kingdom.
Since the Elves practiced balancing newborns with those who migrate to
Evermore
in order to control population,
Inioch proclaimed that the newly born princess would be a "replacement" for Eleanor and that she would be named Julia, a name that was previously used by Eleanor. As a result, Meandor was less than pleased.
The arrival of the Humans had caused serious problems in the Kingdom. Human colonies had experienced hardship as subjects of Inioch. Furthermore, the Elven community had split into two opposing philosophical camps,
those supporting peace with the Humans, and those opposing the Humans. The latter camp included a secret faction of Elven rebels in the Elven Court known as the Dark Elves. Meandor sought out the Dark Elves, where he found support for his hatred for the Humans and his repulsion for those Elves, especially his stepmother, whom he felt had forsaken him.
Eventually, the Humans rose up in rebellion against Inioch, sacking the palace of the Elven Court and slaying Inioch himself. During this episode, Meandor conspired with the Humans to gain control of Inioch's throne for himself.
When the Humans attacked, Meandor betrayed his father by guaranteeing that the latter would not be able to escape from the Humans.
However, the Human rebels did not support Meandor. Instead, the Humans slew Meandor's relatives and closest friends,
while taking the Valley of Wonders for themselves. Meandor was gravely wounded and mistaken for dead. The slaughter was so great that the Humans piled bodies in the ruined central court. Meandor’s gravely-wounded body was thrown into the heap of dead to rot.
Creating the Cult of Storms
Though gravely wounded, Meandor survived. In 977 LIR, the Dark Elves crowned Meandor as the rightful monarch. Few other races acknowledged the act, and many back Queen Elwyn, while others simply refuse to accept any ruler. Meandor attempted to flatter the Elves of Silvanus into joining with him, but those Elves refused, and Meandor’s plans for retaking the Valley were largely ignored.
After a short period of being ignored, Meandor formed the Cult of Storms, dedicated to the destruction of humanity.
So zealous in its goals was Meandor's new faction, that even its followers considered it a religion, hence the name “Cult of Storms.”
The Cult, originally a faction of Dark Elves,
employed any means necessary to inflict havoc upon the usurping Human race, which held the Valley of Wonders and grew steadily in power.
Meandor also claimed that he should be the rightful heir supreme, as by Elven custom the rightful heir is traditionally named in the 1000th year of a king’s rule. But Inioch's early death was unprecedented, and most Elves feared Meandor—even those few who follow him fear the outward changes in his appearance and the company he keeps. Many Elves looked to Julia as a true heir. Thus, Meandor declared that the fair surface Elves have committed high treason and vowed to see them all punished.
Despite their objectives, the Cult of Storms did not have success in become a major power over the next two century. In 1101 LIR, Melenis, Meandor's favorite wife, activated the Horn of the Dead, unleashing the Undead on the surface world. While the Undead would eventually be beaten back by the Highmen and an alliance of races (which the Dark Elves notably did not join), this event would have a lasting effect on the Cult of Storms. Over time, Meandor saw the Undead more favorably. In 1164 LIR, the Cult agreed to a number of non-aggression treaties with the Undead, and Necromancers begin to hold influence among the Cult of Storms.
Resurrecting Inioch
In 1216 LIR, a star appeared over the Valley of Wonders, a phenomenon seen by sages as a sign of changes to come. Within days of the star's appearance, Meandor was seen walking among the shadows of Inioch’s ruined Court, plotting the resurrection of Inioch and the rule of a new world order that would include the extermination of the Humans.
Julia, who had become leader of the Keepers, gathered her forces and set off for the Valley of Wonders to rebut whatever conspiracy her half-brother had in mind. Unsuspectingly, Julia had already fallen for the first part of Meandor’s plan to cripple the Keepers.
While Julia was away from Aldor, Meandor's and the Cult of Storms successfully assassinated Elwyn in Aldor, with the use of Goblin tunnels that had been dug in advance.
As Keeper and Cult forces battled each other to find allies to retake the Valley of Wonders, Meandor resurrected Inioch at a mountain formation known as "The Skull."
Although the Cult had intended to resurrect Inioch under the belief that Inioch was the greatest Elven lord,
Inioch did not ally with Meandor. Instead, Inioch unleashed the Undead across the land, seeking to turn all life, Elves and Dark Elves included, into Undead under his rule,
while also seeking to regain his throne at the Valley of Wonders.
Realizing that a mistake was made, Meandor retreated into hiding.
Battle for the Valley of Wonders
Inioch's return placed Meandor in a state of torment. His nights were fitful and his days were filled with visions only that he could see. Inioch sent Undead forces to capture Meandor, but those forces were repelled. When Meandor's torment finally stopped, Meandor ceased to hope for his father.
Instead, Meandor and Cult allies gathered their forces, including a number of Dark Elves who had risen from their underground cities, to faces the forces of the Keepers, Highmen, Humans, and the Undead.
However, at the battle for the Valley of Wonders, Meandor and his forces met defeat, and Meandor was slain,
while Julia's Keepers emerged as victors. Although Meandor's spirit would ling on after his death in mortal form, Meandor's defeat resulted in the dissolution of the Cult of Storms.

Name
By default, the Stormlord protagonist is a Dark Elf named Talic. However, the player is free to customize the protagonist's name and race, as well as the protagonist's portrait, starting stats, attributes, and selections of magic spheres (by way of
leader customization
). If customized, the protagonist's race can be Dark Elf, Orc, Goblin, Azrac, or Lizardman.
Unlike the Keeper protagonist, whose name appears in the Dark Elves route of the
final scenario
of campaign, the default name of "Talic" is not referenced in any other part of the game or in any other media of the series. In all versions of the final scenario other than the Dark Elves route,
Meandor
's second is the Orc hero Gadran. Thus, the only circumstance in which a hero by the name of Talic can appear is if that name is selected by the player. Furthermore, unlike how the Keeper protagonist is referred to as "the Keeper" in the Cult of Storms campaign, no equivalently phrased reference exists for the Stormlord protagonist exists in the Keepers campaign.
The development team decided not to explicitly reference the protagonist of the first game, due to the variability of the character.
Background and personality
Little is mentioned of the Stormlord protagonist's background, except for being a member of the Stormlord knew
Melenis
, whom the former regards as "my favorite."
The relationship between the two is left ambiguous as to whether it was friendship or romantic.
In the Undead route of the campaign, the protagonist refers to Melenis as "my dear Melenis."
In this route, the Stormlord defects from Meandor and joins
Inioch
, who grants the former's wish for Melenis to be brought back. However, Inioch brings her back as a undead lich, which results in the journal entry, "How could Inioch grant what I wanted, yet be so cruel?...How can I dispose of this horrific gift?" The resurrection of Melenis and the plot elementing of using her to lure the Keeper protagonist into a trap at the Wizard's college ties into the Keepers campaign. This gives rise to a possible interpretation that at least one Stormlord (potentially, but not necessarily, the default Stormlord of the Cult campaign) defected to Inioch during the events of the Keepers campaign. This notion is consistent with the fact that when playing as the Keepers, Meandor's second hero in the campaign finale is an Orc and not a Dark Elf.
The Stormlord protagonist displays an arrogant personality in the game's story text, as seen by, for example, the statements "only I am worthy to wield the just blade in the Valley of Wonders" (in the campaign's introduction) and "Am I the only Stormlord of ability?" (upon learning that Melenis was defeated by the Keepers).
The protagonist also highly values strength, calling Meandor "cowardly" in the Undead route of the campaign,
and similarly referring to Gabriel as a "coward" in the Highmen route.
