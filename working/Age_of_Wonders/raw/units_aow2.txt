Units
in
Age of Wonders: The Wizard's Throne
are entities that can move, capture structures, engage in combat, and perform other interactions and gameplay functions.
Overview
Wizards, heroes, and standard units
For details on wizards and heroes, see:
Wizard
,
Hero
, and
Hero library (Age of Wonders II)
Units in
Age of Wonders II: The Wizard's Throne
fall into three general categories: wizards, heroes, and standard (i.e., normal) units.
All units are subject to the
unit statistics and combat mechanics
system.
All units can
move
on the overland and combat maps.
All units can use
abilities
.
Heroes and standard units have
unit morale
.
Heroes and standard units can
gain experience.
Heroes have a specific class-based hero-leveling system, whereas standard units have two attainable experience levels (silver and gold) with fixed benefits.
Wizards cannot gain experience or level up. Wizards are central to additional game mechanics, including the
domain
system and the use of wizard towers.
Wizards and heroes can equip
items
. Standard units cannot equip them, but can still teleport items on the same hex to a hero or wizard.
Wizards specialize in casting spells. Heroes have limited spell casting ability. Standard units cannot cast spells, but often have abilities that can replicate some of the functions of spells.
Each
race
has a roster of trainable units, including race-specific units and units common to multiple races. Stats of standard units are listed below on this page. For stats of wizards and heroes, please see the articles linked above.
Acquiring standard units
Units can be produced in
cities
when the requisite structure has been built.
Summoning spells can summon units at the expense of mana.
Friendly units may offer to join your cause when approached. Units in stacks labeled as "refugees" will join at no cost.
Units can be acquired from certain structures on the world map.
Enemy units can be captured in combat by unit-capturing abilities, such as dominate.
Unit attributes displayed on this page

. Movement points are given along with an icon corresponding to a movement ability:
- The unit has Walking only
- The unit has Forestry in addition to Walking
- The unit has Mountaineering in addition to Walking
- The unit has Cave Crawling in addition to Walking
- The unit has Flying (
) or Floating (
Flt.
)
- The unit has Swimming in addition to Walking (

Sail.

Combat Abilities
- The unit's Combat Abilities, which are abilities that are actively used during combat.
