The
Cult of Storms
is an order of retributionists, originally composed of Dark Elves, dedicated to the destruction of humanity as the primary means for reclaiming the Valley of Wonders following the fall of the
Verdant Kingdom
. The Cult was founded by
Meandor
and became a rival faction to the
Keepers
. Members of the Cult of Storms are called "Stormlords."
In the
campaign mode
of
Age of Wonders
, the Cult provides one of two selectable options for the campaign's narrative perspective, namely the
Cult of Storms campaign
.
History
The Cult of Storms originated from a secret faction of the
Elven Court
known as the "Dark Elves." During
Inioch
's rule, the Dark Elves were skeptical of the Keepers' policy of allowing Humans to settle in the Valley of Wonders. Thus, the Dark Elves resisted through political opposition and instigation of tensions between the Humans and other races in the Verdant Kingdom. These actions, however, only led to further instability of the Kingdom, which, in turn, contributed to its downfall when the Humans under Inioch rebelled, destroyed the Elven Court, and captured the Valley of Wonders.
After the fall of the Elven Court, Meandor, who had allied with the Dark Elves, founded the Cult of Storms in 985 LIR. This new faction was dedicated to the destruction of humanity and reclaiming the Valley of Wonders.
So zealous were its goals that its followers considered it a religion, hence the name “Cult of Storms.”
The Cult employed any means necessary to inflict havoc upon the usurping Human race, which held the Valley of Wonders and grew steadily in power.
Membership in the Cult was originally drawn from the Dark Elves,
who over time became a people distinct from and hostile to the fair Elves. Meandor served as the leader of both the Cult and the Dark Elves. The Cult became a rival to the Keepers, who still believed in forging harmonious race relations, and were supported by fair Elves under Queen
Elwyn
.
Despite its goals, the Cult did not achieve any notable military success against the Humans or its rivals Elven factions. However, in 1101 LIR, the Stormlord Melenis caused considerable disruption to the continent by using the
Horn of the Dead
, which caused Undead forces to attack all races on the continent's surface. Although the Undead were eventually driven back by the Keepers and the Highmen, this event had a lasting effect on the Cult. Over time, the Cult became more favorable to the Undead. By 1164 LIR, necromancers began holding influence among the Cult. Presumably inspired by the Undead's influence, Meandor sought to resurrect Inioch, whom the Cult regarded as the greatest elven lord.
Battle for the Valley of Wonders
In 1216 LIR, after a mysterious star appeared over the Valley of Wonders, the Cult assassinated Elwyn. Shortly afterwards, Meandor completed the resurrection of Inioch. However, the risen Inioch did not ally with Meandor. Instead, Inioch sought to capture him and turn all living beings, including the Dark Elves, into Undead and to recapture the Valley of Wonders for himself. The Cult, although weakened by the loss of Melenis at the hands of the Keepers, managed to gather a force of Goblins, Orcs, and Dark Elves to fight for Meandor at the Valley. There, they were opposed by the Inioch's Undead, Julia's Keepers, the Highmen, and the Humans. In the resulting multi-sided battle, Julia's forces emerged as victors over all others, including those of Meandor, who was slain during the battle.
After Meandor's defeat at the Valley, the Cult disintegrated.
Although Meandor would return after death to take part in affairs on the continent, the Cult did not rise again.

Keepers insignia
The
Keepers
are an order of scholars and peacekeepers formed during the reign of
Inioch
. After the fall of Inioch's
kingdom
at the hands of rebel Humans, the Keepers attempted to reestablish peace among the races of the continent, and were opposed by the newly formed
Cult of Storms
.
In the
campaign mode
of
Age of Wonders
,
the Keepers provide one of two selectable options for the campaign's narrative perspective, namely the
Keepers campaign
.
History
The Keepers were created by Inioch when the Highmen first arrived at his court. The Highmen warned that the Humans will soon arrive on the Blessed Continent,
and wished for the Elves the Elves depart to Evermore so that the Humans may thrive freely on the continent without the constraints of Inioch's rule.
However, Inioch refused to cede the Blessed Continent to the Humans. Instead, he intended to incorporate them into his kingdom. In doing so, he formed the Keepers, who were given the task of maintaining peace among the races in his realm.
The Keepers were hailed as the "Fruit of the Elves" and as a symbol of all that was good and right about Elven rule.
However, Inioch had hidden the official communications between his court and the Highmen, keeping their requests to himself. Thus, the Keepers carried out their mission of maintaining order without being informed of the Highmen's intentions of establishing humanity's dominion over the Blessed Continent.
Even centuries later, prominent Keepers considered the Highmen to be enigmatic, and were not aware of the Highmen's involvement with the Humans.
When the Humans finally arrived at the Valley of Wonders, the Keepers allowed them to settle in the Valley over the objections of other elements in the Elven Court who feared the Humans. However, due to multiple factors, including the strictness of Inioch's rule, hardships faced by the Human colonies, and political agitation from a secret faction in the Elven Court known as the "Dark Elves," the Humans eventually rebelled, slaying Inioch and capturing the Valley of Wonders in the process. This traumatic event in Elven history led to the formation of the Cult of Storms from the ranks of the Dark Elves, dedicated to the destruction of humanity.
After the fall of the Elven Court, the Keepers attempted to establish peaceful relations with the Humans through diplomacy, but were without success. After many failed attempts at diplomacy, the Keepers discovered that one rationale behind Human mistrust of the fair Elves was because they believe all Elves, including the Dark Elves, to be the same. In an attempt to prove their differences, the Keepers stood against the Dark Elves in open war. However, the Humans only took advantage of the situation to further expand their territory.
Driven from the Valley of Wonders, the Keepers found refugee at the court of
Elwyn
, who had fled the Elven Court and settled with her followers in Aldor. With the resources of Elwyn and the Elves of Aldor, the Keepers were able to assert their influence on the continent, such as by forming alliances against the Azracs, and later the Undead. Elwyn's daughter Julia joined the Keepers, eventually becoming their leader in 1204 LIR.
Battle for the Valley of Wonders
In 1216 LIR, after a mysterious star appeared over the Valley of Wonders, Julia set out to retake the Valley. While she was away, the Cult of Storm successfully assassinated Elwyn in Aldor. Though weakened by the loss of Elwyn, the Keepers managed to rally a force of Halflings, Dwarves, and Elves, which they brought to reinforce Julia at the Valley of Wonder. There, Julia and the Keepers triumphed over all other forces, including those of Meandor, Inioch (who had been resurrected as an Undead), and the Humans.
The Highmen, who had placed the star above the Valley, also conceded and removed the star, but condescendingly entrusted the Keepers to "guide" the destiny of the Humans. The remaining other races sued for peace, and the Keepers vowed to rebuild and share the Valley according to Julia's vision.
The Second Age
More than three centuries after the Keepers' victory at the Valley of Wonders, the Keepers continued to play a role in Julia's kingdom. However, when rogue Wizards of the
Circle of Evermore
brought cataclysms and destruction to the continent, the Keepers were defenseless against this new magic.

The
Verdant Kingdom
(also known by other names such as the
Elven Kingdom
,
the
Blessed Kingdom
,
and the
Northern Kingdom
) was a historical kingdom ruled by the wizard-king
Inioch
.
History
The Verdant Kingdom was governed by the
Elven Court
based in the Valley of Wonders. Inioch reigned over the Kingdom and the Elven Court as emperor for 970 years, until rebel Human settlers sacked the Elven Court and captured the key parts of the Valley.
At its height, the Verdant Kingdom was the preeminent kingdom on the
Blessed Continent
, ruling over multiple races, including the Dwarves who served as sentinels for the Valley. Inioch entrusted the the task of preserving order and maintaining peace among the races to the
Keepers
.
However, when Humans settled in the Valley of Wonders at the permission of the Keepers, Inioch and the Keepers were unable to maintain political and social stability within his Kingdom.
Eventually, the Humans rebelled, sacking the Elven Court and killing Inioch in the process. Inioch's death marked the end of the Verdant Kingdom, after which Elven civilization on the continent fractured into multiple centers of powers, including the Elves of
Aldor
led by
Elwyn
, the Dark Elves led by
Meandor
, and the independent Elves of Silvanus.
