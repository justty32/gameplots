Akinah
Drawing by

Type
City
Location

Akinah
, also known as
The Rock of Secrets
,
The Void's Playground
, and by dozens of other unknown names, is a city in Aimia, an island west of the mainland of
Roshar
. The city was built among natural stone formations, presumably sometime during or before the
Heraldic Epochs
. Its underlying stone pattern exhibits a
ten
part divine symmetry, accentuated when the city is viewed from above.
The city plans are found in the
Palanaeum
and were a basis for a study into the natural symmetry of world cities. It is one of the standard four cities within which patterns can be reproduced with a
cymatic plate
.
Events
In
Oathbringer
,
Kaza
and a group of sailors approach the island to uncover its hidden secrets. Soon after, every member is poisoned by
cook
,  who is a member of the
Sleepless
. She poisons everyone to protect Akinah from the discovery of men.

Azir
Type
Nation/Kingdom
Location
Roshar
Inhabitants
Azish
Azir
is a nation/kingdom in south-western
Roshar
. Ethnically
Makabaki
, the people from Azir are known as Azish. Azir is descended of
Makabakam
, the largest of the
Silver Kingdoms
.
The
Bronze Palace
in Azir is located in the capital city of Azimir.
Azir has been suggested to be warmer than
Alethkar
, but cooler than
Iri
.
Azir is the location of one of the
ten
Oathgates
.
Legends there say that those who consume Azish
truthberries
speak only the truth until the next sunset.
The
viziers
of Azir have explained that although Azir might
claim
to be an empire, and most of the other countries in the region play along with this, as soon as demands therein grow to be too extravagant, the others might not necessarily obey.
Azimir
Azimir is larger in population than
Kholinar
.
Azish
Appearance
Folio: Azish Public Servant Designs
Drawing by
Dan dos Santos
Azish have dark brown skin and black hair and speak with a smooth accent.
The inscription on the image reads as follows:
"This folio page shows contemporary designs out of Azir, using local models. Though these are specifically male civil servant outfits, the styles have deeply influenced all Azish fashion."
Lift
notes that important Azish folk wear big, drooping coats with really wide sleeves and patterns that argue with each other. She compares them to children who had snuck into their parents' wardrobe, but she thinks their hats to be 'nifty'.
The Azish are normally quite proper, overly encumbered with robes and hats.
Regal robes of Azish patterns weigh
Prime Aqasix
Yanagawn
down, along with the Imperial Yuanazixin: a fancy hat with sweeping sides.
Characteristics
Lift states that people in Azir
love
to write stuff down ... and they love information. That they love trading it with the people of
Tashikk
.
Wyndle
notes that this is a distinguishing factor of their cultural identity.
Lift thinks that everyone in Azir doesn't shut up about the patterns in their clothing.
Religion
The Azish worship
Yaezir
,
Kadasix
of Kings, and hold in high esteem the Kadasixes, or
Heralds
, and their spoken word.
Governance
Azir's ruler is the Prime Aqasix, who is elected by the viziers of the nation based on the applications they receive from those interested in the position. In the case of Gawx, the viziers determined that he had submitted the best application by performing the miracle of Regrowth (actually Lift's doing) and was thus named Prime.
“
"He was dead and he returned. What better application could we ask for?"
”
Although the Azish have a large bureaucracy, a change in government could cause upheaval.
Renarin
thinks it might be easier for the new Azish emperor to change policy, since policy is well defined enough to change.
Navani
believes the Azish will listen to reason, because they always do.
Notable Azish
Falksi
- One of
Janala Lustow
's handmaidens.
Sigzil
- A
Worldsinger
and
bridgeman
in
Bridge Four
.
Tigzikk
- An aging thief and an accomplice of Lift's.
Huqin
- Supposedly a master thief and an accomplice of Lift's.
Gawx - Former thief and accomplice of Lift's, now Prime Aqasix, Ruler of Azir.
Maxin
- A young thief and an accomplice of Lift's.
Noura
- A vizier in the Bronze Palace.
Dalksi
- A vizier amongst the Azir.
Unoqua
- A
scion
of Azir.
Warren
- A
Skybreaker
.

Celebrant
Type
City
Location
Shadesmar
Inhabitants
Spren
Celebrant
is a city in Shadesmar. It isn't as densely populated as most human cities, but the variety of spren there is stunning.
Reachers
like
Ico
and his sailors are common, but there are also spren that look much like
Adolin
's
sword
, at least before she'd been killed. They are made entirely of vines, though they have crystal hands and wear human clothing. Equally common are spren with inky black skin that shine with a variety of colors when light hits them right. Their clothing seems part of them, like that of the
Cryptics
and
honorspren
.
There are other spren with skin like cracked stone, molten light shining from within. Still others have skin the color of old white ashes - and when
Kaladin
saw one of these point toward something, the skin stretching at the joint of its arm disintegrated and blew away, revealing the joint and knobs of the humerus. The skin then quickly regrew.
The variety reminds Kaladin of the costumes of the
Cult of Moments
- though he doesn't spot a single honorspren. And it doesn't seem to him like the other spren mix much. Humans there are rare enough that he - and
Syl
, imitating an
Alethi
- turn heads.
Kaladin observes that there are no children in the city. Syl tells him that there have been a few, but that they just don't look little, like human children.
Description
Adolin describes it as a city on an island, which is actually a lake in the
Physical Realm
called the
Sea of Spears
, in the southeast of
Alethkar
by the ruins of
Rathalas
.
Pattern
describes it as a prominent city. That in it, they could find passage wherever they wished to go.
Buildings are constructed using bricks in a variety of colors or blocks of many different types of stone. Each building is a hodgepodge of materials with no pattern Kaladin can determine.
When he asks how they get building materials, and whether there quarries on this side, Syl tells him that she's not sure, but that she thinks maybe the spren make it appear on this side, somehow, from the other side (i.e., the Physical Realm), perhaps like Ico did with ice.
Towers in the city's center show the time by way of fires burning in a set of vertical holes.
Market
The market is composed of mostly roofless stalls open to the air, with goods piled on tables. Even in comparison to the improvised market of
Urithiru
, this seems ephemeral to Kaladin.
In a merchant stall, Kaladin found sitting in a locked, glass-topped box, a long thin silvery chain, for sale for a thousand
broams
.
*
He looked down at the box, which was locked to the table and guarded by a small orange spren that looked like people.
There is also a Returned painting
**
- described as triangles of red, black and gray - in a merchant stall in the market, but just how it came to be there remains unknown.
Fashion
Adolin observes that the spren seem to wear whatever. He points out an Alethi officer's coat over an
Azish
scribe
's vest, a
Tashikki
wrap worn with trousers, and almost a full
Thaylen
tlmko
, without the boots.
Notes
*
The price on this chain is likely as high as it is because it is made of
Threnodite
silver, which is extremely valuable there given the world's
Cognitive Shadows
.
**
The paintings are how
Endowment
gets messages to the Returned. Someone of the Third Heightening has enough Breath that when they are in the throes of the creative muse, the Shard can channel into them, and they can paint a message into their art which someone with a Divine Breath is
Connected
enough to receive.
Whether the painting in Celebrant is the same as that observed in
Warbreaker
remains unknown.

Jah Keved
Type
Kingdom
Location
Roshar
Inhabitants
Veden
Jah Keved
is one of the five
Vorin kingdoms
, west of
Alethkar
. Its capital is
Vedenar
. It is also the second-largest kingdom on Roshar.
During the
Heraldic Epochs
, the region was part of the former kingdom
Valhav
.
Geography
Jah Keved is situated in the eastern half of the continent, additionally bordering
Herdaz
,
Tu Bayla
and
Triax
. The
Reshi
and
Tarat
seas hold it to the north and south. It has two centrally located mountain ranges: the
Horneater Peaks
- home to the
Unkalaki
(Horneaters) - and
Bavland
, a small mining region.
Settlements
Vedenar is the capital city of Jah Keved.
Valath
, the city's 'Holy Enclave', holds one of the finest collection of tomes and scrolls on Roshar.
Northgrip
Elanar
Ironsway
Silnasen
Characteristics
Jah Keved, along with Alethkar, is said to possess the most
Shardblades
(around 20 each). They are also the only known nation to possess the
Half shard
, a
fabrial
-powered shield capable of deflecting a Shardblade.
Veden People
The people native to Jah Keved are known as Veden. They are characterized by pale skin, reddish hair, violet eyes,
and are considered to be very formal around
ardents
.
Characteristics
Women do not change their last names when they marry in or outside Jah Keved.
Vedens do not change their names when they re marrying, regardless. It might be different for the royal family.

Purelake
Type
Lake
Location
Roshar
Inhabitants
Purelakers
The Purelake
is an immense lake, the largest inland body of water in
Roshar
. It is located in the heart of the continent, in the land of the former
Epoch Kingdom
,
Sela Tales
.
The people of the Purelake are ethnically Selay, however, they refer to themselves as Purelakers.
“
The Purelake was life itself.
”
–
Ishikk
Geography
The Purelake is hundreds of miles wide with a glassy and perfectly transparent surface. At its deepest, it is never more than six feet from the bottom and, in most places, it is pleasantly warm: low-moving water comes up only to about mid-calf. It is filled with tiny fish, colorful
cremlings
, and eel-like
riverspren
.
The earth beneath it is occasionally rocky or uneven, but slowly walking across it is easy and commonly done.
According to
Zahel
, it is ridiculously shallow, like an endless bay, mere feet deep, the water warm, lulled by calm breezes.
The Purelake drains into pits and holes during
highstorms
.  Purelakers are able to take shelter in crevices between ridges of stone during the storms, which are weaker than they are in the east.
Characteristics
It never gets cold in the Purelake, except during
highstorms
, and one can last through those right well,
Nu Ralik
send the way.
The Purelake drains into pits and holes when the storms come, and so one just shoves one's raft into a crevice between two ridges of stone and huddles up next to it, using it to break the fury of the tempest.
The storms aren't as bad here as they are in the east, where they fling boulders and blow down buildings.
To
Szeth
, the lake seems to extend forever, wide as an ocean. His steps within it startle schools of fish. Gnarled trees poke from the shallows, gorging themselves on the water while their roots grasp the many holes and furrows in the lake bed. Rock outcrops break the lake near the coast, but inward the Purelake is placid, more empty.
Purelakers
The Purelakers have adapted over time and, as such, have special feet to fit their situation. Most natural selection does not work on the timescale of the
cosmere
so there probably have to be some magical foundations for this. The fact that everyone on Roshar is
Invested
with a bit of Investiture more than average is going to push people over time in a way.
Intent
and such things are so important  cosmerelogically that evolution occurs on a faster scale in most of the cosmere. Readers can see this in the adaptations that have happened since the history of Roshar itself, the arrival of humans on Roshar, and other such happenstance.
Characteristics
Purelakers never cover their shoulders when the sun is shining.
Purelakers like it when the floors of their cottages are low enough that a few inches of water still cover them. It's natural, though if the tide drops, sometimes buildings will drain.
Society
It is pre-industrial with a low population.
Notes
Also according to Brandon, there are parallels between the fish in the Purelake and the
Aviar
in
Sixth of the Dusk
.
The Purelake was originally called the Everlake. Then, Brandon just didn't like that. He decided that since it's really shallow, that's the cool thing about it.
Brandon has said that the Horneater Lake is the same as that one in
Elantris
.

Taravangian
.
Kharbranth is considered a center of academic life on
Roshar
. It possesses the most extensive library in the world, known as the
Palanaeum
. Kharbranth is also well known for its extensive hospital system in which none are turned away and aid is given to all who are in need. The hospitals are funded by the king from the dues charged for entry into the Palanaeum.
The city is built upon the side of a steep incline and is wedge-shaped, as if it is built into a wide crack, with the open side toward the ocean. The buildings are blocky with square windows, and appear to have been constructed of some kind of mud or daub. They are painted bright colors, reds and oranges most often, but occasionally blues and yellows too. Many bells, both great and small, are situated throughout the city, and some people hang them near their homes.
Kharbranth is a free city. A small, politically fragile city-state, it holds little territory but has docks open to all ships that pass, and it asks no questions about nationality or status. People flock to it.
In Kharbranth - which doesn't wage war - a mild, congenial figurehead makes sense. The city is supposed to be unassuming, unthreatening.
Dalinar
has heard people joke that in Kharbranth, one had to hang a bell outside to know when a
highstorm
had arrived, because otherwise one would miss it.
The city's slightly southern orientation prevented such devastation from the
Everstorm
as was experienced in
Thaylen City
.

When initially there, neither Lift nor Wyndle can see smoke trails in the alleys; only the occasional pinprick of

Lift notices that people within the city keep their spheres in rows, strung on long strings, even if they put them in pouches. All the spheres of currency she sees have holes in their bottoms just so one could do that.
Lift does not see many beggars in the city.
She observes that people there
love
to write things down. And, that they love information, and trading it with one another, to which Wyndle agrees, indicating that such information would be kept in a safe place.
The seasons have gone insane. It is supposed to be raining constantly, but it hasn't rained for days.

shiquas

Anyone can come and cook in the communal ovens. (The people can't have fires in their own places. Early in the city's life, a fire had blazed through various slums and killed
tons

rockbud

People in Yeddaw keep their spheres in rows, strung on long strings, even if they put them in pouches. All the currency Lift sees has holes in the bottoms of the glass spheres. She wonders how the people count exact change, but is relieved that they use spheres.
Further, the people there expect some measure of jostling as they move through the streets.
Children at their lessons sit in rows and listen to a teacher while doing some menial task, like sewing, to pay for the education.
Some of the people Lift notices have
parshmen
slaves.
Having survived the
Everstorm
