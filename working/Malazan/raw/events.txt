The
Aren Rebellion
occurred around 1152/1153
BS
when
Kellanved
was still Emperor of the
Malazan Empire
.
The revolt in the
Holy City
of
Aren
was put down by the
Logros
T'lan Imass
and led to a mass slaughter of its citizens. The victims of the slaughter were buried in great barrow mounds running parallel to the eastern side of the
Aren Way
.
The
Tanno
guard captain
Turqa
said "the slaughter at Aren revealed the Empire's madness", and it led to
Kimloc
's peaceful capitulation of
Karakarang
. The
Spiritwalker
did not want to subject his people to the same loss of life. The harsh response to the revolt was generally considered a mistake and an embarrassment by the soldiers of the
Malazan Military
.
In
Deadhouse Gates
Apsalar
still retained
Cotillion
's memories after being possessed by the
god
in the guise of
Sorry
. She recalled from his thoughts that the violent response at Aren appeared to have been instigated by
Surly
during the period in which she served as Imperial Regent while
Dancer
and Kellanved were away exploring the
Azath Houses
--although no written orders were ever found. After the slaughter was over Dancer was sent to Aren to investigate, resulting in an argument between the two.
In
Memories of Ice
Dujek revealed to
Whiskeyjack
that the T'lan Imass slaughter at Aren by the Logros T'lan Imass had not been what it seemed.  It was not Surly who ordered the massacre, but Emperor Kellanved who was not quite as dead as it was believed.  Dujek claimed the insane former Emperor had returned to the
First Throne
and ordered the slaughter to "wreak vengeance on Laseen, to shake her grip on the Empire."
In
The Bonehunters
Barathol Mekhar
was reviled as the villain of the Aren uprising. The
Red Blade
officer had murdered the
Fist
of Aren and been sentenced to be crucified on Aren Way. Before his punishment could be carried out the city rose in rebellion in his name, slaughtering the Malzan
Garrison
and driving the Red Blades out of the city. When the Empire's T'lan Imass arrived to put down the populace, Barathol was spotted opening the north gate to them. Thousands of citizens were killed in the slaughter that followed and Barathol's name carried ten thousand curses. Many sought to slay him in revenge following him all the way to
Karashimesh
before he killed the last and was able to disappear. But popular perception of his action at the gate was mistaken. As Barathol argued, what need did the T'lan Imass have of an open gate?
In
Reaper's Gale
Gesler
had been present for the rebellion and recalled how the Logros T'lan Imass had risen from the dust of the streets to cut down the city's citizens with their stone weapons. Gesler and the other soldiers could only stand and watch. If not for a former Red Blade who had opened the city's gates to open a path to escape, none would have survived except for the Malazan occupiers.
Gesler's recollection put Barathol's actions at the gate into new light.
Trivia
The truth of who ordered the T'lan Imass attack during the Aren Rebellion is obscured by several contradictory tales told across the Malazan series.  During an author Q&A, a fan asked
Steven Erikson
if the true story would ever be revealed. Erikson replied, "Will we ever clear up the Aren events? No, why should we? Those were confused times and I assure you, both
Cam
and I share in that confusion."

The
Awl Campaign
was Factor
Letur Anict
of
Drene
's attempt to finally exterminate the
Awl
after
Redmask
returned to lead his people against their
Letherii
oppressors. The Factor had engaged in a years long effort to seize Awl lands and herds in the
Awl'dan
for his own benefit and to expand the reach of the
Letherii Empire
. Those few Awl that remained had gathered under the banner of
Hadralt
, who the Factor had co-opted with coin and poor quality arms and armour, as well as promises of a personal estate and slaves.
When Redmask returned from exile to lead the Awl against the invaders, Factor Anict's plans were thwarted and he authorised
Atri-Preda
Bivatt
to lead the
Eastern Army
into the Awl'dan to eliminate the Awl once and for all.
Brohl Handar
, the Factor's ostensible
Tiste Edur
overlord, demanded that the campaign be targeted only against Awl who had slain Letherii settlers and that the Awl be given a chance to concede rather than face indiscriminate slaughter. But the Letherii would accept no less than the Awl's complete surrender and enslavement.
Battle of Bast Fulmar
See also:
Battle of Bast Fulmar
The first battle of the Awl Campaign took place at the valley of
Bast Fulmar
. The incautious Letherii marched on Redmask's position confident that their overwhelming numbers and unmatched sorcery would easily carry the day. But the Awl warleader had chosen the battle site for its unusual anti-magic properties that eliminated the Letherii mages and the Edur
K'risnan
as a threat. This surprise, as well as innovative tactics and the unexpected terror caused by Redmask's personal
K'Chain Che'Malle
guardians, led to a rout of the Letherii forces.
Battle of Pradegar
See also:
Battle of Pradegar
After Redmask's initial success, the Awl disappeared into the grasslands as the Letherii desperately sought to bring them to battle. The Awl kept the Letherii blind, ambushing their scouts and taking their weapons and horses. Bivatt refused to increase the scale of her losses by sending out patrols in greater force insisting that Redmask would grow impatient and launch an attack on her still superior forces. The Atri-Preda's strategy appeared to bear fruit on the plains of
Pradegar
. Rather than immediately attacking, Redmask telegraphed his intentions allowing the Letherii column to draw into defensive positions. When the Awl finally attacked in the dark, they saw large numbers of casualties due to the unleashed Letherii sorcery.
Battle of Q'uson Tapi
See also:
Battle of Q'uson Tapi
Both Redmask and Bivatt sought a final battle to end the war. Bivatt's situation was complicated by the disruption of her supply line back to Drene, although she was unaware of the civil disorder taking place there. Her soldiers were starving and needed the campaign to end.
Meanwhile, Redmask was sure that he was undefeatable, a chosen one of the K'Chain Che'Malle.
The battle took place on the
Q'uson Tapi
salt plain where Redmask forced the Letherii troops to slog through the mud to his readied positions. Superior Letherii arms, armour, and numbers eventually turned the tide and the Awl were crushed. Redmask himself was slain by his K'Chain Che'Malle guardians for failing them.
But Bivatt could not exult in her victory as one-third of her own army was slain as well. And even as the fighting was winding down, a third force appeared on the scene eager to slay both the Awl and the Letherii.
Onos T'oolan
and his seventy thousand
White Face
Barghast
sought to avenge the
Grey Swords
, who had been betrayed by the Awl and killed by the Letherii.
In the end, the Awl people were effectively annihilated and the Eastern Army destroyed. Only a handful of Awl children survived as Onos T'oolan's favour to his friend, Toc Anaster, who had died saving them from the Letherii.
And the war's instigator, Factor Letur Anict, was assassinated in Drene by the
Liberty Consign of Merchants
, who thought he had gone too far.

"
Bast Fulmar sings this day. It sings: there is no magic. There is no magic!

―The Awl
The
Battle of Bast Fulmar
was the first major conflict in the
Awl Campaign
after
Redmask
united the
Awl
against the
Letherii Empire
. Under Factor
Letur Anict
of
Drene
, the
Letherii
had engaged in a years-long strategy to conquer the lands of the
Awl'dan
and subjugate its people using theft, subterfuge, and outright extermination.
The Factor's plot to buy off
Hadralt
, the Awl's last major warleader, collapsed when Redmask returned from exile to lead his people in their darkest hour.
Redmask launched the fighting with a raid on one of Factor Anict's drover camps, killing all the Letherii and their families who tended thousands of the Awl's stolen
Myrid
and
Rodara
. In response, the Factor sent
Atri-Preda
Bivatt
and the
Eastern Army
to kill Redmask and shatter his army.
Overseer
Brohl Handar
, Emperor
Rhulad Sengar
's representative and the Factor's theoretical
Tiste Edur
superior, accompanied and funded the expedition.
He hoped to end the fighting quickly with minimal bloodshed and seek terms with the Awl. Bivatt and the Factor would accept nothing less than the Awl's complete surrender and enslavement.
Bivatt was confident of her army's success as the Awl's undisciplined fighting style and their intra-clan feuds and rivalries made them ill-suited for any organised combat beyond raids and ambushes. The Letherii's mastery of unit tactics, combined with their powerful sorcery and their superior armour and weaponry, had proven insurmountable against the Awl in the past. The Letherii had only to force the Awl to stand and fight and the enemy would be annihilated.
Internal politics involving the Factor's corruption and the Overseer's threat to expose and remove him meant that the Overseer's safety on the battlefield was not guaranteed even in his own camp.
The Battlefield
Bast Fulmar
was a grassy valley running roughly north to south with broad, gentle inclines. The long-dead river that once ran through was now only hardpacked sand, but it had carved a broad curved bend on its western side. The eastern slope was more undulating with a bulge at its centre. The valley's northern end narrowed to a defile below a bluff while the south offered a wide approach. The floor of the valley was wide and flat.
The summit of the valley's eastern ridgeline was largely bare rock overlooking another valley where the Awl made their camp a third of a league distant. Visibility was high within the valley, with no features beyond the rolling hills to block lines of sight.
But once down in the valley, the ridgelines on either side prevented sight outside the immediate area.
Seven narrow, shallow drainage channels ribboned the valley's flanks, separating those areas into segments. Three were in the valley's southwestern quadrant, two in the centre-east of the valley, and one each in the northeastern and southeastern quadrants.
The Awl established no pickets around their camp allowing Bivatt, Handar, and a small troop of scouts to observe them the night before the battle. Based on the campfires, Bivatt estimated the Awl numbers at ten to eleven thousand warriors and an untold number of non-combatants.
In the final two bells before dawn, she stole a march on the Awl in an attempt to enter the valley and array her troops so that Redmask would lose the initiative. But the Letherii army was still a half-league from the valley when their scouts discovered the Awl already there in strength.
Spear-wielding Awl foot soldiers formed in five distinct wedges, each with a thousand warriors, on the northern end of the valley's eastern slope.
To their south was a chaotic mass of mounted horse-archers, kicking up dust that obscured their numbers.
The Letherii's
Dresh
ballistae were mounted on the bluff above the defile on the valley's northern side where they could not be assaulted from the valley floor. Bivatt anchored her line on this position, placing her troops along the western ridge in a line running south. She intended to sweep down into the valley and flank the Awl from the south. Then she would push them north into the chokepoint of the defile where they would be cut down from behind, devastated by artillery, and trampled in their own panic.
Directly south of the ballistae, on the Letherii's left flank was a shield-wall of fifteen hundred
Medium infantry
from the
Crimson Rampant Brigade
followed by a sawtooth formation of the brigade's thousand
Heavy infantry
.  To their right were three half-legions of heavies from the
Artisan Battalion
screened in front by a thousand
Light infantry
Skirmishers
of the
Harridict Brigade
. Three companies of
Bluerose Lancers
waited behind the Artisan Battalion ready to sweep around the ballistae from the north and bottle up any Awl caught in the defile's trap.
At the Letherii line's centre where Bivatt commanded was the thousand heavy infantry of the
Merchants' Battalion
in sawtooth formation and fifteen hundred medium infantry from the
Drene Garrison
. The army's right flank consisted of a thousand light infantry skirmishers placed in advance of five hundred heavies of the Harridict Brigade and fifteen hundred medium infantry of the Crimson Rampant Brigade in shield-wall formation. Another thousand light infantry skirmishers of the Crimson Rampant Brigade marked the end of the army's right flank. A further fifteen hundred Bluerose Lancers were placed behind the right wing's infantry with orders to instigate the push north. A majority of the Letherii army's Medium infantry were held in reserve on the western ridgeline in platoons of five hundred, each protecting a Letherii
mage
.
Brohl Handar and the Edur forces were left west of the valley midway between the Letherii reserves and the baggage train, divided into eight cohorts of 250 warriors. Four hundred mixed Letherii infantry guarded the supply camp itself.

The Battle
Atri-Preda Bivatt's plans for the battle went awry from the start. Redmask counted on Letherii overconfidence and developed unexpected tactics to neutralise their enemy's superiority. He chose Bast Fulmar as the battleground because an
ancient ritual
had left the ground inimical to magic. Letherii sorcerers would not be able to unleash their dreaded offensive power or monitor the strength and movements of the Awl.
When Letherii scouts located the Awl encampment, Redmask let them see only the fires of the Awl elders, lame, and young while the warriors hid elsewhere on the plains. This led Bivatt to underestimate the size of her opponent's forces by as much as forty percent.
The night before the battle, Bivatt hoped to secure her position early on the field, but Redmask was already in place putting the morning sun in the Letherii's eyes when the fighting began.
The Awl also used poisoned meat to eliminate the fierce Drene war dogs.
Bivatt launched the battle by sending the Harridict Brigade skirmishers and Crimson Rampant heavies forward on the left flank and the Artisan Battalion skirmishers and Crimson Rampant light infantry forward on the right. The left flank Bluerose Lancers were ordered to take their position north of the battlefield blocking the exit from the defile. The Awl side of the battlefield remained obscured by the sunlit dust cast into the air by their horses. Next to move forward were the Bluerose Lancers, Crimson Rampant mediums, and Harridict heavies on the right flank.
Awl skirmishers moved to meet the Letherii advance in the north and south and launched javelins against the Drene Garrison troops in the centre. Meanwhile, the Awl horse-archers had largely disappeared sending sporadic flights of arrows into the enemy skirmishers from the eastern ridgeline. When both forces were about to make contact in the valley, the Awl hastily withdrew. Bivatt hoped this was the moment the Awl would break and sent in the heavies of the Merchants' Battalion and the Crimson Rampant Brigade on the left flank. Suddenly a narrow column of Awl horse-archers charged toward the centre of the battlefield. In a complicated maneuver they reformed in a line to race south and attack the flank of the Artisan skirmishers, routing them, before realigning to fire arrows into the lines of the Harridict heavies and Crimson Rampant mediums before racing back to the east.
The Bluerose Lancers on the southern flank began to charge after the horse-archers before Bivatt could stop them. But a new line of heavier-armed and armoured Awl cavalry appeared on the eastern ridgeline, swept through the Letherii skirmishers, and hit the outnumbered Bluerose Lancers. Bivatt sent forward the Harridict and Merchants' heavies, but the Crimson Rampant skirmishers and Bluerose Lancers were largely wiped out deeply wounding the Letherii right. Even as Bivatt prepared to respond, the Awl skirmishers reappeared at the top of the hill, this time bearing spears and copper-sheathed shields. Then reports arrived that the Bluerose Lancers sent beyond the northern defile had been routed by thousands of Awl dogs hidden in the tall grass. Bivatt moved her Merchants' medium reserves to her left flank as the widely spaced wedges of Awl spearmen in the north moved to meet the Crimson Rampant heavies.
Bivatt hoped to fire her ballistae into the advancing spearmen, but frenzied barking and cries from the north revealed they had been overriden by the Awl dogs. She sent her Artisan medium reserve to handle the dogs when Redmask launched his next surprise. Through the gaps in the spearmen wedges came as many as a hundred enormous Awl wagons barreling down the hill. Each was crowded with spearmen and protected by forward-facing shields. The wagons tore apart the Crimson Rampant heavy infantry before the spearmen disembarked and the Awl wedges charged forward. Bivatt had seen enough, sending the Artisan heavy infantry to cover the army's retreat even as another charge of wagons hit her southern flank.
Meanwhile at the Letherii army's supply camp, the
K'ell Hunter
Sag'Churok
, slaughtered the mixed Letherii infantry guards and tore apart their tents. Those Letherii who fled the carnage were killed in ambush by Awl hiding in the high grasses. Brohl Handar led his
K'risnan
, warlocks, and Edur warriors to defend the camp, but the K'ell Hunter easily dispatched the warlocks and sent the Overseer's horse crashing to the ground. Handar suffered a serious wound to his leg, but his life was saved by his K'risnan.
The other K'Chain Che'Malle,
Gunth Mach
, attacked one of the Artisan reserve units and dismembering its mage within sight of Bivatt's retreat up the hill. It was eventually forced to flee as the Letherii mages discovered their magic returned once out of the valley. Redmask was content to remain at Bast Fulmar as the Letherii retreated.
Aftermath
The Letherii suffered over eight hundred dead and wounded, and remained camped for two days on the south side of Bast Fulmar giving the Awl plenty of time to disappear. Afterwards, the Letherii desperately tried to draw the Awl into battle, but Redmask's forces only ambushed and killed the Letherii scouts, collecting their horses, weapons, and armour. Bivatt refused to send out a reconnaissance in force to find the Awl knowing that Redmask would overwhelm and slaughter any such expedition. Brohl Handar led thirty of his best riders out in search of the Awl everyday without success. With their supplies dwindling and their wagons crowded with wounded, he soon concluded their best option was to withdraw to Drene to force Redmask to bring the war to them, but he deemed Bivatt unlikely to approve such a strategy.
The armies of the Awl and the Letherii would next meet again at the
Battle of Pradegar
.

The
Battle of Bayen Eckar
was part of the
Trell Wars
and the first of seven battles fought between the united
Trell
clans under
Trynigarr
and the
Nemil
under General
Saylan'mathas
.  The battle was the result of Nemil's expansion into the Trell borderlands.
It took place at least two hundred years before the events of the
Malazan Book of the Fallen
.
Elder Trynigarr was a last minute choice as warleader for the Trell after the other three elders were accidentally fatally poisoned by a shamanistic brew they had consumed in preparation for the fight.  This was his first ever battle as warleader and he was not well respected by his warriors, who mocked him amongst themselves.
Saylan'mathas was a much-feared Nemil general with a hand-picked force of superbly disciplined soldiers.  His heavily armoured
cataphracts
were the most feared cavalry west of the
Jhag Odhan
and largely made up of extended noble families, who were highly motivated
and likely difficult to rout.
The Battlefield
The battle took place in the
Valley of Bayen Eckar
, a narrow river basin running in a north-south direction in the Trell borderlands near Nemil.  The
Bayen Eckar
river running northwards through the valley was stony, barely knee-deep, and lined by thickets, shrubs, and clumps of grey-leaved trees.  Less than fifteen hundred paces to the river's east was
Orstanz Ridge
.  Another unnamed ridge rose on the river's western bank.
A thousand Trell, including Trynigarr and
Mappo Runt
, stood on the slope of Orstanz Ridge facing their opponents on the other side of the river.
Unbeknownst to their opponents, the Trell had hidden the majority of their forces beyond the crest of the ridge or on the Nemil's side of the battlefield near their supply camp.  To make their ruse more plausible, rumours of the alliance's break up had been deliberately spread for the Nemil to hear.
The Nemil gathered fifteen hundred paces away, across the river on the western slope of the valley.  Their units of infantry, archers, and slingers moved in an ever-shifting presentation. General Saylan'Mathas traversed the western ridge atop his horse flanked by his retainers.

The two sides faced each across the Bayen Eckar river from opposite slopes of the valley. The Trell archers on the Orstanz Ridge rained arrows, javelins, and bags of hundreds of black scorpions into the infantry phalanxes. Nemil was defeated.
Aftermath

Human

Years later, Trynigarr ended his life as a drunk, dying in an alley, and the Nemil succeeded in their original objective by other means.  They and their half-breed Trell scouts organised a sustained slaughter of the
Bhederin
the Trell relied on for food.  Driven by starvation, the Trell were forced to abandon their wild-lands.

The Battle
Despite the appearance that battle was imminent, the sun set without an Awl attack. The Letherii could tell they were being encircled even before then and their mages blackened whole swathes of grassland in frustration.

The Battlefield
Q'uson Tapi had once been the bed of an ancient inland sea, but now it was only salt-crusted bed of clay and silt that spanned for leagues with no rise or feature.
After rainfall, attempts to move through the area stirred up mud that clung to feet in oversized clumps.
Well ahead of his pursuers, Redmask chose a position on the sea bed 500 paces south of the sea's former shoreline.
There the Awl broke their wagons down into wood, hide, and canvas to construct a defensive position that would keep them above the mud. The wagon beds became raised platforms standing on bundled spear-shafts connected to each other by wooden walkways. The canvas and hides were staked into the ground creating rows with squares behind each row and linked by flattened wicker walls that had once been hut-frames. His warriors would occupy the platforms, while reserves stood ready at the canvas squares.
Storm clouds gathered, bringing rain. Redmask could afford to wait and force his enemy to trudge through the mud as the Awl had butchered their herds and were well fed.

The two mages were separated and placed on the front flanks of the outer wedges where they were surrounded by a protective ring of soldier carrying oversized shields.

Bluerose Lancers

The Battlefield
The Heart of Raraku
The Pan'arak Oasis was the site of an ancient city which had once sat on the shore of a great inland sea.  South of the city was the bed of a dried up harbour which had baked over the centuries into a flat basin of hard-packed, crackled clay.
The basin was not large enough for massed cavalry charges and left little room for manoeuvre.
Several fans of silt led from the area of the city down into the basin.  They had been hardened by the sun into seven broad, solid ramps.  The oasis' eastern flank was protected by an escarpment that dropped eighty arm-lengths to the salt flat below.  On the western flank were deep, soft sand dunes rife with sink-pits.
Sha'ik established her forward post to observe the battle on the hilltop to the west of the Dogslayers' positions.
Four thousand paces south of the ramps, on the other side of the basin, was a vast arc of what had once been coral islands.  The sand that had once covered these coral reef remnants had been stripped away by the Whirlwind making their footing treacherous.
Although flat-topped, their flanks were ragged and pitted.  The low channels between them were narrow, twisting, and full of sharp rubble.
Korbolo Dom
placed the
Dogslayers
Heavy infantry
at the tops of the steep ramps where trenches had been dug and
Mathok

The ramps had been smoothed and cobbled to make them as difficult to climb as possible, especially when slick with blood.  Likely, Korbolo Dom hoped to keep the Malazans crowded and struggling to climb the ramps while an endless hail of missiles and sorcery rained from above.
Any Malazans who made it to the top would face his elite soldiers.
Meanwhile, mounted lancers and horse warriors remained behind the Apocalypse lines waiting to sweep down on the Malazans if they tried to pull back.
The Malazans occupied the coral reefs on the other side of the basin in units of a hundred and quickly began digging in.  Although not ideal places to assemble troops, they proved to be as defensible as mountain fortresses once occupied.  The troops constructed barriers and low walls from the coral rubble as well as ramps on the islands' south sides.
The Malazan cavalry remained south of the islands while a road was constructed leading to the basin.
The Adjunct placed the
8th Legion

The Chain of Dogs march, part 1
"
Once again, it was not to be the Whirlwind's day.
"
―Duiker
The
Battle of Sekala Crossing
(also known as the
Battle of Sekala Plains
)
was the second major battle during the
Chain of Dogs
. Three months and 160 leagues after leaving
Hissar
,
Fist
Coltaine
's
7th Army
continued to seek safe harbour from
Kamist Reloe
's pursuing army of the
Whirlwind
. Coltaine's immediate aim was to reach
Ubaryd
on the
Dojal Hading Sea
coast.
While attempting to ford the
Sekala
River near the small village of
L'enbarl
they were attacked by Reloe from the east and by
Semk
tribes and
Guran
heavy infantry legions from the west.
Opposing forces
Malazan
Coltaine's mixed force of 7th Army veterans, Hissar auxiliaries,
Sialk Marines
, and
Wickan
warriors established a hastily fortified camp on the western side of the river along both sides of the trader road leading to the ford. The
Weasel
Clan manned the outermost entrenchments facing Kamist Reloe's army. Within the camp, the army's 350 wagons were kept north of the road while its massive herd of cattle was kept to the south. The army's thirty thousand refugees clustered about an oxbow island near the ford. The river's marshy original channel passed around the eastern side of the island and was crossed by a makeshift wooden slat bridge.
Running south from the ford was a road leading over a low set of hills to the nearby village of
L'enbarl
. This was patrolled by the
Crow
Clan.
The river itself was about 420 paces across at the ford and four and a half feet deep. The ford was four to five arms spans wide to either side before becoming too deep to cross. The 3rd and 5th companies of the 7th Army were sent west across the river to dig defensive works.
Coltaine's headquarters was a sturdy farmhouse on the southern side of the trader road leading to the ford between the cattle and the oxbow island.
Coltaine's army possessed a distinct advantage over Kamist Reloe regarding the use of sorcery. Due to the effects of the
Path of Hands
, access to the
warrens
was severely hampered on
Seven Cities
. While the High Mage could make no use of his magic
Sormo E'nath
and his warlocks were able to turn their efforts "to the old ways, using the sorceries of the land, of life spirits and totem beasts." The warlocks also handled communications.
Captain
Mincer
and his
Sappers
appeared to operate somewhat independently of the regular chain of command.
Whirlwind
The Malazans faced two armies of Whirlwind, one on either side of the river. On the east side of the river was the army of
High Mage
Kamist Reloe
. His forces consisted of the soldiers of four cities (including Hissar and Sialk) and twelve towns, the warriors of countless tribes, and an enraged peasant horde "as vast as an inland sea."
Reloe's camp was set along the trader road about five hundred paces to the east of Coltaine's Weasel Clan pickets. In the front lines were the Hissar and Sialk regular infantry positioned across the trader road. Behind them and to the north of the road were one thousand tents of
Tithansi
horse warriors. To the south of the road were the twenty thousand tents of the peasant army.
North of Coltaine's camp at the ford was a
tel
atop a broad hill separated from the oxbow island by marshy ground and a thin stand of leadwoods. Here Reloe placed his two legions of Tithansi archers and his two thousand hand-picked elite
heavy infantry
.
The second Whirlwind army arrived on the western side of the river after the Malazan crossing had already begun. It consisted of three legions of
Guran
heavy infantry, Tithansi lancers, and fierce
Semk
warriors and sorcerers. Their relationship to their tribal god meant the Semk sorcerers had full access to their warrens.
The Battle
Evening
Overnight, Coltaine sent the
Foolish Dog
Clan to reinforce Captain
Chenned
and his 7th Army troops on the western side of the river. Then he began moving first his wagons and wounded, followed by livestock and refugees, over the ford in the dark. The sappers were to accompany them. Captain
Lull
was sent to charge of the rearguard defense against Kamist Reloe while Coltaine commanded the Crow clan blocking the road to the south.
Morning
At daylight, warlock magic was used to obscure the view of the ford as the cattle finally began crossing the river.
Baria Setral
's
Red Blades
were sent across the river to support the 7th Army's advanced picket as the Guran army arrived and began skirmishing.
Kamist Reloe began his attack by sending his Hissar and Sialk infantry forward on the right. His Tithansi horsemen Hissari lancers began a series of feint attacks on the Weasel clan lancers on the center and left respectively. Meanwhile, Coltaine and the Crow Clan blocked an attack from Sialk cavalry from the hills near L'enbral.
Reloe's Tithansi archer legions began descending from the tel to fire on the cattle crossing the ford, but were repelled by a thirsty marsh spirit whom Sormo had promised "a day of warm blood."
The pressure of the Hissar and Sialk infantry and peasant horde forced the rearguard defenders back to the oxbow island where they established a defensive line on the western side of the former river channel. The warlock
Nil
then summoned the spirits of an ancient tribe that had been massacred on the site. These undead warriors rose bearing rusted swords and encrusted armour, and descended on the Whirlwind army with blood-curdling joyous laughter and chant-like singing.
Afternoon
By noon the bulk of the refugees had crossed with the Crow Clan horsewarriors herding them using long poles much as they had the cattle. Reloe sent his elite infantry down from the tel in a wide arc east of the spirit protecting the marsh. The Weasel Clan began evacuating the rearguard using their horses and Coltaine and the Crow Clan retreated towards the ford from the burning L'enbarl. Reloe's elite infantry formed into two rectangles before the oxbow channel as Captain Lull reorganized his shrinking troops into a three sided ring to face them. Coltaine led his horse warriors in a slashing attack on the peasant flank before calling for a general retreat.
As the remaining Malazans raced to ford, the peasant horde was unleashed. It was at this point that the Malazan trap was revealed. Captain Mincer and his sappers had spent their time in camp turning the muddy ford into a paved underwater road. This enabled the Malazans to cross in record speed. But Mincer's engineers had also laced the road with fifty-five
cussers
. Once the Malazans had safely crossed and ford was occupied by thousands of screaming peasants,
Cuttle
blew the road with a concussion "like a god's fist." Thousands were killed and the ford was destroyed.
On the western side of the ford, the Guran legions remained unengaged to the north while the Semk warriors and Tithansi lancers were crushed by the now reunited Malazans. Baria Setral's Red Blades were particularly deadly for the Semk and during the fighting the Semk god killed many of his own sorcerers with his unthinking rage by channeling too much power into them. With the crossing complete, one enemy cut off on the other side of the river and the other refusing to engage, the Battle of Sekala Crossing was over.
Aftermath
The battle was won but the cost was high for the Malazans. The Sialk Marines and Red Blades lost half of their soldiers, and the auxiliaries lost even more. The Weasel Clan had been badly mauled in the withdrawal and faced a severe shortage of horses. Two of Sormo E'nath's young warlocks fell and a surprisingly large number of the 7th Army's officers and squad sergeants were lost.
Coltaine ordered new fortifications raised and existing ones reinforced. The refugee camp was placed on the stony plain to the west while the cattle were driven by the Foolish Dog Clan into the grassy Barl Hills to the south. The following day was to be a day of rest.
In addition to Kamist Reloe's list of casualties, the High Mage now had to contend with his inability to reach the Malazan army. He began a march north to the town of
Sekal
, the nearest place where the Sekala River could be crossed. But this journey would require ten to eleven days before he could return to the other side of the ford. Meanwhile, his Guran allies remained relatively unblooded and unengaged to the north of Coltaine's army. The Semk tribes were devastated by their losses, which were made worse when Sormo E'nath, Nil, and
Nether
unleashed the spirits of the land to devour and dissipate their tribal god.

Map of the Chain of Dogs march, part 2
"
The River Vathar's mouth gushed blood and corpses into Dojal Hading Sea for close to a week after the slaughter.
"
―Duiker
The
Battle of Vathar Crossing
was the fourth major battle during the
Chain of Dogs
. It was also known as
Coltaine's Crossing
, the
Vathar Massacre
, and in the
Can'eld
dialect as
Mesh'arn tho'ledann
or the
Day of Pure Blood
.
After the victory at
Gelor Ridge
,
Fist
Coltaine
faced calls from Captain
Sulmar
and the Council of Nobles to seize
Ubaryd
and await relief from Admiral
Nok
's fleet rather than continue on the long road to
Aren
. Coltaine rejected the demand noting that the city would prove a fatal trap prone to attack from land and sea. No rescue from Aren would be sent as
High Fist
Pormqual
had already dismissed countless opportunities to dispatch the army or navy to assist. The Chain of Dogs would continue their march south.
The Battlefield
Six months after leaving
Hissar
, Coltaine's forces rushed to reach the crossing over the River
Vathar
ahead of the army of the
Apocalypse
.
The river ran from west to east through a narrow gorge along the northern edge of the
Vathar Forest
. At the ford the river was forty paces across and waist deep. Where the road approached the ford on the northern shore was an open flat bordered on both sides by thinned stands of cedar. The road on the southern shore quickly disappeared into dense forest. Upstream to the west were steep cliffs on both sides of the river. At the time the battle took place the river and trees were covered with countless yellow butterflies amidst their annual migration.
Opposing Forces
Malazan
Coltaine's forces were lessened after the fight at Gelor Ridge, although he did receive approximately fifty new recruits into the 7th Army consisting of former members of the nobles' serving class.
The number of refugees was slightly reduced at forty-five thousand.
Coltaine had full use of only one warlock,
Sormo E'nath
, as
Nil
and
Nether
were still weakened by Gelor Ridge, and his healers were already taxed beyond their limits.
Whirlwind
After Gelor Ridge, the Malazans faced a new enemy commander, renegade Fist
Korbolo Dom
, who had been passed over for command of the 7th Army in favour of Coltaine. Former commander
Kamist Reloe
was not a military leader and relied solely on his overwhelming numbers. Dom was a very able military commander and tactician, familiar with Malazan methods, and resentful of the man who took his place.
Additionally, the Whirlwind's mages once again had access to their
warrens
as the
interference
preventing their use on
Seven Cities
had ended.
The Battle
When the vanguard of the Malazan army was still seven leagues from the Vathar ford, scouts spotted an advance force of at least thirty companies of Whirlwind cavalry attempting to reach the river ahead of them. Coltaine sent the remaining eight hundred
Foolish Dog
Clan horse-warriors with Nether and
Duiker
racing ahead. They were accompanied by twenty
Wickan cattle-dogs
. The Wickans reached the ford first and began creating entrenchments on the northern bank in anticipation of the enemy cavalry that outnumbered them greater than three to one. At the river they also discovered the
Silanda
, a magic vessel crewed by Captain
Gesler
and members of the
Malazan Coastal Guard
.
Dom's advance force did not confront the Wickans. Instead it established a camp three hundred paces upriver and began felling trees. Gesler took his ship upriver to reconnoiter the enemy's activity and discovered over 100 Ubari archers had crossed to the southern bank by a makeshift rope bridge. When the rest of Dom's forces arrived Coltaine was still two hours away. The Whirlwind army surrounded the Wickans on the northern bank from a distance of seventy paces, but left the road open for Coltaine. Tree felling operations began downstream as well.
Coltaine and the rest of the army joined the Wickans after nightfall. Gesler was ordered to load his ship with as many wounded as it could carry and depart for Aren. Dom sent an emissary to the Malazans proposing safe passage south of the river for the refugees. Coltaine dismissed the offer despite objections from the Council of Nobles. Dom's sorcerers used their returned powers to block attempts to observe preparations at their camp.
The following morning Coltaine received word that the nobles had accepted the offer of a second emissary. The Fist made no attempt to stop the surge of refugees but assigned the
sappers
and
marines
to accompany them in disguise while a dozen Wickans guided the mob into the water on horseback.
Once the refugees and noble wagons began reaching the southern bank, Dom's main force attacked Coltaine's rearguard on the northern bank. Hidden Ubari archers on the southern bank let loose with waves of arrows killing the oxen pulling the noble wagons and blocking the ford. Floating bridges guided by ropes and carrying Whirlwind pikemen and archers appeared on both sides of the ford and began slaughtering refugees. Panic set in among the refugees and many began to be trampled and drowned. The sappers, marines, and a few Wickans rallied on the southern bank to assault the three companies of archers in the cedar woods. Desperate refugees attacked and destroyed the upstream floating bridge while the downstream bridge was destroyed by Sormo and Wickan archers. Sormo perished in the process. Duiker unleashed a stampede of refugees against the archers on the southern bank and by the battle's end the 7th Army followed them across.
Aftermath
'Day of Pure Blood' by
Corporal Nobbs
"
What has happened? Have we won?
"
―Nethpara
The cost of the battle to the Malazans was staggering. Some twenty thousand refugees were dead, a disproportionate number of them children. Seven hundred soldiers of the 7th Army also perished. Only a dozen sappers and a score of marines still lived. The Foolish Dog Clan could field less than five hundred fighters, and the other two clans were almost as badly mauled. Coltaine's most powerful warlock was dead.
The river ran red and black with blood for leagues, and it took almost a week for the pallid, bloated bodies to completely wash out into the
Dojal Hading Sea
near Ubaryd. For fishermen it was the Season of Sharks and spoiled catches.
Although Whirlwind casualties were much lighter than those of the Malazans, the massacre brought no peace of mind, only fear. A Malazan punitive fleet was on its way from
Unta
"commanded by a
woman
as hard as iron. What happened at Vathar Crossing was a whetstone to hone her deadly edge."

The
Battle of the Plains
, also known as the
Battle of the Crossroads
,
was the culminating fight of the Malazan civil war.  It took place in the late summer of the 1166th year of
Burn's Sleep
and was actually three separate battles occurring over three consecutive days.
In
Return of the Crimson Guard
The
Malazan
forces were led by the
Empress
herself against an uprising by the
Talian League
close to the city of
Li Heng
. To complicate matters, at the same time, the
Crimson Guard
, sworn to bring down the Malazan Empire, also returned, so the Malazans were faced with multiple fronts.
The Talians were betrayed by the
Seti
, who left the battlefield, confident they could take Li Heng without any outside help. The Imperial troops managed to win the battle against the Talian League and pressed the remaining troops to fight on behalf of the Empire against their common foe, the Crimson Guard. This threat was also neutralized.

Tayschrenn
disappeared into a chaos portal opened by
Yathengar
, Laseen's position seemed more secure than ever.
Choss
and

were reported dead,

was missing, and

's attempt was successfully thwarted by

but

Genabackis
Blackdog Forest by
Corporal Nobbs
Blackdog Forest
, or
Swamp
, was a large boreal forest surrounded by swamp
located on the shield bedrock of the continent of
Genabackis
. Beginning around 1156
BS
,
it was the site of major battles between the
Malaz 5th Army
and the allied armies of
Caladan Brood
and the
Crimson Guard
during the
First Campaigns
.
The 5th Army spent at least four years there, battling Brood and the Guard.
Timber wolves lived within the forest.
Blackdog Campaign
Blackdog campaign map by
Steven Erikson
While the events of the Blackdog campaign were complete by the beginning of the
Malazan Book of the Fallen
, members of the Bridgeburners often referred to events that occurred there.
In
Gardens of the Moon
The
Ilgres
Barghast
were amongst those who had joined the Crimson Guard at Blackdog Forest.
In
Deadhouse Gates
Kalam
recalled a war as much against mosquitos, leeches, poisonous snakes, and blood-sucking lizards as against an organised enemy. At a time when the enemy had cut their supply lines and the
Moranth
were pulling back, Kalam had seen a select company of Brood's soldiers under the ragged banner of
Kallor
.
In
Memories of Ice
After a
Cusser
had taken out a half-dozen
Tiste Andii
early in the campaign, a squad of Malazan regulars began looting their bodies.  They were angered to find nothing of value before
Whiskeyjack
and the
Bridgeburners
drove them off. Whiskeyjack recovered a knotted strip of coloured cloth and a stream-polished stone from the bodies before ordering them buried.
Black Moranth
commander
Twist
"shook hands with a
Rhivi
spirit" in Blackdog Forest resulting in his permanently withered hand.
The necromantic injury began to spread and was expected to slowly kill him over time.
Picker
and
Blend
remembered a night retreat that had brought them stumbling into a Rhivi Burn Ground. The pair had been surrounded by malign spirits rising from the ashes before they were spotted and pulled to safety by a wing of Black Moranth on their
Quorls
.
In
House of Chains
Kalam used the pseudonym "
Ulfas
", the name of a
Barghast
war chief killed at Blackdog Forest, when confronting the renegade Malazan officer,
Irriz
.
In
The Bonehunters
For a time,
Quick Ben
had masqueraded in Blackdog as a
High Mage
named
Kribalah Rule
. In this guise he had rescued
Tayschrenn
from assassins before apparently perishing in a horrible conflagration. It was also at this time that he pilfered a vial from Tayschrenn containing the demon
Pearl
that the Bridgeburner mage later sent after
Anomander Rake
in
Darujhistan
.
Kalam recalled a nightmare war fought in black spruce stands, vast swamps, mud, lagoons, and bogs against the
Mott Irregulars
, Crimson Guard, and
Nathii
First Army. The Bridgeburners had been hollowed out and numbed by the experience. Weeks of slogging through the swamps left their skin hanging in discoloured strips.
In
Toll the Hounds
The man who would later be known as
Monkrat
had deserted at Blackdog Forest in 1159
BS
. He was thought dead and his name was listed with the fallen there.
He recalled how the red
dragon
Silanah
had wreaked devastation there from the air.
In
Dust of Dreams
Trotts
recalled burying the Bridgeburner,
Cage
, in Blackdog. But they never did find his head.
In
Blood and Bone
Remembering the Blackdog campaign,
Murken Warrow
winced at what a mess it had been. The memory of what he had seen as fresh young mage still made uncomfortable.
Trivia
The Blackdog campaign was one of many events developed by authors
Steven Erikson
and
Ian C. Esslemont
in
role-playing
sessions before any of the Malazan novels were written. Erikson served as referee and Esslemont as player controlling the members of the Malazan army.
Before the publication of
Gardens of the Moon
, Erikson and Esslemont first tried to bring their creation to life in two screenplays. One screenplay, called
Blackdog Blues
, covered the events of the Blackdog campaign.
Erikson likened the Blackdog Forest of the script to the forests of northern
Saskatchewan
where he had experience doing archaelogical surveys. He described the forest there as thick elm thickets and dogwood, unlike the cathedral-style forests typically seen in fantasy films. "You have to claw your way through...even a machete would be hopeless--you've got to bull your way through. And quite often you're closing your eyes because the branches would just potentially, you know, poke your eyes out...It's one of those settings you almost never see in film and television."

The
Death Night
was a ritual 'rite of passage' conducted by several tribal peoples in the
Malazan Book of the Fallen
.
Awl
For the
Awl
, it was an ordeal that older male children had to undergo to bring about their spiritual 'rebirth' and thus mark their transition into manhood. The rite involved the youth being buried, for the duration of a night, in a coffin (a hollowed-out log)
which was then covered by an "arm's reach" (about a yard/meter) of earth and stones. Usually the Death Night was preceded for a period of time by a variety of traditional rituals, the whole process being in the hands of a clan's
Shouldermen
.

Seti

was the

Buried alive, the child was said to have died and the adult have been born. The ritual was a test against the spirits of madness that lived inside each person as worms coiled around the base of the skull. The Seti child learned a chant to help them through the ordeal.

Once the soul had broken free of the body, it could forever after wander free in the realm of dreams. Sometimes spirits lurking in the peat fought with the witches to claim the bodies of their young charges, pulling their bodies into the foul deep. These lost souls emerged "only once every year, on the Night of Awakened Children with blue-brown skin and hollowed-out eye sockets, with hair the colour of rust or blood, with long polished nails – walking the swamp and singing songs of the earth that could drive a mortal mad."

To '
Hobble
a woman' was the term used for a long-standing practice common, if not universal, amongst the clans of the
White Face
Barghast
. The word
Hobbler
was used to refer to a woman who had been hobbled - not to the one(s) who had done the hobbling.
In
Dust of Dreams
"
A woman hobbled has her feet chopped [i.e., her toes cut off] and can refuse no man or woman or, indeed, camp dog.
"
―
Spax
of clan
Gilk
explaining to Queen
Abrastal
of
Bolkando
what 'hobbling' meant
The reasons for the hobbling of White Face Barghast women were varied, but usually included motives - openly acknowledged or not - such as: vengeance; envy; greed; punishment for 'major crimes' - real, perceived, or invented; or the removal of an inconvenient and/or hated woman from a position of influence and/or power. The result of hobbling was the extreme humiliation, total degradation, and complete dehumanization of the woman, thus making of her an object always available for unbridled abuse - sexual or otherwise.
The details of hobbling seemingly varied from clan to clan of the White Face. For example, the people performing the hobbling could be male
or - apparently more usually - female.
Although hobbling was always done to women, the White Face Barghast had a somewhat analogous custom directed towards men - which involved a "shaming" 'show trial' which would result in a sentence of castration.
The relative frequency of these two practices was never indicated.
The White Face Barghast insisted on retaining the custom of hobbling, as "vicious" as it was, even though
Onos Toolan
, when he became Warchief of the combined clans - along with the 'Great Warlock',
Cafal
- wished to completely outlaw the custom.
Even
Bakal
, who had once been one of Tool's "worst critics" and "fiercest rivals" in clan
Senan
,
finally realized in the end that hobbling was actually a symptom of all that was 'wrong' with the White Face clans and that it was a particularly telling symbol of the Barghast drive to "self-destruction".

of clan

Speculations
There was a mention of the 'cutting off of toes' elsewhere in
Dust of Dreams
in a completely different context, and for a completely different reason, than those of the White Face custom of 'hobbling'. It involved Warleader
Gall
of the
Khundryl Burned Tears
- fighting the
Bolkando

Vedith
before an upcoming battle. Gall finished his orders by telling Vedith not to let his warriors indiscriminately slaughter the enemy, but rather to capture them, then cut off their toes and leave these, now maimed and useless, soldiers to be a continuing burden upon Bolkando resources and an ongoing reminder of defeat.
This sort of thing was probably a common tactical practice on many different continents, amongst many different peoples, and may have been the original genesis - eventually corrupted - of the White Face Barghast custom of hobbling. The author,
Steven Erikson
, may have intended this Khundryl episode to be a hint of how the practice of hobbling amongst the White Face clans could have come about.

Quon Tali
'Itko Kan Massacre', Interpretation by
Corporal Nobbs
Itko Kan
was a coastal region east of the
Dal Hon Plains
, on
Quon Tali
.
In
Gardens of the Moon
In the 7th year of
Laseen
's rule, during the growing season,
a massacre occurred in Itko Kan. It happened towards the end of a day.
Instigated by
Ammanas
,
the
Hounds of Shadow
killed all 175 members of the 19th Regiment of the
Itko Kanese 8th Cavalry
, their 210 horses, as well as an unknown number of civilians and animals in the surrounding area.
The total number of fatalities was estimated to have been in the region of over 400.
The massacre happened on a part of the Imperial coast road, a raised gravel track with a white, dusty surface, built by the Empire
which was lined with heather and gorse. The road, with the sea to its left, at that point dipped after the crest of a hill for about a fifth of a league, climbing again to a promontory.
On the slope leading up to the promontory, a narrow track went off to the left, leading down towards the sea and a small fishing village. The path, the edge of which dropped away to rocks sixty feet below, skirted the promontory's bluff, descending down towards a small fishing village situated on a broad, grassy shelf at the promontory's foot, overlooking a crescent shaped beach.
The carnage stretched from the crest of the hill, for half a league, all the way to the fishing village, which consisted of a dozen or so huts and half a league inland.
The huts had bodies in all but two.
All the dead soldiers had their weapons bared, indicating that they had unsuccessfully tried to defend themselves.
There were no witnesses.
The body of an old woman, presumed to be
Rigga
, was found on the road about half a league to the south of the crest of the hill.
The massacre was probably not discovered until at least the day after, coinciding with the eighth day of a recruitment drive for the
Genabackis Campaign
in the region. The local Captain deployed three companies,
a total of eleven hundred men,
to guard the site which by now was covered in carrion-eaters like gulls and crows.
Adjunct Lorn
arrived by
Warren
not ten minutes after the departure of the guarding companies
to investigate the massacre.
Lorn ordered the captain to have the dead removed and erase all evidence of what had happened as quickly as possible.
She involved
Ganoes Paran
, then a Lieutenant new to the local command,
in her investigation and attached him to her staff.
Lorn was able to detect that sorcery had been involved and that they were dealing with a set-up designed to divert them from discovering something else that had happened.
Aftermath
Paran was dispatched to
Gerrom
by Adjunct Lorn to follow-up clues. When he arrived, he found the town abandoned. He estimated that the flight of the inhabitants had happened no more than a day before his arrival.
He found no bodies, other than the occupants of the Constabulary who were all dead within the building. The soldiers in the outer room, which was filled with black pigeons, looked as if they had suffocated. Their captain was dead at his desk in his office.
The investigation eventually led them onto the trail of
Sorry
, later known as
Apsalar
, who was abducted from the area during the massacre.
Investigations made later by
Kalam Mekhar
indicated that a
Claw
came in the wake of the massacre and silenced near everyone in the City Guard who might have talked.
Quick Ben
deduced from Lorn having been called in to investigate the massacre, that high sorcery must have been involved.

The
Korelri Campaign
was the invasion of
Korel
(specifically the
Fist
subcontinent) by the
Malazan 6th Army
. The army was commanded by
High Fist
Greymane
, a native of
Geni
, whose knowledge of the waters between
Quon Tali
and Korel was deemed an asset.
It was not revealed whether the Malazans encountered the
Marese
fleet in the initial invasion. But once the main invasion fleet made landfall, a Marese blockade prevented all but token numbers of Malazan reinforcements from arriving. Lieutenant
Rillish Jal Keth
led the last fleet to attempt a crossing from
Unta
and lost four-fifths of his ships.
While successful at first, the invasion soon became a stalemate. Amongst the problems were delays caused by rains and other natural phenomena generated by
the Lady
, the subcontinent's patron goddess.
Spoiler warning
:
The following section contains significant plot details about Korelri Campaign.
Greymane's downfall came when he attempted to open communication with the alien
Stormriders
, mortal enemies of the Korelri. Meeting at the edge of the
Ocean of Storms
he learned that the Stormriders viewed themselves as the injured party, claiming the peoples of Korel were denying them access to their own territory and preventing them from their ancient obligation. As a gift for allowing them to speak, they presented Greymane with an
ancient blade
they had found at the bottom of
the Cut
.
When the Fist's actions were discovered, Malazan Governor
Hemel 'Et Kelal
,
Enesh-jer
, and several minor officers court-martialed him. The trial was largely a sham as the evidence had been slight, if not fabricated, and the military had been mainly interested in finding a scapegoat for the failing campaign.
After his conviction, Greymane escaped and was known as the Great Betrayer by the people of Korel.
A price was placed on his head by both the Malazans and the Korelri.
Governor 'Et Kelal and the 6th Army ended up creating their own nation from the kingdom of
Rool
, in effect, mutinying from the Malazan Empire. By retaining the tax monies collected for the Empire they established themselves as the new noble class above the Roolian natives.
See also:
6th Army
for more information
Significant plot details  end here.
In
Forge of the High Mage
The Malazan invasion of Korel was revealed to have begun and failed even before the Quon Tali continent had been completely pacified under Malazan rule. Emperor
Kellanved
made the decision that Korel was a lost cause and reassigned the army meant to serve as the Korelan relief force to instead join his planned invasion of
Falar
, while allowing the army already on Korel to withdraw.
Kellanved told
Dancer
they should have "known better than to meddle in that region", but did not consider it instructive for the planned campaign in Falar.
The Emperor seemed willing to allow the blame for the Korel disaster to fall upon Greymane's shoulders rather than his own via the Imperial Historians, much to Dancer's dismay.
By the time the Falari invasion was in full force,
Admiral
Nok
and the southern fleet had evacuated most of the Malazan troops from Korel.

The
Mott Campaign
was a military operation during the conquest of
Genabackis
by the
Malazan Empire
. Amongst the Malazan forces involved were the Malazan
2nd
,
3rd
,
and
5th Armies

Their most well-known opponents were the
Mott Irregulars
,
the
Crimson Guard
,
Anomander Rake
and his
Tiste Andii
, and the
Eleint
,
Silanah
.
History
The campaign began in 1156
BS
when the 2nd and 3rd Armies, fresh from the capture of
Malyntaeas
, made landfall on the southern shore of the
Malyn Sea
. Two days south of the landing, the
Bridgeburners
were given their first supplies of
Moranth munitions
after a successful treaty was concluded with the
Moranth
.
Whiskeyjack
ordered his
Sappers
,
Fiddler
and
Hedge
, to begin experimenting with the new devices.
The city of
Oraz
fell to the Malazans first and the city of
Mott
quickly surrendered. Then
High Fist
Dujek Onearm
sent the Bridgeburners into
Mott Wood
to root out the renegades who threatened the supply lines of his armies as they readied to move inland.
Thus began the nightmare of Mott Wood.
The native Mott Irregulars ran circles around the Bridgeburners and Malazan troops for over a year and a half.  Whenever the Irregulars chose to stand and fight, "it was as if some dark swamp god possessed them" and they bloodied the noses of the imperial soldiers.
The
Gold Moranth
sent to assist the Malazans fared no better.
The Malazans faced the Tiste Andii and Crimson Guard as well.
Spinnock Durav
recalled Silanah sending flames ripping down into the trees with a deafening concussion, killing countless creatures both friend and foe.
Just the passage of the ancient dragon over the treetops left her enemies quaking in terror.

Cowl
's sorcery, which sent the bleeding dragon back to
Moon's Spawn

Mage cadre
commander
Nedurian
was a casualty of the fighting resulting in
Tattersail

It was during this time that Fiddler and Hedge built a warped and misshapen table that was the scene of many heated Bridgeburner card games. The table was lost when the Mott Irregulars overran their camp and stole it.
It was also the campaign where
Storo Matash
's squad played games of
Stones
against the wood's defenders, calling their moves out into the night.
Eventually, Dujek pulled the Bridgeburners out and the enemy warlord,
Caladan Brood
, brought the Irregulars into his own army.
It was likely that the Bridgeburners moved on to the
Blackdog Campaign
.
At some point during the years-long
Siege of Pale
, Dujek briefly attached the Bridgeburners to the 5th Army while the Mott Campaign was still ongoing.  They took some mercenaries prisoner in
Greydog

Nathilog

The
Season of Rot
was a ceremony performed by priests of
Hood
in
Unta
during a pestilence.
It culminated with the Thirsting Hour, in which the priests and acolytes of Hood stripped naked and covered their bodies with the blood of executed murderers.
Thus attired, they roamed the streets in procession to attract Hood's sprites (or flies), which swarmed their bodies.
Residents who were able sealed themselves within their homes, their windows shuttered and covered in cloth.  Braziers were lit to burn istaarl leaves causing a billowing acrid smoke.
A similar ceremony was held in the city of
One Eye Cat
on
Genabackis
.
The festival there was called "Dawn of Flies" and the priests of Hood covered themselves with honey, which was said to represent blood.
In
Deadhouse Gates
In 1163
BS
, the Season of Rot did not come about by religious proclamation or imperial decree.
It was politically orchestrated by arousing the populace with rumor and innuendo.
Thus the Empress
Laseen
could veil the politically motivated
Cull
as a spontaneous religious phenomenon.
The thousands in the mob believed they were excising rot.
After the Thirsting Hour,
Heboric
,
Felisin
,
Baudin
and other victims of the Cull were deported on a slave ship.

A
Shorning
was a ritual process used by the
Tiste Edur
of
Lether
to mark someone as an outcast.
"
The Shorning was an absolute thing, an irreversible act of severance. He was now outcast. To his brothers, he had ceased to exist. He would not be mourned. His deeds would vanish from memory along with his name. His mother and father would have birthed one less child. This was, for his people, the most dire punishment—worse than execution by far.
"
―Trull Sengar recalls the act of Shorning
The Ritual
A circle was inscribed on the outcast's forehead with a dagger.  Then a slash was cut through it to break that circle and ashes were rubbed into the wound.  The outcast's hair was then roughly hacked away and smeared with an unguent to cause permanent baldness.
An element of sorcery was involved that was designed to prolong suffering.
The ultimate intent was to remove any physical sign that the outcast had ever been Tiste Edur.
Shorning in other cultures
Returning to
Unta
after his early service with the
Malazan Military
in
Itko Kan
,
Ganoes Paran
felt out of place, saying he felt "shorn, outcast".
Karsa Orlong
spoke of denouncing
Dayliss
for having lain with a man not her husband and having her shorn from her tribe.
In
House of Chains
Trull Sengar
was brought to the
Nascent
to be shorn for the crime of betraying his
brother
and people.  Afterwards he was chained to a great seawall and left to die in the rapidly flooding world.

The Siege
Preparations
As the Pannion Domin expanded northwards conquering

,
Setta

Lest

Jelarkan

He also significantly drained the city treasury purchasing bows, arrows, ballistae, mangonels, and other weapons of slaughter.
Interpretation of the Grey Swords by
dejan-delic
When the Grey Swords arrived in Capustan, they recruited from the local population expanding their forces from 7000 to 8200.
Mortal Sword
Brukhalian
assumed that a breach of the walls was inevitable and planned a defence of measured, organised contraction. The city's unusual construction--open concourses surrounding self-contained tribal camps--required little effort to convert into killing grounds. Strong-points built of stone, wood, and soaked bales of hay were placed at various approaches so that any enemy caught in the open was subject to enfilading fire.
Ancient fortress-like structures within the city, such as Jelarkan's Palace and the Grey Sword barracks, served as natural defensive points.
Additionally, the Grey Swords secretly constructed tunnels that made use of the city's extensive underground barrows. These were converted into defensible shelters to hold as many as twenty thousand citizens.
Capustan received some additional forces from cities that had already fallen to the Pannions including companies of the Lestari Guard and Prince
Arard
's
Coralessians
.
Despite Brukhalian's advice, members of the
Mask Council
insisted that hundreds of their
Gidrath
defend the redoubts outside the city's eastern and southern walls.
Meanwhile, the legions of the Pannion Domin under Septarch
Kulpath
made their way north. First Child
Anaster
went to
Bastion
,
Outlook
, and other cities gathering his hordes of
Tenescowri
.
The
Pannion Seer
sent dozens, and possibly hundreds, of his undead
K'ell Hunters
to cut off the city from the outside world.
The
K'Chain Che'Malle
attacked caravans and killed travellers across the
Vision Plain
, even attracting notice from the
Barghast
shamans of the
Barghast Range
.
Several opportune alliances were made by the Grey Swords in secret in the final weeks before the battle. First, the Grey Swords were contacted by the newly allied forces of renegade
Malazan
High Fist
Dujek Onearm
and Warlord
Caladan Brood
. The mercenaries accepted their help with suspicion, but with the knowledge that the two armies were six weeks' travel away.
Also, while on patrol outside the city, several wings of Grey Sword cavalry led by
Shield Anvil
Itkovian
came into contact with the Seer's K'ell Hunters for the first time. Only the coincidental gathering of an army of
Kron T'lan Imass
and

Jaghut

While Brukhalian made outward promises of success to the Prince and the Mask Council,
his true expectations may have been more pessimistic. Itkovian claimed that the Mortal Sword shared his view that their efforts were ultimately meaningless.
As the Pannion hordes began making their way across the
Catlin
River to encircle the city, the Shield Anvil argued with the panicked prince that any attempt to flee to safety would result in their entrapment and slaughter. Their only choice was to defend the city and their nearest reinforcements were weeks away.
First Days
Septarch Kulpath's army made its way across the Caitlin River by barges and pontoon bridges
and gathered in view of the city to the south and east. His original plan called for sending eighty or more K'ell Hunters against the city's North Gate. Once the creatures had broken inside they were to strike terror in the defenders through the scale of the ensuing slaughter. Then the
Urdomen

But the Septarch did not account for the city's T'lan Imass allies. The Kron and T'lan Ay intercepted and destroyed the K'Chain Che'Malle, leaving Kulpath and his forces in disarray.
For five days the Pannion army waited immobile and confused, giving Itkovian, who commanded the city's defences, more time to prepare.
Capustan
Day 1
Then the wait was over as the Pannion forces began assembling and Itkovian made his way to his command post atop the Grey Sword barracks on the southeast wall.
Over a hundred catapults sent balls of fire into the city in waves from the north and west setting the city afire.
At the same time,
Beklite
infantry surrounded and assailed the East Watch and South Watch redoubts.
Beklites, heavily armoured
Betaklites
, Urdomen,
Scalandi
skirmishers, and
Seerdomin
shock troops attacked the Port Gate in the east with battering rams amidst arrow and ballista fire into the gate's towers. The gate was defended by Grey Swords, Capanthall, and Lestari, and it changed hands several times. At one point, the Scalandi made it as far as the barricades on Inside Port Street before finally being driven off by crossbow and arrow fire.
Amidst this fighting,
Stonny Menackis
, who had volunteered and assumed a leadership role, had her fateful encounter with one of the Seerdomin.
From his position on the wall, Itkovian watched as the Gidrath held against multiple Beklite assaults on the East Watch redoubt. By dusk, the Pannion siege engines were launching boulders at the redoubt's walls.
Pannion assaults on the North and West Gates proved to be poorly executed feints.
The main attacks continued on the eastern and southern walls where
Desandi
engineers attempted to scale ladders while the Beklites and Betaklites used battering rams on the gates. They made no attempts to protect themselves and were slaughtered in large numbers, but managed to make their way into the city. The city's layered defences proved effective as the intruders were driven back by arrow fire and the fleeing invaders were cut down by disciplined sorties.
Evening 1
By nightfall the city was aflame, but the walls, gates, and redoubts still held. There was no rest for the city's defenders as Kulpath renewed the attack with fresh troops and the fiery bombardment resumed.
Thousands of Beklites and Scalandi raised ladders on the east wall and swarmed the battlements, once again seizing the Port Gate and the square of New East Market. The city's southern Main Gate was breached by catapult fire, and Jelarkan Concourse was occupied by a legion of Betaklites. The Capanthall West Barracks went up in a massive blaze after being struck by a fiery bomb. The North Gate was breached by Urdomen and Seerdomin shock troops who invaded the Nildar Camp and slew everyone inside. Jelarkan Palace remained impervious to attack.
Itkovian directed the city's response from his rooftop position. He sent Grey Sword Manes and Wings to reinforce the East Inside, West Inside, and Ne'ror Towers that controlled the exits from Jelarkan Concourse.  He sent more Manes to retake the North and East Gates and to rally the Capanthall at Tular Concourse. Then he summoned one of his
Trimasters
to lead a sortie of Wings out to the East Watch redoubt to retrieve any Gidrath survivors.
At this time, powerful new allies of the city made themselves known. The caravan guard captain
Gruntle

Then his group fought and killed the Urdomen besieging
Lestari House
before making their way to the occupied West Gate.
While Gruntle was leading the resistance on the street, the merchant
Keruli
entered the chambers of the Mask Council at the
Thrall

Hetan
and

As dawn approached, Itkovian's forces had successfully retaken the North and East Gates. Mortal Sword Brukhalian took two Manes, two Wings, and 600 recruits to Jelarkan Concourse to battle over a thousand Beklites for the South Gate. At the West Gate only the arrival of Gruntle and his forces prevented calamity. Senar Camp had fallen and Jehbar Tower was besieged. Gruntle rallied the Tular Camp to his side using the bloodied tunic of a slaughtered Senar child as evidence of Pannion cruelty. Then gathering scattered squads of Grey Swords, Capanthall, Gidrath, and Coralessians he had marched to the relief of Jebhar Tower.
By now, Itkovian's Trimaster returned reporting that he had successfully retrieved the surviving Gidrath from the East Watch redoubt. But he also warned that the Tenescowri were finally on the move. The Shield Anvil ordered the tunnels below the city opened and the citizens evacuated to them.
Day 2
As the sun rose, Gruntle and his fighters just outside the West Gate dispatched a company of Beklites sent to reinforce the ones already slain inside. Five hundred more Beklites flanked by Urdomen and Betrullid marshalled nearby masking the arrival of thousands of Tenescowri. Gruntle pulled back inside as masons and labourers worked to repair the gate. A representative of the Grey Swords arrived to offer Gruntle and his troops official sanction and supplies. Then the former caravan guard took his rag-tag militia back to his tenement on Old Daru Street to make their stand there. A late arriving Itkovian was stunned to see in Gruntle the power of
Treach

Fener

The necromancers,
Bauchelain
and
Korbal Broach

Afterwards they took crow form to fly over the city, now completely ringed by a thick band of over 300,000 Tenescowri. The necromancers attacked the peasant hordes surrounding their master Anaster in the east with magic and summoned demons, but were rebuffed by sorcery.
Interpretation of the Tenescowri by
Jeanfverreault
The Tenescowri swarmed every wall breaking through the gates and flooding the city. A raving mob soon surrounded Itkovian and three Manes of Grey Swords outside their barracks. The Shield Anvil was dragged down from his warhorse and severely wounded before Brukhalian and
Karnadas
led a force to his rescue. But the Grey Swords were nearly eliminated as an organised force and the Destriant gave his life pulling Itkovian back from the brink of death.
At Gruntle's tenement, a human tide swept through the bottom floors forcing the defenders upwards. The halls and rooms of the building were packed chest high with the enemy dead. Stonny fought along side Gruntle's second-in-command, a former
Lestari sergeant
. Treach's influence manifested as blackened stripes across Gruntle's face and preternatural fighting skills. As he and his soldiers retreated further up towards the roof, the Lestari identified Gruntle as Trake's Mortal Sword.
As the city succumbed to the inevitable Brukhalian received word from
Rath'Fener
at the Thrall. The priest invoked

to summon the Mortal Sword and the Grey Swords to his aid. To Brukhalian, the request was a clear act of betrayal luring the last of the Grey Swords to their doom. But the words of the Reve were to be obeyed and vengeance would have to be left to Itkovian. Outside the Thrall, the Mortal Swords's force of 400 was met by Septarch Kulpath and five thousand of his elite. Itkovian, still hovering near death, saw their fall in a vision and declared that Rath'Fener the betrayer would be his.
At Bauchelain's estate the Tenescowri hordes and a company of Beklites were no match for the necromancers' sorcery and undead guardians. Those attackers who did not join Korbal Broach's army filled the compound's courtyard in ankle-deep ash.
The City Falls
"
But I am not done yet.
"
―Itkovian
Evening 2
By nightfall, rain fell over a city of gutted buildings and smoldering ruins. The Tenescowri feasted on human flesh roasted over huge bonfires. Squads of Pannion soldiers, unaware of the Grey Sword tunnels, angrily searched for the city's residents. Gruntle's tenement was surrounded on all sides well above ground level by piles of enemy corpses.
Itkovian awoke to find that he was the last of the Grey Sword commanders. Only 137 Grey Swords remained, of whom 96 were Capan recruits. The Grey Sword barracks were burned and no one knew the fate of Jelarkan and his palace.
While the enemy chiefly surrounded the Thrall, the Shield Anvil organised an expedition to scout the palace in force. There they found Anaster and his Tenescowri occupying the main hall feasting on the cooked remains of Jelarkan and his guards. The Prince's skin hung from pikes near his throne. Itkovian confronted the First Child, startling and enraging him by claiming to sense his despair. Itkovian's offer to take that despair upon himself to unburden Anaster broke the First Child who ordered his hidden Seerdomin to attack and escaped weeping. The Grey Swords cut down the remaining Tenescowri and forty Seerdomin to take the palace.
Afterwards, Itkovian ordered Jelarkan's skin to be sewn back onto his body which was then placed upon his throne.
Brukhalian had asked Itkovian to hold the city for six weeks, but the Shield Anvil had given him less than three days.

"

"

The Pannion army was irretrievably broken and the siege was over.
Aftermath
Itkovian watched the Pannion's change in fortune from Jelarakan's Palace which he had defended from assault for four days since driving the Tenescowri out. Once the streets were clear he and his 112 remaining Grey Swords made their way towards the Thrall escorted by Barghast warriors.  The buildings of the Daru District were almost completely burned or collapsed except for Gruntle's tenement which nearly buckled under the weight of the Pannion corpses inside.
Arriving at the plaza where Brukhalian had died outside the Thrall, they were greeted by Humbrall Taur, Gruntle, Ganoes Paran, and the Bridgeburners. Keruli and the Mask Council soon joined them from the Thrall, its protective glow now faded away. Itkovian ordered Rath'Fener seized, and charged him with betraying Fener and the Grey Swords.  He invoked the Reve and delivered Fener's judgement by cutting off the priest's hands. Then the Shield Anvil, knowing that the fallen Fener no longer awaited in his realm, took Rath'Fener's pain upon himself to free his soul to

While the Barghast dug mass graves north of the city for the dead, twenty-thousand non-combatants were released from their underground refuges.
Approximately two hundred thousand demoralised Tenescowri huddled a league and a half west of the city, but Whiskeyjack refused to send his troops to slaughter them en masse. He argued that the starving peasants were victims of the Pannion Seer. Only Anaster and the Pannion officers would face Malazan military justice if captured.
The Mask Council eventually coronated Prince Arard as the city's new ruler.

The Siege of Pale
"
In the Year of Burn's Sleep 1163, the Siege of Pale ended with a now legendary sorcerous conflagration.
"
―
Imrygyn Tallobant
The Siege of
Pale
marked a turning point in the war on
Genabackis
. It lasted three years,
ending in late winter
of the 1163rd year of
Burn's Sleep
.
Background
Official map of
Genabackis
The
Malazan Empire
began its
conquest of the Genabackan continent
in the 1152nd year of Burn's Sleep.
The
Free Cities
of Genabackis formed a loose alliance in an attempt to stop the empire's advance, but over the course of twelve years of war, all but two of the major Free Cities had been subjugated and conquered. These two remnants were the central Genabackan cities of Pale and
Darujhistan
.
The intervention of other foreign belligerents strained the ability of the Malazan army to continue its campaign southwards. The
Tiste Andii
of
Moon's Spawn
, the mercenaries of the
Crimson Guard
, and the warlord
Caladan Brood
all entered the war on the side of the Free Cities, bogging down the Malazan forces in the
Blackdog Forest
and
Mott campaigns
alongside local forces.
When the Malazan war-machine finally began the siege of Pale in 1160, the floating fortress of Moon's Spawn itself appeared to defend the city. The final assault on Pale wouldn't begin until the end of 1163.
The Malazans had also allied with the local
Moranth
people in 1156, historical rivals of Pale.
The two sides had fought wars in the past over the ownership of trade routes, which Pale had usually won. Their assistance in the siege was secured in return for the promise of an hour of slaughter once the city was taken, as vengeance for each individual Moranth killed in their past conflicts with Pale.
Opposing sides
The besieged
Pale had massive ancient stone walls, the foundations of which were built on a huge glacial dump which covered the entire valley area. Below the city walls this rock pile filled a giant crevice reaching so far underground that even with magic it was hardly possible to find its bottom.
Pale's resident mages had allied themselves with the
Lord of Moon's Spawn
, the leader of the
Tiste Andii
. The presence of his fortress,
Moon's Spawn
, hanging a quarter-mile over the city was the reason that the siege had gone on for three years.
The besiegers
The combined
Malazan 2nd
and
6th armies
under
High Fist
Dujek Onearm
were the primary forces outside Pale. They were comprised of seven thousand veterans and four thousand recruits and were accompanied by several allied
Black Moranth
legions.
High Mage
Tayschrenn
was part of the Command and pulling the strings behind the scene. He was in constant direct contact with the
Empress
.
The fourteen-hundred
Bridgeburners
of the 2nd army had been tasked with undermining the ancient walls, a somewhat impossible undertaking due to the nature of the geology beneath the walls.
Whiskeyjack
, former commander of the 2nd Army, had years earlier been replaced by Dujek and demoted to a mere sergeant of the Bridgeburner 9th Squad.
Over the course of the three-year siege, the 9th Squad went through four captains,
and the Bridgeburners in general became infamous for the high turnover rate of their appointed officers, some of whom were suspected of dying to "a dagger in their back".
The 2nd's
mage cadre
consisted of three mages: cadre leader
Tattersail
,
Calot
, and
Hairlock
. Tattersail had recently replaced the former cadre leader
Nedurian
, who had been killed in the
Mott Campaign
.
She believed the cadre was severely undermanned and should, at full strength, have held seven mages.
At least two known members of the Claw were permanently attached to the 2nd Army:
Toc the Younger
, and Toc's Clawmaster (who posed as a low-ranking officer).
The Malazan encampment was set up with precise order.
They were camped on the hillsides overlooking the plains outside the city. Rows of grey canvas tents had pathways between them and regimental standards were blowing in the wind. Braziers filled with burning horse dung would keep soldiers on watch warm.
There was also a massive camp of tents and shelters set up by the camp followers.
The final battle
The final battle by
Marc Simonetti (official image)
Motive for the assault
It was the Malazan belief that the Northern campaign of the
5th Malaz Army
had drawn the majority of Moon Spawn's inhabitants away from the fortress, leaving the
Lord of Moon's Spawn
to defend Pale alone.
However, as the 5th Army in the north had been decimated and wouldn't get any reinforcements for some time, the Lord of Moon's Spawn might have his armies back at his disposal at Pale in the near future. Thus the decision was finally made to end the stalemate and attack the city.
Preparations
On the final day of the Siege, in late winter of the 1163rd year of Burn's Sleep, the
2nd Malaz Army
received direct orders from Empress
Laseen
to attack Moon's Spawn.
She had sent an additional member of the
Claw
to infiltrate Pale and eliminate the city's mages as a precursor to the attack,
in order to prevent them from helping any magic user still resident in Moon's Spawn.
Cadre mage Hairlock was sent to inspect the Bridgeburner tunnels and check on their progress. During the inspection he met with the sapper
Fiddler
and made a secret arrangement with his 9th Squad.
At the command meeting in the morning, Dujek informed his mages of the plan to attack.
, with Tayschrenn in attendance. During the meeting, the mages deduced that the as-yet unknown "Lord of Moon's Spawn" must be none other than
Anomander Rake
, the legendary leader of the Tiste Andii race.
It was an overcast day with no wind stirring the midday air and the troops were being bitten by midges as they waited on the plain for the battle to commence.
High Mage
Tayschrenn
led the sorcerous assault on Moon's Spawn. He was atop the hill closest to Moon's Spawn, while the three High Mages,
Nightchill
,
Bellurdan
, and
A'Karonys
, took the center hill. The 2nd army's
mage cadre
, composed of
Tattersail
,
Hairlock
, and
Calot
took the farthest hill (which was situated to the north-west or north of Pale)
The 2nd and
6th
Malaz Armies
, seven thousand veterans and four thousand recruits, formed ranks on the plain and would march towards the city when ordered to do so. The Black Moranth legions lined the ridges to the west, a quarter mile away.
Battle outside the walls of Pale
Battle of Pale (official cover art by Felix Ortiz)
When Tayschrenn began the assault, it became apparent that Moon's Spawn would not flee. The magic attack upon the fortress sent debris of stone flying into the city of Pale and into the Malazan forces on the plain. The first attack also dislodged a huge flock of
Great Ravens
which were nestled among the crags of the fortress, estimated by Hairlock to number thirty-thousand. Anomander Rake then appeared on a ledge of the fortress and opened his own Warren of
Kurald Galain
.
In the following battle, Hairlock was sliced in half by a wave of sorcery and Calot sacrificed himself to save Tattersail from an attack from behind.
Nightchill was dismembered and partially devoured by a
Kenryll'ah
demon and in retaliation for his mate's death, Bellurdan crushed the skull of the demon.
A'Karonys was first frozen solid, then crushed to dust by 'wings of ice'.
Numerous sorcerous attacks launched from Moon's Spawn were deflected by Tayschrenn, causing huge damage amongst the massed armies of the 2nd and 5th, decimating them. His continued assaults sheared enormous cliffs off the Moon's face which rained down upon Pale and the battlefield outside the walls, causing death and chaos among both the city and the Malazan forces alike.
The soldiers in the field also came under attack from demons.
During the assault, all five of the tunnels carved under the city by the Bridgeburners collapsed, killing almost the entire division to a man. Of the fourteen-hundred Bridgeburners, only thirty-nine
were able to escape from one of the tunnels.
After sustaining heavy damage, Moon's Spawn retreated south towards the
Tahlyn Mountains
.
The Free City of Pale then fell. Altogether, about nine thousand men and women died.
Immediate aftermath
Kalam, Quick Ben, and Whiskeyjack standing over dying Hairlock. (Official art by Michael Kormack)
The Ravens of Moon's Spawn feasted on the remains outside the walls after the battle.
The order of the Malazan encampment had disintegrated. Wounded were loaded on horse-drawn carts and the air was filled with the screams of pain.
Among the injured was Toc the Younger, the Claw attached to the 2nd Army, who lost his eye to burning rubble falling from Moon's Spawn.
The few remaining Bridgeburners attempted to dig out the four collapsed tunnels in the hopes of rescuing possible survivors. Tayschrenn stopped them from rounding up other parts of the army to help.
Simultaneously, direct orders arrived from the Malazan court ordering the last Bridgeburners of
Whiskeyjack's
squad on a new mission to seize the final Free City, Darujhistan. This turn of events led Whiskeyjack to conclude that the Bridgeburners were being deliberately marked for death.
About an hour after the end of the siege, as part of the pre-arranged deal between Hairlock and the Bridgeburner 9th Squad,
Quick Ben
performed a soulshifting ritual on the dying mage and transferred Hairlock's soul into a wooden puppet. Tattersail examined the placement of the wounds on Hairlock. The sorcery which had cut him down had come from the plain. It was Tattersail's belief that Tayschrenn had arranged the wiping out of his own mages and that he deliberately decimated Onearm's Host.
The Moranth entered Pale in accordance with the agreement made with the Empire. No one in the city was safe as the Moranth enacted an hour of retribution. The streets ran red with blood as 18,739 residents of Pale were purged, one for every confirmed Moranth death at the hands of Pale.
Adding this figure together with the 9,000 killed during the actual battle, the total death toll of the final assault on Pale was, at the very minimum, around 27,739 deaths.
Tayschrenn held a de-briefing after the battle, sometime later in the day.
Timeline of events in Pale later on
Sometime after the battle, by the fifth day at the latest, the Empire headquarters were re-located to a compound within the city.
Sometime in the days after the battle, a low-ranking officer delivered Tayschrenn's commiserations to the surviving Bridgburners. The officer was found two hours later in his room, expertly garotted.
When
Toc the Younger
accompanied
Paran
into Pale, he told the Captain, that his (Toc's)
Clawmaster
had been garotted two days back.
One early morning, about seven days after the siege (and within about twenty-four hours of the attack on Paran), Whiskeyjack and his squad departed from Pale to Darujhistan by
Moranth
Quorl
.
Within the hour, an attack by the
Hound of Shadow
Gear
on Tattersail in her quarters in Pale, left her unconscious.
The Hound also killed several marines, fourteen according to what Toc the Younger had heard.
When Hairlock met with Quick Ben in the
Warren of Chaos
, her fever had broken the night before. She had screamed something about a coin having fallen and now being in someone's hand, putting the event around the time of
Crokus Younghand
picking up the coin.
Toc the Younger left Pale to rendezvous with
Adjunct
Lorn
sometime soon after the attack by the Hound. He rode for three days before meeting her and returned to Pale with her, riding double.
On the evening of their return, a formal dinner was held for the Adjunct. This was four days after Tattersail had regained consciousness after about six days in a coma.
Between one-and-two weeks after the battle, the first of the Pale refugee caravans arrived in Darujhistan. They had escaped from the city and slipped through the Malazan siege-lines during the chaos of the battle.
Retrospective insights
While conversing with
Baruk
later on, Rake revealed that the wizards of Pale had, upon the first news of the infiltration of the empire's Claws, already fled before the Malazan final assault began. This left him to defend Pale alone. Further, the Claw had actually been rendered ineffective since they had been dispatched by him almost as soon as they had entered Pale. He stated his belief that the Malazan assault would have been repelled had the wizards of Pale not abandoned the city. Rake told Baruk that he had since hunted down the wizards who had fled, tracking the last two to
Darujhistan
. Given the alternative of them dying by
Dragnipur
, the alchemist promised Rake he would have their heads.

The city of
Y'Ghatan
was the subject of many sieges, and each time it was rebuilt. The event which was commonly known as
the

Dassem Ultor

met

Burn's Sleep
.
According to
Blistig

Malaz 1st Army
and the
Malaz 9th Army
had laid siege to the city and a total of eight to ten thousand soldiers had been lost.
The city itself had burned to the ground.
The
Malaz 3rd Army
also played a huge role in the siege.
In
Night of Knives
Siege of Y'Ghatan battle map by Steven Erikson
Temper
had been present at the Siege of Y'Ghatan as part of Dassem's bodyguard known as the
First Sword
, and recalled his memories of the siege in detail. A host of different personages, including several members of the
Old Guard
were present. These included
Surly
,
A'Karonys
,
Amaron
,
Choss
,
Whiskeyjack
et al.
Ullen Khadeve

The City Fortifications
There were four levels of escarpments on the ancient city walls. The fortifications of the first wall looked to be no more solid than simple rammed earth, while at least one of the following walls consisted of heaped sun-baked mud bricks.  While these fortifications held, the
Seven Cities
javelin cavalry and skirmishers usually sallied from the city walls to harass the Malazan Regulars.
Taking the Walls
The Malazan commanders and Sub-Fists headed for their positions, and the Y'Ghatan Guard and skirmisher cavalry withdrew behind the city walls. A'Karonys tried to lash the walls with flames to try and bring it down, but was rebuffed by the
Holy Falah'd
. The saboteurs then emerged, reporting that the walls had been breached. Breaches appeared on the first of the ringed walls, which the defenders held tenaciously. After a long period of continuous assault, Dassem turned over field command to the three sub-commanders: Amaron, Choss and Whiskeyjack.
The Malazan regulars of the Third Army then pushed through to the second level, at which time, Dassem and his Sword took to the field, expecting
Surgen Ress
, the Champion of Y'Ghatan to commit to the field. Crossing the second escarpment, they received intelligence that Surgen had taken the field towards the right flank. Leading the assault, the Sword found itself isolated and encircled by the Seven Cities infantry.
Initially, the fight went in the Sword's favor, with the defenders being pushed back by Dassem, as the Sword defended his back. Soon, things went wrong when a bolt, ostensibly thrown from their own lines hit Dassem, causing the Sword, now encircled by enemy infantry and being pushed by a berserk Surgen, to fight a desperate retreat. In the fight, most of the bodyguard was killed by either the enemy infantry or Surgen, who pursued them with the sole ambition of killing a now injured Dassem. Temper then fought Surgen to a standstill while
Ferrule
helped Dassem fend off the infantry as they retreated. Temper managed to hold Surgen back long enough for relief to arrive in the form of the Malazan Regulars, upon which he lost consciousness.
All this had been a conspiracy enacted by
Surly
to take over the throne and remove Dassem, one of the biggest threats to her chances to become Empress. Surly's
Claws
tried to kill an unconscious Dassem, but failed; and Dassem, Ferrule, and Temper managed to escape.
Meanwhile Choss had been promoted as interim commander of the Third Army, and Surgen succumbed to his wounds in an unexplained manner, most probably assassinated by the Claw.
The City of Y'Ghatan fell three days later, High Fist Choss acquitting himself well in his new role.
In

Officially, Dassem and his First Sword were said to be buried there, but most soldiers believed otherwise.  They believed Dassem's guard was buried in unmarked graves upon the field to prevent desecration and Dassem was buried in an unmarked grave near
Unta

The Cull
, known elsewhere as
the Purge
,
took place during the
Season of Rot
in 1163
BS
.
Empress
Laseen
brought a company of
Seven Cities
Red Blades
(also known as Red Swords) to
Unta

Nobles, academics, and malcontents of the Empire were arrested and enslaved. There were midnight arrests in which the doors to homes were broken down and the families dragged from their beds.

Captain

The Empress is displeased.
Behind the rot of the imperial army's incompetent command, you will find the face of the nobility.
The purchase of commissions is a plague threatening the entire empire. Is it any wonder the Empress is displeased.

the opportunity to round up malcontents and unaligned academics, to close the fist of military presence on the capital, drumming the need for more troops, more recruits, more protection against the treasonous scheming of the noble class. The seized assets paid for this martial expansion.

Felisin
,
Heboric
,
Baudin
and
Lady Gaesen
.
After the Cull, order was reinstated, the victims' assets were seized by the empire and the surviving victims were deported as slaves.

The Drownings
were a form of criminal punishment used in the city of
Letheras
as well as a public spectacle. The basic idea was that an accused criminal was weighed down with their
fine
in
coins
, attached to a rope (for the later retrieval of the coins), and forced to swim the
Quillas Canal
. The amount of weight attached to the criminal was proportional to the crime, although additional weight could be added if a prisoner looked especially capable. Should that criminal emerge from the other side of the canal intact, they were free to go, though usually they drowned.
Crowds watched and wagered on the spectacle from the Low Walk along the canal and the tiers above.
The canal was about one hundred paces wide. Those who failed to survive had their corpse dumped back into the river, which led to the saying
"Guilty as sludge"
.
Given the relative rarity of a successful crossing, the Drownings also became the subject of many
wagers
by the residents of the city.
During the holiday of
Errant
's Festival, Special Drownings were held involving the most notorious criminals, and wagering was frenzied.
The occasional animal was sentenced to the Drownings, such as the feral cat found guilty of "raping a flightless ornamental duck" on the
Rinnesict Estate
.

An accurate historical timeline may be the most confounding aspect of the series for readers who want all the pieces of the novels to fit together in a precise design. Erikson has admitted that some of the dates printed in the ten book
Malazan Book of the Fallen
series are in error, and later books stop using dates altogether. In some instances characters seem to age far quicker than the time between events would seem to allow.
At other times, characters make references that key historical dates are deliberately obscured in the public consciousness by decades.
During a discussion on the
Kharkanas Trilogy
, Erikson did not appear keen on making all the numbers work out. "That's not how histories were originally told anyways. They weren't really about timelines.
Herodotus
would...basically throw out, 'Well, yeah, this was two hundred years ago or this was five hundred years ago', and you know, they're just random numbers basically...And I like that. I think it's cool."
Or as Erikson remarked in a Reddit AMA, "Timeline? Yeah, whatever."
The below timeline tries to make sense of the conflicting data and will continue to evolve as each book comes out. In some cases, potential conflicts between dates are noted and point to more detailed discussions elsewhere.

The
War of Unification
was waged by
Hannan Mosag
to bring the warring
Tiste Edur
tribes of northwestern
Lether
together under his command.  The war lasted a dozen years, coming to a conclusion shortly before the start of
Midnight Tides
.
Casualties were relatively light as warfare among the Tiste Edur was strictly bound by rules and prohibitions.
But during this period, the Tiste Edur faced hunger and other severe privations as all focus shifted to the war effort.
While Tiste Edur warriors fought their fellow tribes, the
Letherii Empire
moved into the temporarily abandoned Edur territory on
the Reach
and claimed it for its own.
The Warlock King of the
Hiroth
tribe maintained his tenuous grip on the new confederation by holding hostage the firstborn sons of the subjugated chiefs as members of his
K'risnan Cadre
.
During the war, the
Merude
were the last tribe to capitulate to Hannan Mosag.  The Merude's combined blooded and soon-to-be-blooded warrior population neared forty thousand, surpassing any of the other tribes.  They also possessed more ships, and their chief,
Hanradi Khalag
, possessed more battle trophies than anyone in generations.  The Merude thought their strength should have made them pre-eminent among the Edur.  What the Merude did not possess was Hannan Mosag's mastery of
Kurald Emurlahn
.
By the end of the war, the Merude stood alone against the other tribes and the Tiste Edur tradition of restrained combat threatened to descend into desperate brutality.  Hannan Mosag ended the war by secretly entering Hanradi Khalag's longhouse and somehow securing the chief's surrender.  Hanradi Khalag's son soon joined Mosag's K'risnan as one of the hostage princes.
Fear Sengar
's martial prowess and
Binadas Sengar
's formidable sorcery were crucial during the campaign.  Meanwhile,
Theradas Buhn
had been unexcelled in leading raids from the sea.
During the starving times,
Feather Witch
witnessed the Edur use their slaves as bait for
Beskra crabs
. Slaves that were too old to work were lowered in cages from Edur knarri to drown and serve as crab bait. Slaves who were not liked were allowed to keep their heads above the tide line so the crabs could devour them alive.

The
Wax Masons
, whose full name was
The Grand High Order of the Wax Masons
, had been a secret society - now defunct - in
Farrog
. A branch of the 'Wax Masons' was the 'Lavender Hive of the Full Moon', whose members were known as 'Honeymooners'. An identifying mark of the 'Honeymooners' was apparently a forearm tattoo of a honeybee - stinger extended - beside a solid circle representing the full Moon. The defining ritual of the 'Lavender Hive' took place once a month on the night of the full Moon - at the third bell before midnight. At that time the 'Honeymooners', having each eaten a basket of lavender flowers, bared their bottoms to the Moon and passed aromatic gas as many times as possible. The individual 'Honeymooner' who could break wind the most number of times in a row became 'Chief Rumpah' of the 'Lavender Hive' - a prestigious honor amongst the 'Wax Masons'.
Notable Wax Masons
Barunko
Borbos
In
The Fiends of Nightmaria
Barunko
, a thief of Farrog and a '
Party of Five
' member - had also been a 'Honeymooner' of the 'Lavender Hive' of the 'Wax Masons'. Barunko had, during one period, 'owned' the title of 'Chief Rumpah' because of his gift of a high degree of flatulence which allowed him to break wind copiously at the monthly full Moon rituals of the 'Lavender Hive'. A fellow 'Honeymooner',
Borbos
- wanting badly to become 'Chief Rumpah' - was unable to achieve sufficient flatulence with just lavender flowers in order to take the prestigious title away from Barunko. Because of this, Borbos started cheating by secretly eating lima beans and cabbage. Barunko, on finding out about this dishonorable behavior, felt that he had to slay Borbos - not only because of his dishonesty in this important matter, but also because honeybees were being killed by Borbos', now potent, flatulence. The ultimate downfall of the 'Wax Masons', along with the 'Lavender Hive', was - according to Barunko - due to a combination of "overblown egos and the excessive use of drugs and hard liquor".
