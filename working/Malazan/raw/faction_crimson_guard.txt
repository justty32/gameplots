Amatt
[Ah-matt] was an
Avowed
member of the
Second Company
of the
Crimson Guard
.
In
Return of the Crimson Guard
Amatt participated in the Guard's invasion of
Unta
, the
Malazan
imperial capital. The former
Claw
Kiska
reported to
Tayschrenn
that she had seen Amatt break through a barricade of burning wagons and timbers simply by walking into it and pushing it aside. She then saw him walk back to his ship pulling out seven crossbow bolts as they struck him.
At the subsequent
Battle of the Plains
, Amatt was in a squad of Avowed commanded by
Lean
that defended the Pilgrim Bridge over the
Idryn
river. He, Lean,
Black
,
Cole
, and
Turgal
resisted numerous attacks (preventing twenty-nine thousand
Kanese
soldiers from reinforcing the Malazans). They fought with four of them fighting whilst the fifth would rest behind them.
K'azz D'Avore
eventually arrived with
Kyle
and the
Lost brothers
to relieve them. Amatt was coughing up blood and bore horrific wounds and whilst some of the others returned to the fighting, for Amatt, it was over as the loss of blood made him unable to stand any longer.
After their detail was stood down, Amatt lifted Turgal. This was likely a continuity mistake as he was meant to be in the same condition as Black who was scooped up by Cole at the same time. The party then left via the portal to the
Imperial Warren
.
In
Blood and Bone
Amatt, as one of the best remaining swords, was chosen by
Shimmer
to be amongst those accompanying K'azz on his journey to
Jacuruku
.
He was armed with a heavy broadsword and a wide infantryman's shield.
In
Assail
Amatt travelled to
Assail
as part of
Shimmer
's expedition to find
Cal-Brinn
and the lost
Fourth Company
. Amatt fell in battle against the mages
Geth Sharr
and
Lurnan Sharr
.

The Crimson Guard was the legendary D'Avore family mercenary company once known for opposing the
Talian hegemony
.
But its modern incarnation was created by a Vow sworn in the 1066th year of

"
We gather here on this day in the sight of one another to swear a binding oath.  What we here swear is unrelenting and unending opposition to the Malazan Empire for so long as it shall endure. To never abandon or turn away from such opposition.  To this cause all gathered here must give their individual agreement and binding commitment. Those of you who know doubt, or who feel unable to pledge yourselves utterly to this cause, are free to go. Nay, are encouraged to go. And all without rancour or ill-feelings...So...this is my Vow. This is what I here pledge and what I, in turn, ask of anyone who would choose to follow me.
"
―K'azz
The Goal
K'azz had been removed from his ancestral home on

Recruitment
See also:
Inductions
It was possible to voluntarily join the Crimson Guard, although newcomers were by definition not part of the original Vow and it was unknown if they received any of its benefits. However, they were trained as formidable fighters.

Replenishing the ranks of the Guard was also achieved through "inductions" — essentially press gangs. Slaves were often purchased and given the apparent choice to join up or be set free, however, those who did not join up were not in fact set free.
There were three known rounds of "inductions" since the original formation of the Guard.

The mercenaries and their commander were commonly considered to be the "worst of the worst" of all the then existing mercenary groups. Many people thought that Skinner and his troop were no more than "hired bloody-handed murderers and killers".
Skinner, at this period, was a tall, powerful-looking man, clad in a long mail coat with a two-handed sword belted at his side. His hair was blond, long and thick, and it and his thick beard were both curled. Others tended to think that Skinner seemed to be "too self-satisfied and smug", with a rather sardonic manner. Surprising many, the
Crimson Guard
under
Courian D'Avore
- usually considered by many to be mercenaries who were "paragons of merit" - recruited Skinner and his troop. Thus, Skinner and those he had brought with him, became Crimson Guardsmen. Both Courian's son,
K'azz D'Avore
, and
Surat
- the Guard's reigning champion - were greatly troubled and made uneasy by this inclusion of Skinner and his troop into the ranks of the Guard.
In
Forge of the High Mage
At the time Malazan Emperor Kellanved sent his army and navy to conquer
Falar
, Skinner was already a year late to bring a contingent to the Guard's
Red Fort
in the
Great Fenn Range
to relieve
Gwynn
and
Blues
.
When it became clear that more than twenty thousand Malazan soldiers were passing through
Eagle Summit Pass
below the fort, Blues and Gwynn reached out to Skinner for reinforcements. Gwynn and
Smoky
struggled to use their magic to open communication with Skinner because a powerful aura shielded his location. When Skinner's image appeared before them, he was wearing a coat of fine dark mail with the tangled coils of a massive snake at his feet and other members of the Guard fighting in the jungle behind him. The irritated Skinner said he could not come to their aid because he was in the midst of a challenging contract in Jacuruku. He recommended they slay the Malazans on their own, then warned they must sever the connection as his new patroness did not suffer trespassers in her demesne. Blues caught a final glimpse of gigantic butterflies descending upon Skinner's soldiers before the connection was painfully cut off. The bleeding Gwynn concluded that Skinner's unnamed patron was Ardata, Witch Queen of Jacuruku.
