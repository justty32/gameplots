The
Ahkrata
were a clan of the
White Face
Barghast
. They distinguished themselves from other clans by their characteristic nose-plugs, single braids and multi-toned armour fashioned from
Moranth
victims - including
Green
,
Black
,
Red
and even some
Gold
clans.
They were reputedly the "meanest" of the White Face Barghast clans.
The Ahkrata were the avowed enemies of the
Ilgres
clan of the Barghast, who had allied themselves with
Caladan Brood
.
In
Memories of Ice
About a thousand Ahkrata were among the Barghast clans who heeded
Senan
Warchief
Humbrall Taur
's call for a gathering. Their contingent was relatively small as they had to travel the greatest distance to the camp site. The Ahkrata amongst the Barghast gathered to witness
Trotts
' fight against
Humbrall Taur's youngest son
. They were initially united with the
Gilk
and the
Barahn
in opposing the effort of the
Malazans
to enlist the help of the White Face Barghast against the
Pannion Seer
.
During the
Capustan
campaign
, the Ahkrata were sent south-east by Humbrall Taur where they were tasked with taking the landings and the floating bridges and barges.
At dawn, they were faced with newly arrived regiments of
Betaklites
and
Betrullid
cavalry and found themselves under siege. Seeing the Barahn slaughtered after breaking, gave the Ahkrata enough resolve to hold until reinforcement arrived at midday in form of the Gilk. The Ahkrata then renewed their own attack, shattered the Betaklites and seized the pontoon bridges and barges.
In
Dust of Dreams
Clan Ahkrata was one of the twenty-seven
White Face Barghast clans which had migrated en masse to the central plains of the
Lether
continent. Once there, they eventually became involved in an unprovoked war with the
Akrynnai
- who were an important nomadic tribal people already inhabiting the area.
Because Ahkrata women wore broad hollow bone plugs in their nostrils,
Ralata
felt that that made them the most beautiful women amongst all the White Face Barghast. Some of these women were members of a sub-group of the Ahkrata clan, known as the
Skincuts
, who were widely feared mounted Amazonian-type 'women slayers'.

Bakal
was a warrior in the
Senan
clan of the
White Face
Barghast
.
In
Dust of Dreams
Bakal was one of
Onos Toolan
's fiercest critics within the Senan war-party that travelled to investigate the destruction of the
Snakehunter
camp on the central plains of
Lether
.
After a challenge issued by
Riggis
was swiftly dealt with by Tool, Bakal and the other Senan warriors present accepted Tool as their Warleader.
Shortly after, Bakal was bid by Tool to come to him and Tool then used Bakal's dagger to take his own life by forcing Bakal's hand, which was holding the dagger, dislocating Bakal's elbow in the process. This saved the nearly a hundred remaining Senan warriors from being killed by Warchief
Maral Eb
and his larger
Barahn
clan force, who were preparing to strike to capture Onos Toolan and were thus led to believe that the Senan had rid themselves of the 'usurper'.
When asked by
Strahl
, Bakal made the decision that the Senan would not warn Maral Eb about the new threat facing the Barghast which Tool had mentioned, instead they would cut themselves loose and resettle in the
Letherii Empire
.
Tool's suicide haunted Bakal, as he felt Tool's phantom blood upon his hands like poison.
With Tool's death, Bakal became leader of the Senan but Maral Eb proclaimed himself overall Warleader of the White Face Barghast, despite many of clan Senan believing that the position was Bakal's right.
On the night of Tool's death, "the night of blood", Maral Eb orchestrated an assassination attempt on Bakal, but Bakal stopped it with the help of
Cafal
.
Bakal himself set out to kill his own wife and her lover, as he knew they would try to kill the lover's wife,
Estaral
. Estaral thereafter felt indebted to Bakal after he had thus saved her.
After this night, Bakal survived several more attempts on his life. He made a plan to free
Hetan
, Onos Toolan's by then
hobbled
widow, collaborating with Cafal
and Estaral.
He asked
Strahl
, who had taken upon himself the role of Bakal's primary bodyguard, to stay with Strahl's own wife that night. Because of this, Bakal died fighting while trying to facilitate Hetan's escape.
Before the final battle between the White Face Barghast and the
Akrynnai
armies, Strahl, who took over command of clan Senan upon Bakal's death, reiterated to his warriors the sentiments of Bakal and Onos Toolan by saying:
"Not this enemy! Not this war!"
As had been the dead Bakal's intention, Strahl had the Senan withdraw - at the last minute - from the battlefield, leaving the remaining White Face Barghast clans under Maral Eb to fight the Akrynnai without the help of clan Senan.

The
Barahn
Clan was a large clan of the
White Face
Barghast
. The Barahn were able to amass up to ten thousand, or more, seasoned weapon-bearers at any one time. The Barahn Warchief,
Maral Eb
, and the Barahn warriors painted their faces with white death-masks streaked with red ochre. Maral Eb and his warriors wore bronze brigandine armour, and adorned their greased, black, spiky hair with red-tipped porcupine quills which clattered as they trotted. When the Barahn were on the move, Maral Eb liked to think of himself in the lead as "the tip of a barbed spearhead" closely followed by warriors bearing standards, sheathed with iron, on which severed heads were impaled.
Clan Barahn's fiercest rivals within the White Face Barghast were the
Senan
, clan of Warchief
Humbrall Taur
.
In
Memories of Ice
The Barahn were among the Barghast clans who heeded Humbrall Taur's call for a gathering. The intense rivalry between clans Barahn and Senan led to more than fifty duels amongst their warriors during the discussions. While initially suspicious of the
Malazan Empire
's intentions, the Barahn agreed to join Humbrall Taur's alliance with renegade Malazan
High Fist
Dujek Onearm
and with
Caladan Brood
against the
Pannion Domin
.
During the
Siege of Capustan
, the Barahn were sent south-east by Humbrall Taur to the
Catlin River
where they and clan
Ahkrata
were tasked with taking the Pannion river landings and their floating bridges and barges.
At dawn, the clans were faced with newly arrived regiments of
Betaklites
and
Betrullid
cavalry and found themselves under siege. The Barahn broke, however, and were slaughtered. This outcome gave the Ahkrata enough resolve to hold until reinforcements arrived at midday in the form of clan
Gilk
. The Ahkrata and the Gilk then renewed their own attack, shattering the Betaklites, and seizing the pontoon bridges and barges.
In
Dust of Dreams
Clan Barahn was one of the twenty-seven
White Face Barghast clans which had migrated en masse to the central plains of the
Lether
continent. Once there, they eventually became involved in a war with the
Akrynnai
- who were an important nomadic tribal people already inhabiting the area and who had done nothing to provoke the war.
Maral Eb, the leader of clan Barahn, was able to become the new Warchief of the combined White Face Barghast.
As the new White Face Warchief, it was Maral Eb of the Barahn who led the combined clans of the Barghast into the ultimate battle with the Akrynnai forces and their allies. Unforeseen mysterious causes, however, resulted in the catastrophic end of all the parties involved - including the entire Barahn Clan.

Barghast concept by
Seraph777
The
Barghast
were a non-
human
pastoral nomadic warrior society
who could be found on several continents.
The Barghast of
Genabackis
were known as the
White Face
, and were concentrated in the mountainous
Barghast Range
of northeastern Genabackis.
The White Face formerly inhabited a far larger territory, but had been pushed to the fringes of civilization over time.
Generally larger than
humans
, they were often seen as exotic barbarians
or savages
by non-Barghast. They had ancient ties with the
Moranth
people, but unlike the Moranth, the Barghast believed they had chosen to walk the path of their ancestors.
Society
"
Those boys will fight anyone, anywhere.
"
―Storo Matash, on the Barghast
Clans & woad
Trotts
and his tattoos, by
Shadaan
.
The Barghast of Genabackis were divided into many clans, with each hunter group identified through their woad tattooing.
The tattoos depicted the history of their families going back as many as hundreds of generations.
Rivalries between clans were fierce, with some clans recognising others as their avowed enemies.
Duels to the death between rival clan members were common and tolerated at inter-clan gatherings.
Each clan was led by a warchief and guided by its
shamans
(also known as
shouldermen
). Questions of leadership could be settled by ritual combat to the death.
Some among the Barghast leadership were capable of speaking
Daru
, the tongue of the most influential human culture in the region.
At times, a clan warchief would manage to unify all the other clans in the tribe under their own leadership. Such arrangements were fragile and prone to being challenged by opportunistic rival warchiefs, who sought the head-warchief's position.
The current head-warchief of the White Face was
Humbrall Taur
of the
Senan
clan.
Shouldermen & ancestor worship
Shouldermen
were the shamans of the Barghast, able to communicate with the spirits of their dead ancestors through dreams, as well as channel their power.
Such communion took the form of a deep meditative trance, where the shaman walked alongside their spirits, or divination
rituals
.
Shouldermen spoke the Barghast holy tongue, which was said to have risen from the pit of darkness from which all thought and words first came. This holy tongue was the same language still spoken by the Moranth, a relic of the two people's shared heritage.
Barghast shamans could create
Blood-iron
to invest weapons with a power that linked the wielder to the weapon.
They could also imbue protective powers on individuals through the use of bone circles.  Individuals were placed inside the circle while shamans danced for days around it.
Bauchelain
attributed the eternal stagnation of the Barghast and their decline before the encroachment of neighboring civilizations to be a product of their dedication to ancestor worship, which was anathema to progress.
Culture
Tools, equipment, fashion
Hetan
by
Dejan Delic
.
Barghast commonly filed their teeth into sharp points, baring them as a mix between a smile and a grimace.
Barghast lived in tents, tipis, and yurts made of wicker and hide.
Their clothing was made of natural materials and found objects unique to each tribe. The
Senan
studded their clothes with coins from an ancient hoard.
The
Barahn
made use of porcupine quills.
The
Ahkrata
stitched together multi-coloured bits of armour from slain
Moranth
.
Every tribe made use of ash, paint, and foul smelling grease on their skin.
Grey ash on the face, for example, was a sign of mourning.
Bhederin
grease applied to the skin kept away biting flies and insects.
They were known for their use of crudely made
hook-swords,
hook-knives,
and lances.
Sexual partnership
Barghast were sexually free and amorous. They openly propositioned those they were attracted to and casually paired off with strangers.
Miscellaneous
Barghast were implied to practice ritual cannibalism, as a form of honour to the devoured companion.
The Barghast remembered the dead with mourning chants.
Barghast were known for their odd sense of humour.
As a sign of respect, they brought the backs of their sword-hands to their brows for a brief brush of glove to skin.
Spirits
Spirit anchoring
Talamandas
by
Johntocaelpiano
.
The Barghast treated spirits according to their age, depending on whether they were ancient or young. The presence of younger Barghast souls in their clans was tolerated because of their lack of power, with their role being to offer comfort to the living.
However, Barghast believed that the powerful ancient souls should not be allowed to wander, and needed to be imprisoned.
The bodies of such spirits were buried in barrow mounds in the low hills along the southern border of the Barghast Range, away from their clans. The trunk of an oak tree would be carved with magical bindings, then pressed upside-down atop the corpse's grave. This served to pin the spirit in place, preventing malicious wandering. Additional traps and guardians were then placed in a circle about the barrow. The souls of Barghast that had been caught in the wooden traps were known as
Sticksnares
. Sometimes, trapped spirits were capable of escaping their bonds. Those who returned to their clans were quickly destroyed, leading most escaped spirits to remain in the lowlands. Some among these spirits retained loyalty to their clans, sending dreams to shouldermen to warn when danger approached.
The effect of this practice was that the living Barghast were cut off from the wisdom of the ancient spirits, leading to their cultural loss of memory and estrangement from their ancestors. In contrast, they were offered only comfort by the younger spirits.
Barghast viewed it as a grevious insult for a mage to enslave their soul-kin, even
sticksnares
who had been spirit-anchored.
Founding Spirits
Capustan, by
Corporal Nobbs
The Barghast once worshipped the Founding Spirits, the ancestor warchiefs of their people, who were the first to arrive on Genabackis. These Founding Spirits were entombed on the site that would later become the city of
Capustan
, which became a vitally important pilgrimage location for Barghast shouldermen. Such a marker of pilgrimage was an exception to the more regular Barghast practice of trapping powerful souls, afforded only to their most pre-eminent spirits.
However, when human settlers arrived in the region and created Capustan, they destroyed the barrows of the Founding Spirits and scattered their remains. Capustan refused numerous demands over the ages to respect the pilgrimage rights of Barghast to the remains of their ancestors.
Afterlife
Barghast souls were meant to travel to the
Barghast Spirit Warren
after their passing. However, due to the Barghast being cut off from their ancestors, their souls were incapable of making the journey, as the Warren had been forgotten. As a result, Barghast souls became lost upon their deaths, scattered both nowhere and everywhere. Only the souls of the Barghast ancestors who had died on the journey to Genabackis dwelled within the Barghast Spirit Warren.
Relationship with Moranth and humans
Twist
by
Yapattack
.
The Barghast had a shared ethnic heritage with the
Moranth
. Both peoples were descendants of a prehistoric lineage of sea-faring
Imass
, which had interbred with
Thelomen Toblakai
, fought against the
Tiste Edur
, and settled mainly on the continent of Genabackis.
This made them close kin to standard humans, who were also descendants of Imass but lacked Toblakai admixture. Humans and Moranth-Barghast were capable of producing offspring with each other.
When the ancestors of the Moranth both made peace with and learned the arts of the Tiste Edur of
Moranth Wood
, the ancestors of the Barghast condemned their cousins for adopting the ways of demons. This resulted in the permanent schism between the Moranth and Barghast peoples, who each accused the other of being fallen kin.
To the modern day, the Barghast still possessed a fear and hatred of the Moranth, a circumstance
Twist
blamed on false memories passed down from ancient times.
History
Prehistory
Imass vs. Jaghut, by
Santiago Lozano
The
Imass
ancestors of the Barghast and Moranth had been driven to the sea in their race's
quest
to hunt the
Jaghut
to extinction, and to avoid the immobilization and starvation caused by the Jaghut ice flows. Consequently, they had not arrived in time for the
Ritual of Tellann
that created the
T'lan Imass
, and remained mortal. From an early time they dwelled among the
Tartheno Toblakai
and interbreeding resulted in a people taller, less squat, and with less pronounced facial features than their Imass forebears.
The ancestors of the Barghast plied the seas in their long, low dugout canoes with prows elaborately carved with scenes from their history.
Their skill at shipbuilding had been such that even though their canoes were assembled in pieces, they appeared to be carved from a single tree. They had also once been excellent swordsmiths, crafting long, single-edged blades with flaring tips and small, diamond-shaped hilts. Made of metals no longer remembered, they were light as a dagger and seemingly incapable of being scratched or broken and required no oiling. But both the Barghast's skills at shipbuilding and metal forging had long since been lost.
Tiste Edur longboat, by
Lauren Sain-Onge
At some point in their history they had come into conflict on the open seas with a grey-skinned race of demons they called the
T'isten'ur
. These creatures of Shadow were known for collecting the heads of their victims while their still-living bodies were enslaved in ceaseless labour.
Post-landfall
After driving their T'isten'ur enemies back into their underworld in the Forest of Shadows in a
distant land
, the vast fleet of the Barghast made landfall at
Capustan
. There, the settlers known as the Founding Families (or the First Families), their numbers now reduced by half in ceaseless battle, buried their dugouts forever.
Around this time, the ancestors of the Moranth split from the ancestors of the Barghast by making peace with the T'isten'ur.
Over time, the truth of their origins was widely lost and the Barghast migrated into the mountains of the Barghast Range. The warchiefs of the Founding Families became worshipped by the Barghast as the Founding Spirits and their barrows were initially visited by pilgrims, but
Daru
and
Capan
settlers later destroyed the barrows when they established Capustan. Ancient bindings and their missing remains left the Founding Spirits as children, unable to
ascend
as true
Gods
. The original
Barghast Warren
became forgotten and the Barghast shamans welcomed only the weaker, younger spirits among the tribes.
In that state, as both
Talamandas
and
Bauchelain
noted, their culture was rotting from within and faced eternal stagnation.
At some point in their history they had been encountered and possibly conquered by explorers and colonizers of the
First Empire
, who considered them ignorant and non-human.
They were unknown to the
Letherii Empire
, with all knowledge beyond their name lost to history.
In
Memories of Ice
The old Barghast Spirits re-awakened and, using
Mallet
as a bridge, negotiated with Barghast warchief
Humbrall Taur
to rejoin the younger spirits and once again be welcomed among the camps of the living Barghast. They were to tell their descendents that the Founding Spirits of the Barghast had awakened but were trapped in
Capustan
. It was also revealed that the
Moranth
were related to the Barghast.
Taur's children,
Hetan
and
Cafal
, presented the clans' demands for the return of their ancestors' remains to the
Mask Council
of
Capustan
.  With nothing to lose on the eve of the
Siege of Capustan
, the Council relented revealing that the ancient bodies had been gathered along with their canoes and entombed below the council chamber in the
Thrall
.
After assisting
Dujek Onearm
's renegade
Malazan
army to defeat the
Pannion Domin
, the Barghast were able to reclaim the bodies. Taur hoped to have the newly elevated Barghast
Gods
blessed by the
Master of the Deck
,
but in their youth they were still weak and naive.
The Barghast Gods threw in their lot with
Hood
to provide
Quick Ben
with access to his Warrens in order to thwart the schemes of the
Crippled God
.
In
Reaper's Gale
Sometime after the end of the
Pannion War
, in the last days of the reign of King
Ezgara Diskanar
of the
Letherii Empire
, the clans of the White Face Barghast "disappeared" from Genabackis and travelled to the north-central coast of the continent of
Lether
, landing just to the north of the
Awl'dan
plains. These Barghast numbered in the hundreds of thousands and their transports had been thousands of huge seagoing war canoes.
Humbrall Taur having tragically drowned at the landing, these Barghast chose
Onos Toolan
, an
Imass
, to be their new warleader. Abandoning their vessels, the Barghast travelled to the southeast, using the
Tellann
Warren
to cloak their movements. They began a long, intensive reconnaissance of this new territory in order to learn the lay of the land and to determine the current political situation of the inhabitants. While reconnoitering, the Barghast learned that the
Grey Swords
of
Elingarth
- with whom they had apparently planned to rendezvous
- had been annihilated by the
Letherii Army
due solely to the treacherous betrayal of the Grey Swords by the
Awl
forces. For Onos Toolan, as a result, there "would be...no room for mercy. Not for the Awl. Not for the Letherii army..."
After allowing the Letherii army to wipe out the Awl army in battle - the two forces for some time having been at war with each other - the Barghast emerged from the Tellann Warren and then finished off the by now exhausted Letherii. The Barghast killed them all in "a frenzy of explosive savagery".
In
Toll the Hounds
Baruk
grew distressed at reports that the White Face Barghast had all disappeared.
Crone
told him they had taken to the seas after the
Tiste Andii
investiture of
Black Coral
. Meanwhile, the Grey Swords had marched back to Elingarth, purchased ships and followed them.
In
Dust of Dreams
All twenty-seven
of the White Face Barghast clans had migrated to
Lether
. After having dealt with the Letherii and the Awl, these Barghast eventually became involved in a war with the
Akrynnai
- who were an important nomadic tribal people already inhabiting the area and who had done nothing to provoke the war.
For a variety of reasons, eight of these twenty-seven Barghast clans had become disenchanted with Onos Toolan's leadership style and with his performance as warchief. These clans had broken off, one by one, to independently settle elsewhere on the Lether plains. Amongst the remaining nineteen Barghast clans, however, there was a great deal of turmoil and unrest and more clans would probably have eventually left if the war with the Akrynnai had not broken out.
As these Barghast clans discovered, however, the quality and quantity of the Akrynnai warriors and their allies - under the leadership of
Sceptre
Irkullas
- were much more potent adversaries than the Barghast had expected. Repeatedly successful in battle, the Akrynnai soon "...left the Barghast reeling".
At the hotly contested ultimate encounter
between the Barghast, led by
Maral Eb
, and the Akrynnai forces an extremely mysterious, cataclysmic event overtook the battlefield and destroyed all of the combatants - both Barghast and Akrynnai alike. The war that these Barghast had begun so rashly, proved, in the end, to be catastrophic for all the parties involved.

As the race under the purview of

The
Barghast Warren
was a long-dead
Warren
that was also known as
the First Landing
. It had been forgotten by the living
Barghast
and was decayed by the loss of mortal memory. Nevertheless, it was where
Quick Ben
suspected the Barghast travelled after death, assuming they made it that far.
The Warren was a insect-filled swamp lit by a pallid, faintly yellow light. Rising from knee-deep, black and foul, swirling water were moss-covered cedar boles and low rises. High-prowed canoes lashed together by rope slowly rotted in the swamp while bundles of supplies on land were covered in mould and fungi. Sound was strangely muted but for the croaking of frogs and dripping of water. The air smelled faintly of saltwater.
The Warren was inhabitated by the Barghast warriors who had not survived their race's long sea journey to
Genabackis
. They were squatter, wider, and more robustly boned than their more modern kin. They clearly resembled the mix of
Imass
and
Toblakai
blood that had spawned their race. Hooded brow-ridges hid their eyes and they wore their long, black hair parted down the centre and tied in rough braids. They wore close-fitting sealskins decorated with bone, antler, and shell and carried long, thin-bladed knives as well as harpoons made of bone.
In
Memories of Ice
During the
Pannion War
, the Barghast
Sticksnare
,
Talamandas

Sticksnares

and

Quick Ben
released his bonds.
In return, Talamandas helped the mage resist the
Crippled God
's poisoning of the
Warrens
, riding his shoulder and allowing him to continue using his magic.

Yedin
was a nine-year-old girl of clan
Senan
of the
White Face
Barghast
.
Spoiler warning
:
The following section contains significant plot details about Yedin.
In
Dust of Dreams
In the Senan encampment on the central plains of
Lether
, Yedin very reluctantly assisted three women--
Balamit
,
Jayviss
and
Hega
--who
hobbled
Hetan
after the death of her husband,
Onos T'oolan
. Yedin was intimidated into participating by Hega, and cauterized Hetan's wounded feet.
Later the same night, Yedin was killed by clan
Barahn
men--confederates of
Maral Eb
--along with the three women who had been directly involved with the hobbling.
Significant plot details  end here.

Zaravow
was a member of the of the
Snakehunter
Clan of the
White Face
Barghast
. He was a huge man, an experienced warrior of twenty-four years, and despite his size he was known to be agile and light-footed in battle. Zaravow wore a badly worn
bhederin
half-cloak and fought with a cutlass.
In
Dust of Dreams
At the Snakehunter's camp on the central plains of
Lether
, Zaravow finally realized that his last remaining wife - out of a previous three - had been cheating on him for some time with a scrawny young Snakehunter warrior,
Benden Ledag
.
Because of this betrayal, Zaravow planned, that very day, to challenge Benden - whom he had never liked - to a duel, at which Zaravow planned to cut the young warrior into pieces. Then Zaravow intended to
hobble
his wife, himself, using his own cutlass to cut off her toes.
However, before Zaravow could take action, the camp was unexpectedly attacked by an unknown enemy and Zaravow and all the other clan members were killed, or died later of their wounds.
