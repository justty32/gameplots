Lady Aegis
was the lady of
House Haran
, an outlying
Tiste
estate of
Kurald Galain
inclined to isolation. She was tall and attractive in a regal fashion, somewhat diminished by her obvious efforts to maintain that regal air and spoke in a clipped manner which hinted at impatience and contempt.
In
Fall of Light
Lady Aegis had set herself in opposition to
Hish Tulla
's plans to unite the Houses in the
First Son
's absence. She maintained that the Tiste civil war was between two opposing religious factions and that the nobility had nothing to do with it. A compromise could be reached.
Vanut Degalla
alluded that Aegis had not been happy with
Andarist
's choice of bride, possibly coveting the position for herself, and that she was more than happy with the schism that had grown between the two brothers.

Agalas
was a sergeant in the
Houseblades
of
House Enes
. She was a sour-faced woman with flat eyes.
Corporal Rees
once got her drunk enough that she told him her life story. He said she had survived torture.
In
Forge of Darkness
Agalas accompanied the House Enes delegation to bring Enesdia to her wedding at
Andarist
's home.
Along the way they discovered evidence of raiders preying on the
Deniers
living on Enes land.
Lord Enes
sent Agalas,
Cryl Durav
, and a contingent of
Houseblades
back to protect the family home while the rest of the wedding party went on. On the way back, Agalas's band ran into
Narad
, the Urusander raider scout.
Suspicious of his presence, Agalas and two other Houseblades followed after Narad only to be ambushed by members of
Urusander's Legion
. Agalas did not survive the ambush.

Altras
was a
lieutenant
in
Urusander's Legion
.

Fall of Light

Wardens of the Outer Reach
. He led all of them to their deaths when he drew out too far from the pike line.
Hunn Raal

Andarist
Biographical information
Pseudonyms
Darist
Race
Tiste Andii
Gender
Male
Appearance
Black skinned, amber eyes, tall, white haired and white bearded.
Family
Anomander Rake
(brother)
Silchas Ruin
(brother)
Phaed
(grandniece)
Andarist
was a
Tiste Andii
and the younger brother of
Anomander Rake
.
Unlike Rake, Andarist had not become a
Soletaken
dragon
and thus, although still long-lived, he aged.
He was described as an extremely tall, ancient old man. His hair was long, white and dishevelled and he wore a beard. His skin was black as ebony and his eyes glittered amber, actually emitting light. The heat radiating from his body shrivelled plants in his vicinity.
He had weathered features.
He spoke very good Malazan, although haltingly, as if he rarely used the words.
Andarist had long surrendered himself to a grief that could never heal.
He did not at first appear much of a warrior as he carried himself like an absent-minded scholar who bore a perpetual look of confusion.
He wielded the huge, double-handed sword
Vengeance
(also called
Grief
).
His silver chain armour had seen centuries of use.
Andarist possessed a stoic attitude having seen many of his kin perish over the years. He did not concern himself with events beyond the island.
He was the author of a treatise entitled
Combat and Negotiation
.
In
House of Chains
The Fall of Darist by
Sam Burley (official artwork)
Calling himself Darist, Andarist lived on the floating island Drift Avalii with a number of young descendants of his brother, Anomander. They had been settled on the island by Rake to guard the
Throne of Shadow
, but Rake had never returned and they had been isolated for many decades.
Left alone to face the Throne's enemies, he appeared to resent his brother for his neglect and for Rake's eternal youth as a draconic Soletaken.
When Andarist detected a half dozen
Tiste Edur
ships off the island's coast, he sent
Phaed
and his other young relatives into hiding on the island. Andarist fully expected to perish while defending the Throne.
Cotillion
sent
Cutter
and
Apsalar
to the island to help defend the Throne, but their boat wrecked on the island's treacherous coast. Andarist fished Cutter from the water, but Apsalar was lost. The Tiste Andii suspected that Cutter was an agent of Rake, but allowed him to accompany him to the Hold of the Throne.
When asked about his sword by Cutter, Andarist remarked that it had been made by Rake who named it
Vengeance
. The sword came into Andarist's possession when Rake found
Dragnipur
more suited to his nature. Andarist called the sword
Grief
and claimed that if it was wielded by an individual with enough willpower, the sword was unbeatable in combat. He added that he did not possess such talent and so he expected the upcoming battle to be his last.
During the first Edur assault, Andarist proved to be an extremely skilled fighter, using both sword and magic to repel a score of attackers. Before he and Cutter could be overwhelmed, Apsalar appeared with Andarist's kin, who she had found in hiding. Cutter later secured help from
Traveller
and a small number of Malazans who had been washed up on the island. Cotillion himself appeared during the final Edur attack, but it was not enough to save Andarist, who fell to Tiste Edur sorcery.
Traveller claimed Andarist's sword, Grief, something Cotillion advocated, but returned its name to Vengeance. Then he vowed to take up defense of the Throne for a time.
In
Midnight Tides
Shortly after the sundering of
Kurald Emurlahn
, the
Malazan world
was invaded through a violent
rent
by the combined armies of
Silchas Ruin
's Tiste Andii and
Scabandari Bloodeye
's Tiste Edur. After the invaders defeated an army of
K'Chain Che'Malle
, Scabandari betrayed his fellow commander and noted that Silchas Ruin was brother to Andarist and Anomandaris Irake. Scabandari claimed that Andarist had long ago surrendered his power in answer to a grief that would never heal. Scabandari also claimed to secretly be the hand that had delivered Andarist's grief.
In
The Bonehunters
The Tiste Andii of
Bluerose
possessed an ancient tapestry depicting
Mother Dark
and the three brothers born in Darkness who were her most cherished children: Andarist, Anomandaris Purake, and Silchas Ruin. But all three had eventually been cast out by her. Andarist was the first, after Mother Dark made an accusation of betrayal that all knew to be mistaken. Filled with unbearable grief, he accepted his banishment by Mother Dark, but vowed to continue his guardianship of her from exile. Anomander and Silchas saw Mother Dark's turning away from her son as a crime and Anomander confronted her. Afterwards, he too walked away, denying the Darkness in his blood and seeking out
Chaos
. Silchas Ruin's indecision and failed efforts to forge reconciliation led to the greatest betrayal of all—an alliance with Shadow.
In
Reaper's Gale
Silchas Ruin told
Fear Sengar
that before the sundering of
Kurald Emurlahn
, Andarist had been betrayed in a manner that set such grief upon his soul that he was driven mad. It was implied that Scabandari was somehow involved.
On
Second Maiden Fort
with
Adjunct
Tavore Paran
's
Bonehunters
,
Nimander Golit
despaired that Andarist had chosen him to lead the other Andii survivors of Drift Avalii. Andarist had known he was going to die in defence of the island, but Nimander believed his faith in him had been mistaken.
In
Toll the Hounds
Atop
The wagon
carrying the damned of
Dragnipur
,
Ditch
encountered a Tiste Andii artist named
Kadaspala
, who was the brother of Andarist's wife,
Enesdia
. Kadaspala had been among the first to fall to the sword after he attempted it to wrest it from
Draconus
to kill Anomander Rake. The artist believed Rake deserved to be enslaved by the sword for "what he'd done. To his brother. To my sister." The sight of Rake's unspecified act had driven the artist to blind himself.
Nimander recalled the first time he had met Andarist. Scores of Anomander Rake's progeny spent their childhoods together in a snowswept keep in a remote mountain range. Rake lived among them in godlike indifference, but their daily needs were tended to by
Endest Silann
. The priest looked upon the children with dismay, knowing what kind of future awaited them. Consequently, he withheld his warmth and treated them like an ogre, although he never did them harm. The children endlessly tormented the old man, mocking and tripping him. Eventually, an aged and stooped Tiste Andii came to the keep and they were shocked to learn it was Rake's brother, Andarist. Rake and Andarist argued bitterly for days. When it was over, Rake told the children Andarist was taking them away to an island. Andarist proved a stern teacher, ending any insolence with a cuff to the head. The children eagerly took up arms and Andarist made them soldiers.
Although each of the children bore the blood of the
Eleint
, a bitter Andarist commanded they suppress it, trying to make them more his own children than Rake's.
Endest Silann recalled the days of civil war among the Tiste Andii when the
High Priestess of Kharkanas
sent him to
Suruth Common Forest
to witness a meeting between Anomander Rake and his brothers, Silchas Ruin and Andarist. Endest's modest rank had been a sign of the High Priestess' contempt for the participants, but the three were among the few not trying to kill each other. The brothers cryptically discussed a strategy for ending the conflict that brought the parties together to form a pact. Andarist, who claimed not to have the same courage or cruel madness to follow his brothers' path, would turn away and do nothing. Ruin, his eyes wet with despair, would be required to betray Andarist.
The next time Endest saw Rake was at the
Citadel
of Kharkanas, which was strewn with bodies from the various factions, including those of the three brothers. Endest had guided Rake, now a Soletaken with the blood of
T'iam
, to the chamber of Mother Dark. Rake reported that he would unite the Tiste Andii and resistance from Andarist,
Drethdenan
, and
Vanut Degalla
was ending, while Silchas Ruin,
Hish Tulla
, and
Manalle
had fled. The goddess scolded her son for his shortsighted tactic that had ended the war, before forsaking him and turning away from her children.
Endest also remembered standing over the body of Andarist's wife, Enesdia, with blood-covered hands as Kharkanas burned.
In
Forge of Darkness
Millenia before the events of the
Malazan Book of the Fallen
, Andarist and his brothers, Anomander Rake and Silchas Ruin, were still
Tiste
. Their father
Nimander
had been dead for two years, but Mother Dark had rewarded his loyalty by granting each of his sons the title, Son of Darkness.
Andarist became one of the Tiste Andii, his skin turned black by exposure to Mother Dark, alongside his brother Anomander.
Andarist's Grief by
Daniel Knoblich
Andarist's betrothed, Enesdia, was brutally murdered on the way to their wedding as part of
Hunn Raal
's political schemes in
Kurald Galain
.
Her brother Kadaspala, who ended up being held prisoner in
Dragnipur
, would later claim that Enesdia was Andarist's wife, but the marriage never actually took place. The murder caused a rift between Andarist and Anomander when Anomander was inspired by the crime to name his sword "Vengeance" rather than "Grief" as Andarist requested.
Enesdia was posthumously titled High Priestess of Dark by Mother Dark as the acolyte,
Endest Silann
, bled over her grave, an event that happened slightly differently in later memory.
He [Endest] had clasped the hand of the dying High Priestess, sharing with her the bleak knowledge that nothing waited for her, nothing at all. He had stood, gods, so long along now, staring down at his blood-covered hands, above the body of a sweet, gentle woman, Andarist's wife.
―Endest Silann •
Toll the Hounds

Speculation for

It is very likely that the entry

which appears in the

Appearance
Tulas Shorn was tall and gaunt, his skin was grey as cobwebs and dust. With long pallid hair, ropy with filth. He wore the remnants of a long chain hauberk, unbelted with an empty splintered scabbard hanging from the baldric beneath his right arm. Leggings of some kind of thick hide, scaled and the hue of forest loam, reached down to grey leather boots that rose to just below the knees. No light was reflected from the pits of his eyes.
In his Eleint form, he was an enormous undead monstrosity with a rotted wedge-shaped head of gleaming bone and shreds of desiccated skin.
Beneath gnarled brow ridges, shadowed pits nearly obscured his withered, grey and black mottled eyes.
His mouth displayed massive fangs and shredded gums.
His gaunt forelimbs bore a tracery of fine roots that were a colourless mockery of blood vessels.
The sails of his wings were torn and his tail was tattered.

PAGE: Mad Poets
CATEGORIES: Tiste
URL: https://malazan.fandom.com/wiki/Mad_Poets
======================================================================

The
Mad Poets
were members of a
Tiste
school of poetry in the ninety-third century of ancient
Kharkanas
which was known for producing verse of extreme terseness. The poetry of the Mad Poets was characteristically untitled and constructed of two very short clauses, simply phrased, separated by a comma.
However, by the following century, the Mad Poets' poetry had become so reductive in style as to be increasingly uninteresting and squeezed dry of meaningfulness - thus catalyzing the formation of a movement which ushered in an age of art and oratory of extravagant lushness that became known as the era of the "Flowering Bright".
In
Toll the Hounds
The Mad Poets and their work came to the mind of
Endest Silann
as he thought about the meaning of life and about the
Dorssan Ryl
River which had flowed darkly through the center of the city of ancient Kharkanas - themes which were not infrequently found in the poetry written by the Mad Poets.
Poetry of the Mad Poets remembered by Endest Silann:
"Water in Darkness, dreams in sleep."
"Essence of truth, senses will lie."
"Certainty scours, a world without wonder."
"Ask no question, the river shall answer."
"Question the river, find the answer."
Speculations
Although not specifically identified as products of the school of the Mad Poets, the following two 'poems' appear in the same chapter as the above examples and are certainly possible works of actual Mad Poets, or strongly influenced by their style:
"The moon does not rain, but it weeps."
"Mercy in compassion, no dragon lives."
A third possible 'poem' of the school of the Mad Poets, although occurring somewhat later than the above examples and being formatted somewhat differently, is also remembered (or composed ??) by Endest Silann, who certainly seemed to have had their work running through his mind:
"Listen to the river. Remember the voices."

It is likely that the name Dathenar Gowl which appears in the Dramatis Personae of
The Crippled God
is a conflation of the two names of Dathenar Fandoris and Prazek Goul. The name does not appear in that form anywhere else. The
Shake
were a subjugated people within the
Letherii Empire
, who were the original inhabitants of the empire's central west coast and a number of islands off shore.
They were hated and feared by both the
Letherii
and their tribal neighbours, and their population dwindled in numbers.
Many of the Shake had interbred for so long with the Letherii that they retained only remnants of their original Shake blood.
The Shore
The Shake religion was known as "The Shore"
and the Shake people were known as "of the shore" or "children of the shore."
They believed the world began with only the sea and would one day return to the same. The sea's thirst was endless, gnawing at the shore, endlessly rising, and devouring all. The Shake were but helpless witnesses to the inevitable. Their god was the shore who was engaged in daily battle with the sea. Unlike the other tribes conquered by the Letherii, they had raised no army against the invaders as their belief in the shore held no vast power. It was a belief in the mutable and in transformation. Their god had no face but every face. Their temple was the shore where land and sea waged war eternally--an endless cycle of building and dissolution. It was the same battle fought by the Shake.
The high-cliffed island of
Second Maiden Fort
was known to the Shake as "The Isle" and was their sacred place, desecrated by the Letherii prison that now occupied it. It had once been connected to the mainland until the sea had risen to isolate it in the
Katter Bight
.
The Shake calendar was based on the Year of the Isle with the events of
Reaper's Gale
taking place approximately in the 2113rd year of that calendar.
The word "Shake" could also be used to precede the given name of someone who was of the Shake.
For example, the man named
Brullyg
could be referred to as "Shake Brullyg".

History
The Shake appeared to be descendants of a small number of
Tiste Andii
refugees who had hidden among the local
Human
population after the fall of the
K'Chain Che'Malle
and
Scabandari Bloodeye
's betrayal of
Silchas Ruin
, although this knowledge had been lost to them.
Their royal titles, based on the stages of night between sunset and sunrise, originated from the Andii and retained their names from the
Andii language
.
The Shake bloodline was also impacted by the K'Chain Che'Malle as some children were born with features reminiscent of that lizard-like race. The children thus marked who were not killed at birth were trained as witches and warlocks.
Deadsmell theorised the Shake guarded the Shore because that was where the newborn K'Chain Che'Malle not claimed by a
Matron
came from.
The Coven
The two hundred Shake
shoulderwomen
(or witches) who remained were known as the coven and keepers of the Old Ways.
Although the Shake were nominally ruled by their royal family, true power lay with the coven.
Their magic came from
demons
of the sea who had once faced a war of slaughter. Determined to survive, these demons had cast their seeds into the waves and tidal pools where they sought the right mother. From time to time they found a woman's womb where a child was already growing and stole its fate. A child thus born was considered demon-kissed and possessed true power from even a single drop of demon blood in their veins. Often such children possessed taloned hands and feet and they were brought into the coven to learn the Old Ways. But for most the taint was deeper, giving the infant scaled, elongated faces, twitching blunt tails, and lurid green eyes. These were seen as abominations and cast from the cliffs on the Isle as gifts of mortal blood to the thirsty sea.
Male warlocks were much fewer in number than their female counterparts because male infants were more likely to possess unacceptable demonic traits and be thrown to their deaths.
Individually they were prodigious casters of curses, but united together they could "work nastier rituals."
Their practices did not endear the Shake to their neighbours. The
Tarthenal
viewed them with horror and the
Nerek
hunted their people in the forests. The
Faraed
thought them child-snatchers and left husks of bread for them on tree stumps as if they were malignant crows.
The Letherii had launched a pogrom to exterminate the coven seeing them as a seed of resistance. But the coven remained.
Shake witches and warlocks were able to communicate with each other long distance through a method known as "cob to web."

The queen of the Shake was
Yan Tovis
, or "Twilight", who had been an
Atri-Preda
in the
Letherii Empire
before she absconded and returned to the Shake to claim her throne. Her brother,
Yedan Derryg
held the titles of Prince and Watch of the Shake.

Despite their subjugation, the Shake maintained a matrilineal royal line.

At

When she simultaneously discovered the Malazan invasion of Lether and reunited with her half-brother,

Letherii Army
to return to the Isle and take her throne.
Already at the Isle was Shake

When

Both Adjunct Tavore and Yan Tovis reached the island at nearly the same time, and negotiations for its future began. Yan Tovis was convinced not to have Brullyg thrown out a window and the
Malazans
were encouraged to depart and support Shake sovereignty over the Isle.
As Queen of the Shake, Yan Tovis broke from tradition and refused to bend to the will of the coven. No more would the witches and warlocks be their peoples' secret rulers, nor would theirs be the sole decision on the choice of the next Rise.
At the same she needed the witches on her side to maintain control over the Isle's large population of Letherii criminals, who had freed themselves during the
Tiste Edur

In

Following the chaos brought by the Malazan defeat of the Letherii Empire and the destruction of their homeland, the Shake were forced onto the mainland where they were brutally attacked by the inhabitants. Yan Tovis made the decision to save her people by attempting to find their ancestral homeland, hidden somewhere within a
Warren

Tiste Liosan

Trivia
Steven Erikson
says the Shake "became favourites of mine" during the writing of the series. "Sometimes it just happens, and you don't plan it...I had everything worked out in terms of where [the series] was going to end up, but I left plenty of room for things [like the Shake] to just emerge spontaneously that would become interesting. The races known as the

[TĪST]
were immigrants to the world of the
Malazan Empire
who arrived long before the events of the series.
They were divided into three major groups, each with its own racial
Warren

Mother Dark
.  They were shown to be frequently at odds during the series.

Mother Dark begat three children,
the First, Tiste Andii, were her dearest,
dwellers of the land before Light.
Then were birthed in pain the Second, Tiste Lians,
the burning glory of Light itself,
and so the First denied their Mother,
in their fury, and so were cast out,
doomed children of Mother Dark.
She then gave rise, in her mercy, to the Third,
spawn of the war between Dark and Light,
the Tiste Edur, and there was shadow
upon their souls.
"
―
Kilmanar's Fables
Sebun Imanan
Language
For a list of known Tiste words and phrases as well as translations please visit the

page.

,

, and
Tiste Edur

Lifespan
Various references were made regarding the lifespan of the Tiste. See
Talk:Tiste
. When asked how long an average Tiste lived (i.e., one who was not a Soletaken Eleint),
Steven Erikson
said he never really asked that of himself and suggested it was as "long as is necessary for the story." But he also noted that the Tiste are "imbued with magic, they are magic users and I think one of the unspoken rules [of the Malazan world is that] magic itself conveys longevity in some fashion or another, so they will be long-lived for sure. Which then poses...other questions of the psychological effect on being around for as long as they are."

Vanut Degalla
was the
Tiste
lord of
Vanut Hold
in the time of ancient
Kharkanas
.
He was the husband of
Syl Lebanas
and the brother of
Lady Degalla
.
Jureg Thaw
was his brother-in-law.
In
Toll the Hounds
Endest Silann
recalled an important moment from the long-ago civil war amongst the Tiste Andii when he was just a lowly Acolyte serving the Temple of
Mother Dark
in the
Citadel of Kharkanas
.
The Temple sent him to
Suruth Common Forest
to witness a meeting between
Anomander Rake
,
Andarist
, and
Silchas Ruin
. Rake expected the unasked-for intrusion while Ruin was suspicious as the three meant to discuss a secret plan involving treachery and the spilling of kin's blood. When Ruin asked Endest why the Temple had chosen to trust them as opposed to
Hish Tulla
or
Manalle
, Endest revealed it was because the three brothers were talking rather than killing each other. The three brothers' plan called for offering themselves as mitigators between Vanut Degalla and
Drethdenan
. Hish Tulla and Manalle had already agreed to a pact though Ruin was worried that Manalle was no fool and was already suspicious.
Later, Endest Silann witnessed Rake come to the Citadel, freshly changed by the murder of
T'iam
, to confront Mother Dark. Rake brought Endest Silann with him as they walked to Mother Dark's temple. War had already come to the Citadel and bodies from the various Tiste Andii factions littered the ground. Amongst those Endest recognized were Andii from the factions of Vanut Degalla, Anomander Rake, Andarist, Silchas Ruin, Hish Tulla, and Drethdenan. Rake told Mother Dark that he planned to unite the Andii and end the civil war. He said the resistance of Vanut Degalle, Andarist, and Drethdenan was ending, while Hish Tulla, Silchas Ruin, and Manalle had fled. In response, Mother Dark forsook him, denied all her first children, and closed the Dark to them all.

Fall of Light

Vask
was a
Tiste
poet or bard.  At the time of the Tiste civil war, he had been dead for a hundred years.
In
Fall of Light

Prazek
and
Dathenar
marched the
Hust Legion
towards battle in the Tiste civil war, they spent their time trading bits of verse from the "wretched trench" of
Kurald Galain
's lesser poets and bards.  Vask was among the targets of their barbs.  Dathenar thought him mired in mediocrity.

Venes Turayd
was a
Tiste
nobleman of
Tulla Hold
who lived in ancient
Kharkanas
and commanded a considerable element of the Hold's
Houseblades
.
He was the estranged uncle of
Hish Tulla
, and she had not seen him in decades. Their disdain for each other was mutual and the two engaged in a "protracted dance of avoidance in the managing of family lands and interests" with the nobleman open about his instincts to oppose her on principle. It appeared a drunken Venes had long ago attempted to sexually assault his niece, who had responded by stabbing him. Venes supposed Hish had kept this knowledge from
Gripp Galas
because he had not yet been forced to face her husband's sword.
Venes was tall and thin, and expertly capable with the sword he carried on his belt.
In
Fall of Light
Venes refused
Pelk
entry into Hish Tullas's chambers within
Kharkanas
, and told her to instead share any relevant news with him. Venes and his men attempted to intimidate Pelk, but were forced to provide her entry when
Gripp Galas
approached Venes and stated that he would be visiting his wife within, taking Pelk with him.

CATEGORIES: Tiste
URL: https://malazan.fandom.com/wiki/Wareth
======================================================================

Wareth
[WARE-eth]
was a
Tiste
prisoner in one of the
Hust
prison mines.
He had once served in the
Hust Legion
before experiencing cowardice in battle, and would have been executed on the field but for his
Hust sword
defying his fellow soldiers' attempts to disarm him. He stopped the sword from killing them by surrendering it.
He was widely regarded as the Hust Legion's lone blot of shame, and the reputation of cowardice followed him to the prison mine.
His nine years in the shafts as lead rock-biter had transformed his tall body, broadening his shoulders, thickening his neck, and enlargening his arms and torso so that his upper body was unnaturally proportioned in comparison to his lower, which proved too much for his hips and legs which bowed under the weight. His overworked muscles spread his shoulders wide while drawing him inward at his chest, causing him to appear hunched.
Rebble
, the one fellow miner he trusted, strapped him into his bed at night to force his body to lie flat and Wareth's exhaustion allowed him to sleep despite the agonising pain.
In
Fall of Light

Wreneck
was a ten-year-old stableboy for
House Drukorlas
in
Abara Delack
. He was a sour dog-faced boy with greasy hair and an acne covered brow. His duties as stableboy were much reduced after the estate's stable burned to the ground.
In
Forge of Darkness
Wreneck befriended the young Drukorlas,
Orfantal
, until their friendship was discovered by Orfantal's grandmother,
Nerys Drukorlat
. She severed the bond between the two boys threatening to take away Wreneck's job and leave his family to starve. Wreneck begrudgingly followed the orders of the "evil hag", but tears ran down his face after seeing Orfantal off on the caravan to
Kharkanas
.
When rogue members of
Urusander's Legion
pillaged the Drukorlas estate, Wreneck was stabbed and beaten before being left for dead in the burning Great House. He managed to pull his beloved maid,
Jinia
, from the wreckage, but collapsed before he could look for Lady Drukorlat.

Neret Sorr

Urusander's Legion

Captain Sharenas Ankhadu

Millick

Lord Urusander's
son,
Osserc
, Yeld deliberately obscured the evidence at the crime scene.  He was happy to let
Lieutenant Serap
report the details of the crime to Lord Urusander.
Urusander later sent Yeld to
Kharkanas
to bring
Hunn Raal
back to explain the Legion attacks on the
Deniers
.  Hunn had fled before Yeld arrived and instead Yeld brought word of the massacre of House Enes.
He was also present when High Priestess
Syntara
shared the gift of
T'riss
, the
Azathanai
, transforming him into one of the first
Tiste Liosan
