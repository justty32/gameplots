The Battle Born is an infantry unit that is created as a result of the
Proliferator's
"Parasitism" capacity. Battle Born are added to an army at the end of a battle if the Proliferator successfully affected an enemy unit with Parasitism, and that unit died in battle, or can be added to a city's garrison by using a
Recycled Stockpile
on that city during a
Dust Eclipse
. Battle Born always spawn at Level 1 equipped with the highest-tier Iron or Dust Claws, Helm, Cuirass, and Greaves available, and cannot be retrofit.
One of the unique properties of the Battle Born is that they ignore Army and Garrison size restrictions; even if your max army size is only 4, you can still have dozens of Battle Born units in the army, so long as Proliferators continue to kill units with Parasitism - Battle Born units must be "spawned" into armies to ignore the max, and cannot merge into armies that are over the maximum. However, having more units in an army than you are allowed comes with an exponentially increasing penalty added to the army's upkeep, with the penalty for having 2 units above max being 40
per turn, but having 12 units over max is about 500
per turn. As such, this property of the Battle Born should be used with restraint, or you should heavily invest in reducing the cost of Military upkeep, either by the
Empire Plan
, Hero
Skills
, the
Reliquary of Auriga
, or the
Cannon Fodder
Necrophage Technology.
Lore
Created from their fallen enemies on the field of battle, these soldiers of the hive do not feel pain, pity, or remorse.

The Proliferator is a support unit for the
Necrophages
. The Proliferator can be researched in the second
Technology Era
.
The Proliferator is also a fairly unique unit in that it is able to make more units; because of the Parasitism capacity, if the Proliferator attacks a unit, that unit will be implanted with an egg unless they have the Disease Immunity capacity. If the infected unit dies in battle, then at the end of battle, a
Battle Born
unit will be added to the army the Proliferator came from. These units also ignore the "Max Units" rule imposed on armies, allowing for massive armies, but at a drastically increased cost to upkeep, so this ability should be used wisely, lest you bankrupt yourself.
Lore
The great reproduction factory of the Necrophages, a Proliferator can resurrect both friend and foe by implanting eggs which control their minds.
