Background
The
Arreat Summit
is the peak of
Mount Arreat
and the location of both
the Ancients
and the entrance to the
Worldstone Keep
. Reaching it requires navigating through numerous frozen tunnels carved long ago into the mountains by the
Barbarians
.
Before being allowed entrance into the Worldstone Keep, any traveler must first face the Ancients on the Arreat Summit in combat. Only by defeating the three guardians can any adventurer prove their worth and gain entrance. The sole exception to this rule is the
Relic of the Ancients
, which allows anyone to enter the Keep without facing the Ancients.
In-game
At the center of the summit area is the
Altar of the Heavens
- when touched, it will awaken the three guardians so that they may challenge the one who seeks to enter the Worldstone Keep. For more information on fighting the ancients, see
Rite of Passage
.
In
Diablo II: Resurrected
and
Diablo II: Resurrected – Reign of the Warlock
, the Arreat Summit is one of the very few non-town areas in the game that cannot be
terrorized
. This is of no loss as enemies outside of the Ancients never spawn here.
Development
This area is called "
Rocky Summit
" in the files.
Trivia
This section contains facts and trivia relevant to this article.
"
The Arreat Summit
" is also the name of a section of Battle.net, which acts as an official guide for
Diablo II: Lord of Destruction
(the expansion set).
Arreat Summit, along with Arcane Sanctuary and Canyon of the Magi, is one of the two locations in the game where video Perspective does not work. This is because Perspective requires two layers to work with, one usually being the ground and the other for the objects that move relatively to it, but Arreat Summit has one layer reserved for the background that shows the areas below the peak.

The
Cairn Stones
are a mysterious circle of stones located in
Stony Field
in
Diablo II
.
Five standing stones make up the circle, each marked with a glyph that must be touched in a particular order to activate the stone. A sixth stone sits apart from the rest, unmarked. A
Super Unique
Carver
named
Rakanishu
also guards these stones.
The player will be required to visit these stones during the
Search for Cain
quest. The circle of stones must be touched in a specific order, which will activate them, opening a portal to
Tristram
. There, the player must find and rescue
Deckard Cain
from the hordes of demons that remained in the town.
Trivia
The term "
cairn
" normally refers to piles of small stones, while "
stone circle
" would be more appropriate for the style of the Cairn Stones seen in the game.

Lore
Cyrangar's Depths is filled with
undead

In-game
The central hall of the Depths of Cyrangar
Cyrangar Depths is the site of
Purge the Depths
gameplay. It has a central hall which contains the Accursed Altar. The hall is connected to three wings whose interior are randomized. There's a chance to come across special rooms (Bunkroom, Cellar, Library, Scroll Room, Well etc) whose doors are closed. These rooms have been infested with evil and filled with darkness. The player has to complete a specific task to dispel the darkness. Once finished the rooms will contain at least one Timeworn Chest.

First Antechamber
After going from the stairway room to the first antechamber, there will be two rooms to your left and right. Both rooms will have a lever and some treasure chests with

. Pulling one lever will open an extension to another room, and pulling another in that extension room will lift the cage that separates the first antechamber from the hallway to the second antechamber, where the Skeleton King awaits.

In the second antechambery is an elaborate structure that is supposed to contain Leoric's body, and three rooms to the front, right, and left. There are corner nooks with

, where if you attack enough of them, they will act like a one-way lever and some more room extensions will open up, where you can find extra treasure from
chests
.
Infinite Opportunities
Since the Skeleton King constantly resurrects fallen skeletons (or apparently generates new ones out of the ground on which he stands), they are a constant source of income. Along with the newly raised skeletons come the gold, items, and occasionally even enchanted items. Going at this for many hours can yield you tens of thousands of gold.

A Terror Rift
Terror Rifts
are a feature of
Diablo Immortal
, introduced as part of the
Precipice of Horror
update.
Lore
A Terror Rift opens
Terror Rifts are gateways into
Sanctuary
, bursting out of the mortal realm's topsoil. They were used by
Diablo
's forces in their assault against Sanctuary.
Adventurers
were forced to venture through the rifts to halt the incoming invaders.
Terror Rifts need an
Oblivion Pillar
at a particular location to act as a beacon for them to open.
Gameplay
Terror Rift entrance
Terror Rifts will be active all the time. 1 zone will be invaded at random, with one Terror Rift portal appearing in the zone. The zone rotates every hour. The zone with the active Terror Rift portal is denoted on the world map, as well as the next zone with a countdown timer. Players must track down the portal to enter. After entering, each player gets 13 minutes to complete the Rift, with Stygian Spires spawning at set times. Even if the Terror Rift entrance closes at the hour, players who are already inside can stay for the remainder of their 13 minute periods.
The rift will take players to the Terrorwrought Realm version of the zone where the rift appeared. This map matches the geography of the normal map but the
monsters
are replaced with Terrorwrought monsters. These monsters drop Stygian Fragments, with Elite monsters dropping more fragments. After gathering a certain number of Fragments from fallen foes (displayed via a progress bar), the player must search for a Stygian Spire. The number of fragments required is 100, 150, 250 and 300 respectively for the four Spires. Sacrificing the required Fragments in one of these pointed beacons will initiate a one-on-one fight with a
beefier opponent
. Toppling this enemy grants a reward chest. The player can complete this process as many times as they can within 13 minutes, in order to press onto more plentiful rewards. Rewards may be earned up to 10 times a week from the Terror Rifts, resetting each Monday at 3 a.m. server time. Once a player acquires all weekly rewards, they can continue to run Rifts but will only be able to earn drops from enemies. Different players will see Spires in different locations on the map.
A successful run, whether solo or with a group of up to four others, will be met with a chance to earn
Eternal Equipment
,
Terror Essence
and other rewards from each reward chest the player(s) receive. Terror Rifts can be played starting at
Hell
II difficulty, with the quality of rewards received scaling with the difficulty level. If the first 3 spires destroyed don't drop a Terror Essence, the 4th one is guaranteed to drop one.
Notes
The rift can be entered at any time. Players should sync their entries to make sure their time limits overlap.
When a group of Elite monsters spawns near the player,
Demons have found you!
appears on the top of the screen.
Due to the high spawn rate of monsters, these rifts present a great opportunity to build up long kill streaks, which are required for some
Trophies
.
When the time is up, a mail with a few items is sent depending on the amount of leftover Stygian Fragments. However this only happens if the rift is "complete", which requires destroying at least one Stygian Spire. The same "completion" is what counts against the 10 runs per week.
Some parts of the map are highlighted to indicate that there is a Stygian Spire in them. However monsters still spawn outside those areas and can be killed for fragments.
The Gem Finder buff is active in a full party, allowing
Normal Gems
to be obtained.
Warp points can be warped to just like on the main map, although due to the high number of mobs it is difficult to complete the teleport animation, so the best way is to stand on a warp point in the first place which skips the animation.
History
Upon release, monster kills could not be shared between players, even between party members. The first player to tag a monster got the drop. This was later fixed with an update.
Originally there was no guarantee on the Terror Essence drop. The guarantee was added in the
Nightmare Manifest
update.
In the July 24 2024 update, the Terror Rift icons on the World Map were changed to be greyed out if the rift is not currently active (i.e. until 3 minutes before opening time).
In the
October 30, 2024
update, Terror Rifts were changed in several ways:
Originally Terror Rifts were only open every day from 8 a.m.–2 p.m. and 6 p.m.–1 a.m. server time. After the update, they are open 24 hours a day.
Originally all players had the same countdown timer that was synced to the rift opening times. After the update, each player gets a 13-minute time from the moment they enter.
Originally players shared Stygian Spire locations. After the update, each player gets their own set of locations, which might not overlap.
Originally there were rift entrances on 3 different maps that stayed the same for the current period. After the update, there is only one entrance at a time that rotates every hour.
Originally all Stygian Spires required 200 fragments to open. After the update, the costs are 100, 150, 250 and 300 in sequence.
Originally opening the Spire would first initiate two waves of monsters, similarly to
Hellslayer Bounties
, before spawning the boss. After the update, the boss spawns immediately.
Originally players had to search for Stygian Spires, and the icon would only appear if they were very close. After the update, a small icon appears on the map as well as a pointer in the main view, making finding the next Spire easier.
In the
September 25, 2025
update, Terror Rift was added as an option to the Party Finder, and a button to open the Party Finder was added to the Terror Rift entrance. Trying to enter solo will also suggest creating a party and sending a recruitment message (but no automatic matchmaking is available).

In-game

In-game, however, the dungeon is rather small and can be fully cleared in just a few minutes, for players visit but a small portion of the realm.
Opening the way
There is a very small chance that killing a Treasure Goblin will open a portal through which player(s) may enter the Treasure Realm. The portal can only appear at level 70 in

Greed's Realm will close 60 seconds after opening the boss loot chest, instantly teleporting players still inside back to town. A new Vault portal can be opened after the previous one closes.
Inside the Realm
The game zone is named The Vault, or The Ancient Vault if an Ancient ring was transmuted. It is filled with piles, pots and chests of

Some good choices of equipment can be a pairing of a

The Inner Sanctum
Main article:
Greed
At the end of the Vault lies the Inner Sanctum (previously "Grand Vault of the Realm"), a small circular zone with a massive gem mounted in the center. Greed herself sits on a throne of gold until disturbed. Treasure Fiends jump off her back when she charges into a wall during the

Development
"With the vastness of the cave system that holds the sea of treasures we really wanted to convey the epic size of Greed's Realm. So I created this system of rock formations that elevated the player so they can witness the majesty of this place. This area was one of my favorite places to work on because I was able to use new ways of creating assets as well as the tried and true 2.5D methods that are at the core of Diablo III's signature look."
-
Richie Marella
(src)
The Treasure Realm was designed after the
Hobbit
2014 film, Scrooge McDuck's Money Pit, and a bee hive.
It was intended that the realm's in-game appearance convey how vast the area truly is. As such, rock formations hold the player above the realm as a whole in order to convey its true size. New methods were used to craft in-game assets for the realm.
During the Patch 2.1.0 PTR, the zone name was Treasure Realm, and The Inner Sanctum was called the Grand Vault of the Realm.

Tips for Players
One simple way to kill all of the three Übers in particular, is with the
Smiter
build
.
A fool-proof way to kill the Übers is to run straight up when you enter the portal. Then go around to the left and go halfway down. Then slowly walk toward the center of Tristram. This causes Diablo and Baal to charge at you. Run away to the left and top again so Mephisto doesn't instantly kill you. Then work on Diablo first because his Pit Lords can kill you pretty quickly. Baal comes next. It may take a few tries, but make sure you make your game permanent, so if you die, you can save and exit and come back. After the two easiest are dead, Mephisto is left. He is easily the hardest monster in the entire game, though there are a few different ways to kill him.
Depending on your skill as a player, the speed of your computer, and the ability of your build. The first way to kill him is to just charge in and attack like a madman. If Life Tap isn't cast within the first 10-15 seconds, you will die and have to start over again, though by going to the various magic vendors (such as
Akara

out (if you're wearing
Enigma
), die, or save and exit. Then, after you're back in the game, run straight to the house and attack him. Since he is kept away from his minions, his Conviction Aura is less of a threat, though this may take a few tries.
